CATEGORY 1: Design Concept & Inspiration
1. Research current fashion trends and identify three that resonate with your aesthetic vision.
2. Create a mood board with colors, textures, and silhouettes that inspire your collection.
3. Define your target customer and their lifestyle needs.
4. Sketch 20 rough design ideas without worrying about technical details.
5. Choose a specific theme or story that will unify your collection.
6. Study the work of three fashion designers whose style influences you.
7. Collect fabric swatches and material samples that match your vision.
8. Write a one-paragraph brand story that explains your design philosophy.
9. Visit museums, galleries, or nature for non-fashion inspiration sources.
10. Narrow down your initial sketches to 10 strongest concepts.

CATEGORY 2: Technical Design & Development
1. Learn basic fashion illustration techniques to communicate your ideas clearly.
2. Create detailed technical flats for each of your selected designs.
3. Research and source appropriate fabrics for each garment type.
4. Learn basic pattern-making skills or find a pattern-making resource.
5. Calculate fabric yardage requirements for each design.
6. Create a tech pack with measurements, construction details, and materials.
7. Make a toile or muslin prototype of your most complex design.
8. Test different construction methods and seam finishes.
9. Document any design modifications needed after prototyping.
10. Finalize your technical specifications for production.

CATEGORY 3: Business Planning & Strategy
1. Research your local fashion market to understand pricing and competition.
2. Calculate the true cost of producing each garment including labor and materials.
3. Decide whether to focus on made-to-order, small batch, or mass production.
4. Create a simple business plan outlining your goals and timeline.
5. Register your fashion brand name and secure social media handles.
6. Research legal requirements for fashion businesses in your area.
7. Set up basic accounting systems to track expenses and revenue.
8. Identify potential sales channels like online platforms, boutiques, or markets.
9. Create a pricing strategy that covers costs and generates profit.
10. Build relationships with local suppliers and potential manufacturing partners.

CATEGORY 4: Production & Manufacturing
1. Find local seamstresses, tailors, or manufacturers who can execute your designs.
2. Create production samples to test quality and fit before bulk orders.
3. Establish quality control standards and inspection processes.
4. Set up a workspace for cutting, sewing, and finishing garments.
5. Learn basic garment construction techniques for simple alterations.
6. Source labels, tags, and packaging materials for your finished products.
7. Create a production schedule that accounts for lead times and deadlines.
8. Test wash and care instructions for each fabric and garment type.
9. Document your production process for consistency and scaling.
10. Build inventory management systems to track materials and finished goods.

CATEGORY 5: Marketing & Sales
1. Photograph your designs professionally or learn basic fashion photography.
2. Create social media accounts and post consistently to build an audience.
3. Write compelling product descriptions that highlight key features and benefits.
4. Attend local fashion shows, markets, or networking events to connect with buyers.
5. Reach out to fashion bloggers or influencers who align with your brand aesthetic.
6. Set up an online store or marketplace presence to sell directly to customers.
7. Create lookbooks or catalogs showcasing your collection in lifestyle contexts.
8. Develop customer service processes for inquiries, orders, and returns.
9. Collect customer feedback and testimonials to improve your offerings.
10. Plan seasonal launches and promotional campaigns to drive sales growth.
