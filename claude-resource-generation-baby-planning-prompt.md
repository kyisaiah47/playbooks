## Quality Benchmarks for Baby Planning Content

### Authority Indicators
- Reference current obstetric and pediatric guidelines and recommendations
- Include evidence-based pregnancy and newborn care practices
- Demonstrate knowledge of various birth options and care approaches
- Show understanding of individual family needs and cultural considerations
- Reference reputable maternal and child health organizations

### Practical Value Metrics
- Provide specific preparation checklists and timeline guidance
- Include actionable decision frameworks for birth and care planning
- Offer realistic expectation setting and preparation strategies
- Give cost estimates and financial planning guidance
- Include safety protocols and emergency preparedness information

### Professional Credibility Tests
- Would obstetricians, midwives, and pediatricians recommend this resource?
- Does this include insights known primarily to maternal and child health professionals?
- Are the health and safety recommendations current and evidence-based?
- Does this solve real challenges expecting parents face in preparation?
- Would this prevent common newborn care mistakes and improve family readiness?

## Content Differentiation Requirements

### Never Create Generic Content About
- Basic pregnancy advice available in countless parenting websites and apps
- One-size-fits-all approaches ignoring individual health needs and preferences
- Outdated medical information or practices not supported by current evidence
- Fear-based content that increases anxiety rather than promoting informed preparation

### Always Create Definitive Resources For
- Evidence-based pregnancy and birth preparation with current medical guidelines
- Comprehensive newborn care guidance that builds confidence and competence
- Family-centered approaches that address relationship and support needs
- Financial and practical planning that addresses real costs and logistics
- Safety-focused strategies that prioritize maternal and infant health

## Success Validation for Baby Planning Articles

**Each article must pass these tests:**
1. **Medical Standards**: Would healthcare professionals specializing in maternal and child health endorse this?
2. **Evidence-Based**: Are recommendations supported by current obstetric and pediatric research?
3. **Safety-Focused**: Does this prioritize maternal and infant health and safety?
4. **Family-Centered**: Does this support the entire family's transition and wellbeing?
5. **Practical Preparation**: Can expecting parents realistically implement these strategies?

**The Ultimate Test**: If someone could only read one resource about this baby planning topic, would this guide them to prepare thoroughly for pregnancy, birth, and early parenthood while promoting the health and wellbeing of both mother and baby?
