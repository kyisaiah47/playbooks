CATEGORY 1: Financial Planning & Security
1. Review your current retirement savings balance across all accounts (401k, IRA, savings).
2. Calculate your estimated monthly expenses in retirement.
3. Meet with a financial advisor to discuss your retirement income strategy.
4. Research Medicare options and supplemental insurance plans.
5. Create a budget that accounts for reduced income in retirement.
6. Consider whether to pay off your mortgage before retiring.
7. Evaluate your Social Security benefits and optimal claiming strategy.
8. Review and update your estate planning documents and beneficiaries.
9. Research tax-efficient withdrawal strategies for retirement accounts.
10. Set up an emergency fund covering 6-12 months of retirement expenses.

CATEGORY 2: Health & Wellness Preparation
1. Schedule comprehensive health checkups with all your doctors.
2. Research healthcare coverage options for retirees in your area.
3. Create a list of current medications and discuss long-term management with your doctor.
4. Establish relationships with specialists you may need in retirement.
5. Research fitness programs and activities suitable for your age and interests.
6. Consider preventive care procedures you've been postponing.
7. Investigate mental health resources and support groups for retirees.
8. Plan a nutrition strategy that fits your retirement budget and lifestyle.
9. Research accessibility modifications you might need for your home.
10. Create advance directives and discuss end-of-life care preferences with family.

CATEGORY 3: Lifestyle & Purpose Planning
1. Define what you want your ideal retirement day to look like.
2. Research volunteer opportunities that align with your values and skills.
3. Explore hobbies or interests you've always wanted to pursue.
4. Consider whether you want to continue working part-time or consulting.
5. Plan how you'll maintain social connections after leaving work.
6. Research educational opportunities like senior auditing or community college classes.
7. Decide whether you want to age in place or relocate for retirement.
8. Create a bucket list of experiences or goals for your retirement years.
9. Consider how you'll structure your days without work schedules.
10. Explore creative outlets like writing, art, music, or crafts.

CATEGORY 4: Career Transition & Legacy
1. Create a timeline for your retirement transition over the next 1-2 years.
2. Document your work knowledge and train your replacement or colleagues.
3. Research your company's retirement benefits and exit procedures.
4. Consider whether you want to maintain professional relationships post-retirement.
5. Update your resume in case you decide to work part-time later.
6. Organize and archive important work documents and contacts.
7. Plan how to transfer your professional responsibilities smoothly.
8. Consider mentoring opportunities within your industry or field.
9. Evaluate whether to maintain professional licenses or certifications.
10. Reflect on your career achievements and create a professional legacy document.

CATEGORY 5: Home & Living Arrangements
1. Evaluate whether your current home suits your retirement needs and budget.
2. Research retirement-friendly communities if considering relocation.
3. Plan home maintenance and repairs you can afford on a fixed income.
4. Consider downsizing possessions and decluttering your living space.
5. Research property tax implications and senior discounts in your area.
6. Evaluate transportation needs if you plan to drive less in the future.
7. Create a home safety checklist and make necessary modifications.
8. Research local services like grocery delivery, housekeeping, or yard care.
9. Consider proximity to family, healthcare, and social activities in housing decisions.
10. Plan how to manage household tasks if your physical abilities change.
