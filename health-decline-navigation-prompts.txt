CATEGORY 1: Medical Care Coordination
1. Schedule an appointment with your primary care physician to discuss your health concerns and symptoms.
2. Request copies of all your medical records from each healthcare provider you've seen in the past year.
3. Create a list of all medications you're currently taking, including dosages and prescribing doctors.
4. Research specialists in your area who treat your specific condition and check if they accept your insurance.
5. Prepare a list of questions to ask your doctor during your next appointment about treatment options.
6. Set up a medical binder or digital folder to organize all your health documents and test results.
7. Contact your insurance company to understand your coverage limits and out-of-pocket costs for treatments.
8. Schedule any recommended follow-up tests or screenings that your doctor has ordered.
9. Find a patient advocate or care coordinator to help navigate complex medical decisions.
10. Create a backup plan for medical emergencies, including hospital preferences and emergency contacts.

CATEGORY 2: Legal and Financial Planning
1. Meet with an estate planning attorney to create or update your will and healthcare directives.
2. Designate a trusted person as your healthcare power of attorney and discuss your wishes with them.
3. Review your health insurance policy to understand what treatments and services are covered.
4. Apply for disability benefits if your condition prevents you from working or limits your income.
5. Set up a healthcare savings account or budget specifically for medical expenses not covered by insurance.
6. Research and apply for financial assistance programs offered by hospitals or pharmaceutical companies.
7. Create an advance directive that clearly states your preferences for end-of-life care.
8. Review and update beneficiaries on all your financial accounts and insurance policies.
9. Consider setting up a special needs trust if you have dependents who will need ongoing care.
10. Document important account numbers, passwords, and financial information for your designated representatives.

CATEGORY 3: Home and Daily Life Modifications
1. Install grab bars, ramps, or other safety equipment in your home to prevent falls and injuries.
2. Reorganize your living space to keep frequently used items within easy reach from seated or limited mobility positions.
3. Research and purchase adaptive tools and equipment that can help you maintain independence with daily tasks.
4. Set up a medication management system with pill organizers, alarms, or automatic dispensers.
5. Create a daily routine that balances rest periods with gentle physical activity as recommended by your healthcare team.
6. Arrange for regular housekeeping, grocery delivery, or meal preparation services if needed.
7. Install emergency alert systems or medical alert devices that can summon help if you fall or need assistance.
8. Modify your bathroom with shower seats, raised toilet seats, and non-slip surfaces for safety.
9. Ensure your home has adequate lighting and remove trip hazards like loose rugs or cluttered walkways.
10. Set up a comfortable space where you can rest and have everything you need within arm's reach.

CATEGORY 4: Support Network and Communication
1. Create a list of family members, friends, and neighbors who can provide different types of support when needed.
2. Join a support group for people with your condition, either in person or online.
3. Schedule regular check-ins with close family members or friends to maintain social connections.
4. Communicate openly with your loved ones about your diagnosis, prognosis, and how they can best support you.
5. Set up a group text, email chain, or online platform to keep your support network updated on your health status.
6. Ask specific people to help with particular tasks like driving to appointments, grocery shopping, or pet care.
7. Connect with others who have successfully navigated similar health challenges for advice and encouragement.
8. Establish boundaries about what information you're comfortable sharing and with whom.
9. Create a rotating schedule so that the burden of helping doesn't fall entirely on one person.
10. Express gratitude regularly to those who are supporting you through this difficult time.

CATEGORY 5: Emotional and Mental Health Care
1. Schedule an appointment with a counselor or therapist who specializes in chronic illness and life transitions.
2. Practice stress-reduction techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or gentle yoga as approved by your doctor.
3. Keep a daily journal to process your emotions and track patterns in your mood and energy levels.
4. Identify activities that bring you joy and peace, and make time for them regularly even if in modified ways.
5. Consider joining a grief support group to process the losses that come with declining health.
6. Talk to your doctor about whether medication for depression or anxiety might be helpful for you.
7. Create a list of coping strategies you can use during particularly difficult days or medical setbacks.
8. Maintain spiritual or religious practices that provide you comfort, adapting them as needed for your current abilities.
9. Set realistic daily goals that give you a sense of purpose and accomplishment without overwhelming yourself.
10. Practice self-compassion and remind yourself that feeling scared, angry, or sad about your situation is completely normal.
