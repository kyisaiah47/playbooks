CATEGORY 1: Understanding Your Rights and Legal Framework
1. Research the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) requirements that apply to your situation.
2. Learn about Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and how it protects you in federally funded programs.
3. Identify which disability rights laws cover your workplace, school, or housing situation.
4. Find your state's disability rights organization or advocacy group for local support.
5. Document your disability diagnosis and how it substantially limits major life activities.
6. Understand the difference between reasonable accommodations and undue hardship.
7. Learn what constitutes disability discrimination and how to recognize it.
8. Research your right to privacy regarding disability information disclosure.
9. Find free legal resources or disability law clinics in your area.
10. Know the time limits for filing disability discrimination complaints in your jurisdiction.

CATEGORY 2: Medical Documentation and Assessment
1. Schedule an appointment with your healthcare provider to discuss accommodation needs.
2. Request a comprehensive medical evaluation that documents your functional limitations.
3. Ask your doctor to provide specific recommendations for workplace or school accommodations.
4. Obtain copies of all relevant medical records, test results, and diagnostic reports.
5. Get a letter from your healthcare provider explaining how your condition affects daily activities.
6. Schedule follow-up appointments to monitor your condition and update documentation as needed.
7. Research whether you need evaluations from specialists for your specific disability.
8. Keep a symptom diary to track how your disability impacts your daily functioning.
9. Ask your healthcare team about assistive technologies that could help you.
10. Ensure your medical documentation is current and meets legal requirements for accommodation requests.

CATEGORY 3: Workplace Accommodation Process
1. Identify your company's HR department or designated accommodation coordinator.
2. Submit a formal written request for reasonable accommodations to your employer.
3. Provide medical documentation that supports your need for specific accommodations.
4. Meet with HR and your supervisor to discuss potential accommodation options.
5. Work with your employer to identify effective accommodations that don't cause undue hardship.
6. Request a trial period to test proposed accommodations before making them permanent.
7. Document all communications about your accommodation request in writing.
8. Follow up regularly on the status of your accommodation implementation.
9. Know your company's policies on disability accommodation and grievance procedures.
10. Keep records of how accommodations affect your job performance and productivity.

CATEGORY 4: Educational Institution Accommodations
1. Contact your school's disability services office to begin the accommodation process.
2. Submit required medical documentation to support your accommodation request.
3. Meet with a disability services counselor to discuss your specific needs and challenges.
4. Work with the counselor to develop an accommodation plan tailored to your coursework.
5. Communicate with your professors about your approved accommodations each semester.
6. Request accommodations for testing, note-taking, or classroom participation as needed.
7. Explore assistive technology resources available through your school's disability services.
8. Know your rights regarding accessible housing and dining accommodations on campus.
9. Understand the process for requesting accommodations for field trips or study abroad programs.
10. Keep documentation of how your accommodations support your academic success.

CATEGORY 5: Public Access and Housing Accommodations
1. Research ADA accessibility requirements for public buildings and services you need to access.
2. Request reasonable modifications to housing rules or policies that create barriers for your disability.
3. Know your rights to accessible parking, entrance modifications, and service animal access.
4. Contact property managers or landlords in writing when requesting housing accommodations.
5. Research fair housing laws that protect your right to accessible and modified housing.
6. Document accessibility barriers you encounter in public places and report them to appropriate authorities.
7. Learn about your rights to accessible transportation and paratransit services.
8. Request sign language interpreters or other communication aids for public meetings or services.
9. Know how to file complaints with the Department of Housing and Urban Development for housing discrimination.
10. Connect with local independent living centers for advocacy and support with public access issues.
