CATEGORY 1: Program Research and Selection
1. Research graduate programs in your field of interest at different universities.
2. Compare admission requirements across your target schools.
3. Evaluate faculty research interests and expertise at each program.
4. Assess program rankings and reputation in your specific field.
5. Review curriculum structure and course offerings for each program.
6. Investigate funding opportunities and assistantship availability.
7. Examine graduation rates and job placement statistics.
8. Consider program location and cost of living factors.
9. Look into program culture and student life aspects.
10. Create a shortlist of 8-12 programs that align with your goals.

CATEGORY 2: Application Preparation
1. Request official transcripts from all previous institutions.
2. Register for and schedule required standardized tests (GRE, GMAT, etc.).
3. Identify potential recommenders and request letters of recommendation.
4. Draft your personal statement or statement of purpose.
5. Prepare a comprehensive CV or resume highlighting relevant experience.
6. Research and contact potential faculty advisors or mentors.
7. Gather writing samples or portfolio materials if required.
8. Complete online application forms for each program.
9. Pay application fees and submit all required documents.
10. Follow up to ensure all application materials were received.

CATEGORY 3: Financial Planning and Funding
1. Research tuition costs and fees for each program you're applying to.
2. Calculate total cost of attendance including living expenses.
3. Apply for federal financial aid by completing the FAFSA.
4. Search for external scholarships and grants in your field.
5. Investigate graduate assistantships and fellowship opportunities.
6. Consider part-time work options during graduate school.
7. Explore employer tuition assistance programs if applicable.
8. Create a budget for your graduate school years.
9. Compare financial aid packages from different schools.
10. Plan for loan repayment after graduation.

CATEGORY 4: Decision Making and Evaluation
1. Create a comparison spreadsheet of all programs you've been accepted to.
2. Attend virtual or in-person visit days at your top choice schools.
3. Connect with current students and recent graduates from each program.
4. Evaluate research fit with potential faculty advisors.
5. Consider long-term career goals and how each program aligns.
6. Assess the strength of alumni networks in your target industry.
7. Compare thesis versus non-thesis program options.
8. Weigh program prestige against personal fit and financial considerations.
9. Set a deadline for making your final decision.
10. Submit your enrollment deposit and decline other offers.

CATEGORY 5: Preparation and Transition
1. Secure housing near your chosen graduate school campus.
2. Arrange for health insurance coverage during graduate school.
3. Connect with your assigned academic advisor or program coordinator.
4. Register for orientation sessions and required graduate student meetings.
5. Purchase necessary textbooks and academic materials.
6. Set up financial aid disbursements and payment plans.
7. Join graduate student organizations and professional associations.
8. Prepare for any required qualifying exams or assessments.
9. Establish a study schedule and academic routine.
10. Build relationships with fellow incoming graduate students.
