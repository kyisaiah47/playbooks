CATEGORY 1: Garden Planning & Design
1. Measure your available garden space and note dimensions.
2. Test your soil pH using a home testing kit.
3. Observe which areas receive full sun, partial sun, or shade throughout the day.
4. Research your local hardiness zone and frost dates.
5. Sketch a basic layout of your garden space on paper.
6. Decide whether you want raised beds, in-ground planting, or containers.
7. Plan pathways and access routes through your garden.
8. Consider water access and plan irrigation or watering systems.
9. Research local building codes for any structures you want to add.
10. Set a realistic budget for your garden project.

CATEGORY 2: Plant Selection & Timing
1. Choose vegetables, herbs, or flowers that match your climate zone.
2. Create a planting calendar based on your last frost date.
3. Select plants that match your garden's sun and shade patterns.
4. Research companion planting combinations that grow well together.
5. Plan succession planting for continuous harvests throughout the season.
6. Choose disease-resistant varieties for your local area.
7. Calculate how many plants you need based on your space and family size.
8. Research which plants to start from seeds versus buying as transplants.
9. Plan for both cool-season and warm-season crops.
10. Select perennial plants that will return year after year.

CATEGORY 3: Soil Preparation & Setup
1. Remove weeds and grass from your designated garden area.
2. Add compost or organic matter to improve soil structure.
3. Till or dig the soil to the appropriate depth for your plants.
4. Install drainage solutions if your soil retains too much water.
5. Build or install raised beds if you've chosen that option.
6. Add mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7. Install support structures like trellises for climbing plants.
8. Set up irrigation systems or soaker hoses if needed.
9. Apply fertilizer based on your soil test results.
10. Create clear borders and edges for your garden beds.

CATEGORY 4: Planting & Maintenance
1. Start seeds indoors according to your planting calendar.
2. Transplant seedlings outdoors after the danger of frost has passed.
3. Water newly planted seeds and transplants consistently.
4. Thin overcrowded seedlings to give plants proper spacing.
5. Install plant labels to keep track of varieties and planting dates.
6. Prune and deadhead flowers to encourage continued blooming.
7. Monitor plants weekly for signs of pests or diseases.
8. Apply organic pest control methods when necessary.
9. Stake tall plants like tomatoes or sunflowers for support.
10. Harvest crops at their peak ripeness for best flavor.

CATEGORY 5: Seasonal Care & Planning Ahead
1. Keep a garden journal to track what works and what doesn't.
2. Save seeds from your best-performing plants for next year.
3. Compost fallen leaves and plant debris in the fall.
4. Protect tender plants from unexpected late or early frosts.
5. Plan crop rotation to prevent soil depletion and pest buildup.
6. Winterize your garden by cleaning up and covering beds.
7. Order seeds and plan next year's garden during winter months.
8. Maintain and clean your gardening tools regularly.
9. Expand successful areas and redesign problem spots for next season.
10. Connect with local gardening groups or extension services for ongoing advice.
