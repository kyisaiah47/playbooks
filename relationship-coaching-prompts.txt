CATEGORY 1: Self-Awareness & Personal Growth
1. Write down three personal patterns that repeatedly show up in your relationships.
2. Identify your core values and how they align with your relationship goals.
3. List five things you love about yourself that you bring to relationships.
4. Reflect on how your childhood experiences shaped your attachment style.
5. Complete a personality assessment and discuss the results with your partner.
6. Journal about your emotional triggers and what situations activate them.
7. Practice daily mindfulness meditation to increase self-awareness.
8. Identify your love language and communicate it to your partner.
9. Write a letter to your younger self about relationships and forgiveness.
10. Create a personal mission statement for how you want to show up in relationships.

CATEGORY 2: Communication Skills
1. Practice using "I" statements instead of "you" statements during disagreements.
2. Set aside 15 minutes daily for uninterrupted conversation with your partner.
3. Learn to pause and take three deep breaths before responding in heated moments.
4. Practice active listening by repeating back what your partner just said.
5. Express one appreciation to your partner every day for a week.
6. Have a conversation about your communication styles and preferences.
7. Practice asking open-ended questions instead of yes/no questions.
8. Share one vulnerable feeling or fear with your partner this week.
9. Learn to say "I need time to think about this" instead of reacting immediately.
10. Create a safe word or signal for when conversations become too intense.

CATEGORY 3: Conflict Resolution
1. Establish ground rules for how you'll handle disagreements moving forward.
2. Practice apologizing effectively by acknowledging impact, not just intent.
3. Identify the underlying need behind each person's position in your last argument.
4. Take a 20-minute cooling-off period before discussing heated topics.
5. Focus on solving one specific issue at a time instead of bringing up past grievances.
6. Practice compromising by finding a solution that gives each person something they want.
7. Schedule regular check-ins to address small issues before they become big problems.
8. Learn to agree to disagree on topics that aren't dealbreakers.
9. Write down three things you could do differently in your next disagreement.
10. Practice repair attempts by using humor or affection to de-escalate tension.

CATEGORY 4: Intimacy & Connection
1. Plan one weekly activity that you both enjoy and can do together.
2. Share three things you're grateful for about your partner each week.
3. Create a ritual for reconnecting after being apart during the day.
4. Schedule regular date nights without phones or distractions.
5. Practice physical affection daily, even if it's just holding hands for a minute.
6. Share one childhood memory or story your partner hasn't heard before.
7. Create a shared vision board for your relationship goals and dreams.
8. Take turns planning surprise gestures for each other monthly.
9. Practice being fully present during intimate conversations without multitasking.
10. Establish a bedtime routine that includes connecting with each other.

CATEGORY 5: Building Trust & Security
1. Follow through on small promises consistently to build trust over time.
2. Practice radical honesty by sharing something you've been hesitant to discuss.
3. Create transparency around finances, schedules, and social media usage.
4. Establish clear boundaries with friends, family, and ex-partners.
5. Have an honest conversation about past relationship wounds and how they affect you now.
6. Practice asking for what you need instead of expecting your partner to guess.
7. Create shared goals and check in on progress together monthly.
8. Be consistent in your words, actions, and emotions over time.
9. Admit when you're wrong and take responsibility for your mistakes quickly.
10. Build emotional safety by responding with empathy when your partner shares vulnerabilities.
