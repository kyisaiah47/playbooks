CATEGORY 1: ENGINE & MECHANICAL SYSTEMS
1. Check engine oil level and color before each boating trip.
2. Replace engine oil filter according to manufacturer's schedule.
3. Inspect belts and hoses for cracks, fraying, or loose connections.
4. Test battery terminals for corrosion and clean if necessary.
5. Check coolant level and top off with proper marine antifreeze.
6. Inspect propeller for dings, cracks, or fishing line wrapped around shaft.
7. Grease all fittings on steering system and engine mounts.
8. Test engine alarm systems and gauges before departing dock.
9. Check fuel lines and connections for leaks or deterioration.
10. Replace spark plugs according to engine manufacturer's recommendations.

CATEGORY 2: HULL & STRUCTURAL MAINTENANCE  
1. Inspect hull for cracks, blisters, or soft spots during haul-out.
2. Clean and wax the hull at least twice per boating season.
3. Check through-hull fittings and seacocks for proper operation.
4. Inspect and re-bed deck hardware if caulking shows signs of failure.
5. Test bilge pump operation and clean intake screen monthly.
6. Check all cleats and hardware for loose bolts or corrosion.
7. Inspect keel bolts and rudder attachments for tightness and wear.
8. Apply fresh bottom paint before launching each season.
9. Check and repair any gel coat chips or scratches immediately.
10. Inspect and maintain dock lines and fenders for wear and proper size.

CATEGORY 3: ELECTRICAL & ELECTRONICS
1. Test all navigation lights and replace burned-out bulbs immediately.
2. Check battery voltage and charging system with multimeter monthly.
3. Inspect all electrical connections for corrosion and tight connections.
4. Test VHF radio and GPS systems before each extended trip.
5. Check shore power connections and GFCI outlets for proper operation.
6. Inspect and clean battery terminals and apply protective spray.
7. Test automatic bilge pump float switch and manual override.
8. Check all fuses and spare fuse inventory in electrical panel.
9. Inspect wiring runs for chafing, cracking, or rodent damage.
10. Update chartplotter software and backup electronic charts annually.

CATEGORY 4: PLUMBING & WATER SYSTEMS
1. Check freshwater tank level and sanitize system seasonally.
2. Test all pumps including freshwater, raw water, and waste pumps.
3. Inspect all hoses and clamps for leaks or deterioration.
4. Check seacocks and through-hulls for proper operation and corrosion.
5. Drain and winterize plumbing system in freezing climates.
6. Test toilet operation and check holding tank level indicator.
7. Inspect sink drains and shower sump pumps for clogs.
8. Check hot water heater operation and inspect sacrificial anode.
9. Test all faucets and valves for proper operation and leaks.
10. Inspect and service water pressure accumulator tank annually.

CATEGORY 5: SAFETY & EMERGENCY EQUIPMENT
1. Check all life jackets for tears, broken zippers, and proper fit.
2. Test fire extinguishers monthly and recharge or replace as needed.
3. Inspect flares for expiration dates and replace before they expire.
4. Check first aid kit contents and replenish used or expired items.
5. Test emergency bilge pump and ensure backup power source works.
6. Inspect safety harnesses and tethers for wear or damage.
7. Check throwable flotation devices for Coast Guard approval markings.
8. Test emergency VHF radio and EPIRB beacon batteries annually.
9. Inspect and restock emergency tool kit and spare parts inventory.
10. Review and update emergency contact list and float plan procedures.
