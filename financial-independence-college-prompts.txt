CATEGORY 1: Budgeting & Expense Management
1. Track every expense for one month using a spreadsheet or budgeting app.
2. Create a monthly budget that allocates funds for necessities, savings, and fun money.
3. Compare meal plan costs versus cooking your own meals to find the most economical option.
4. Set up automatic transfers to savings immediately after receiving financial aid or paychecks.
5. Review and cancel unused subscriptions and recurring payments.
6. Find three ways to reduce your textbook costs this semester.
7. Calculate the true hourly wage of your part-time job after taxes and commute costs.
8. Establish a system for tracking shared expenses with roommates.
9. Create a semester-specific budget that accounts for one-time expenses like supplies.
10. Set weekly spending limits for discretionary purchases and stick to them.

CATEGORY 2: Income Generation & Work
1. Apply to five on-campus jobs that fit your class schedule.
2. Research and apply for three scholarships or grants this month.
3. Create a profile on freelancing platforms for skills you already have.
4. Negotiate your work-study hours to maximize earnings without affecting grades.
5. Start a small service business targeting fellow students' needs.
6. Apply for paid internships in your field of study for next semester.
7. Become a tutor for a subject you excel in through your campus tutoring center.
8. Sell unused items from your dorm room on campus marketplaces.
9. Research tax benefits and file your taxes to maximize education credits.
10. Create a resume highlighting transferable skills from coursework and activities.

CATEGORY 3: Debt Management & Credit Building
1. Calculate the total cost of your student loans including interest over the repayment period.
2. Make a plan to pay interest on unsubsidized loans while in school.
3. Apply for a secured credit card and set up automatic payment for one small recurring bill.
4. Research and compare private loan refinancing options for after graduation.
5. Create a debt avalanche or snowball plan for any existing credit card debt.
6. Set up autopay for all bills to avoid late fees and build payment history.
7. Check your credit report for free and dispute any errors you find.
8. Calculate how extra payments now could reduce your total loan repayment amount.
9. Research income-driven repayment plans and their long-term implications.
10. Open a no-fee checking account at a credit union instead of a big bank.

CATEGORY 4: Investing & Long-term Wealth
1. Open a high-yield savings account for your emergency fund.
2. Start investing $25 per month in a low-cost index fund through a brokerage app.
3. Research and open a Roth IRA to start retirement savings with tax advantages.
4. Learn about compound interest by calculating potential returns on monthly investments.
5. Set up a separate savings account for post-graduation moving expenses.
6. Research your future career's average starting salary and create a post-grad budget.
7. Attend a free financial literacy workshop offered by your university.
8. Start building a network of financially savvy peers for accountability and advice.
9. Create financial goals for 1, 5, and 10 years after graduation.
10. Research and understand your future employer's 401k matching policy.

CATEGORY 5: Smart Student Living
1. Find and apply for student discounts at five local businesses you frequent.
2. Organize a textbook exchange group with students in your major.
3. Plan grocery shopping around sales and create meals from bulk ingredients.
4. Set up a meal prep routine to avoid expensive last-minute food purchases.
5. Research free campus resources like gym, counseling, and career services.
6. Find free or cheap entertainment options in your college town.
7. Negotiate rent or look for cheaper housing options for next year.
8. Form a study group to share expensive course materials and resources.
9. Use campus transportation or bike instead of maintaining a car.
10. Apply for resident advisor positions to receive free or reduced housing.
