CATEGORY 1: Concept and Planning
1. Define your restaurant's cuisine type, target demographic, and unique selling proposition.
2. Research your local market competition and identify gaps you can fill.
3. Create a detailed business plan including financial projections and operational strategy.
4. Determine your restaurant's seating capacity, service style, and dining experience concept.
5. Develop your brand identity including restaurant name, logo, and visual design elements.
6. Calculate startup costs for equipment, renovation, inventory, and working capital needs.
7. Choose between franchising an established brand or creating an independent restaurant concept.
8. Outline your menu concept, pricing strategy, and signature dishes that will define your restaurant.
9. Research and select your ideal neighborhood location based on foot traffic and demographics.
10. Create a timeline for opening that includes all major milestones from planning to launch.

CATEGORY 2: Legal and Financial Setup
1. Register your business entity and obtain your federal tax identification number.
2. Apply for your food service license and liquor license if you plan to serve alcohol.
3. Secure adequate business insurance including general liability, property, and workers' compensation coverage.
4. Open a dedicated business bank account and establish accounting systems for tracking expenses and revenue.
5. Obtain all required health department permits and schedule initial inspections.
6. Apply for building permits if you plan to renovate or modify your restaurant space.
7. Secure financing through loans, investors, or personal funds to cover startup and operating costs.
8. Register for state and local taxes including sales tax collection and payroll tax obligations.
9. Obtain a business credit card and establish relationships with potential vendors and suppliers.
10. Create contracts and agreements for partnerships, investors, or key staff members.

CATEGORY 3: Location and Space Development
1. Scout potential locations considering rent costs, foot traffic, parking availability, and local zoning laws.
2. Negotiate lease terms including rent, security deposit, improvement allowances, and renewal options.
3. Hire an architect or designer to create floor plans that optimize seating, kitchen, and storage space.
4. Obtain building permits and schedule inspections for any construction or renovation work needed.
5. Install commercial kitchen equipment including ovens, refrigerators, prep tables, and ventilation systems.
6. Design and implement your dining room layout with tables, chairs, lighting, and decorative elements.
7. Set up point-of-sale systems, internet connectivity, and any technology needed for operations.
8. Install proper plumbing, electrical, and HVAC systems to meet commercial restaurant requirements.
9. Create adequate storage areas for dry goods, refrigerated items, cleaning supplies, and equipment.
10. Ensure your space meets ADA compliance requirements and all local fire safety regulations.

CATEGORY 4: Staffing and Operations
1. Develop job descriptions and hiring criteria for kitchen staff, servers, managers, and support roles.
2. Create an employee handbook covering policies, procedures, dress code, and workplace expectations.
3. Interview, hire, and conduct background checks on all staff members before opening.
4. Design comprehensive training programs for food preparation, customer service, and safety protocols.
5. Establish vendor relationships with food suppliers, beverage distributors, and equipment service providers.
6. Create standard operating procedures for food preparation, inventory management, and customer service.
7. Develop your complete menu with detailed recipes, portion sizes, and cost calculations for each dish.
8. Set up inventory management systems to track food costs, waste, and reorder schedules.
9. Establish cleaning schedules and sanitation procedures to maintain health department compliance.
10. Create employee scheduling systems and determine optimal staffing levels for different service periods.

CATEGORY 5: Marketing and Launch Preparation
1. Build a professional website with your menu, location, hours, and online reservation capabilities.
2. Create social media accounts and develop a content strategy to build anticipation before opening.
3. Design and print marketing materials including business cards, flyers, and promotional signage.
4. Plan a soft opening for friends, family, and local influencers to test operations and gather feedback.
5. Develop relationships with local food bloggers, restaurant critics, and community organizations.
6. Create a grand opening event strategy with special promotions, entertainment, or community partnerships.
7. Establish partnerships with food delivery apps like DoorDash, Uber Eats, or Grubhub if desired.
8. Design loyalty programs or promotional offers to encourage repeat customers and word-of-mouth marketing.
9. Plan your pricing strategy and create daily specials or seasonal menu items to drive traffic.
10. Prepare for opening day by conducting final staff training, equipment testing, and inventory stocking.
