CATEGORY 1: Career Transition and Employment
1. Research civilian job titles that match your military occupation specialty and transferable skills.
2. Create a civilian resume that translates military experience into business language employers understand.
3. Network with other veterans in your target industry through LinkedIn and veteran organizations.
4. Practice interviewing by recording yourself answering common questions about military-to-civilian transition.
5. Apply for jobs that are 70% aligned with your skills rather than waiting for perfect matches.
6. Attend job fairs specifically designed for veterans and military spouses in your area.
7. Consider using your GI Bill benefits for additional certifications or training in your chosen field.
8. Join professional associations related to your target career to build industry connections.
9. Update your LinkedIn profile with civilian-friendly descriptions of your military accomplishments.
10. Schedule informational interviews with professionals working in companies you want to join.

CATEGORY 2: Education and Skill Development
1. Research degree programs that align with your career goals and accept military credits for transfer.
2. Meet with a school counselor to understand how your military training translates to college credit hours.
3. Apply for the GI Bill and understand all education benefits available to you as a veteran.
4. Explore online certification programs in high-demand fields like cybersecurity, project management, or data analysis.
5. Join study groups or veteran student organizations at your chosen educational institution.
6. Create a realistic study schedule that balances coursework with family and work responsibilities.
7. Research scholarships specifically available to military veterans and their families.
8. Consider starting with community college courses to ease back into academic life.
9. Take advantage of tutoring services and academic support programs designed for veteran students.
10. Set up meetings with academic advisors to create a clear graduation timeline and course plan.

CATEGORY 3: Health and Wellness
1. Schedule a comprehensive health exam with the VA to establish your baseline health status.
2. Apply for VA disability benefits if you have service-connected injuries or conditions.
3. Research mental health resources specifically designed for veterans dealing with transition stress.
4. Establish a regular exercise routine that helps manage stress and maintains physical fitness.
5. Connect with other veterans through support groups to share experiences and coping strategies.
6. Learn about VA healthcare benefits and how to access medical services in your area.
7. Practice stress management techniques like meditation, deep breathing, or progressive muscle relaxation.
8. Create healthy sleep habits and address any sleep disorders that developed during military service.
9. Build a support network of family, friends, and fellow veterans who understand your transition journey.
10. Consider counseling or therapy to process the emotional aspects of leaving military life behind.

CATEGORY 4: Financial Planning and Benefits
1. Meet with a VA benefits counselor to understand all compensation and benefits you're entitled to receive.
2. Create a civilian budget that accounts for different pay schedules and benefit structures.
3. Research VA home loan benefits and determine if you're ready to purchase a house.
4. Open a civilian bank account and establish credit history separate from military financial institutions.
5. Review and update all insurance policies including life, health, auto, and disability coverage.
6. Start or increase contributions to retirement accounts like 401(k), IRA, or Thrift Savings Plan.
7. Research education funding options beyond the GI Bill including scholarships, grants, and student loans.
8. Create an emergency fund to cover 3-6 months of expenses during your transition period.
9. Understand how military retirement pay, disability compensation, and civilian income affect your taxes.
10. Meet with a financial advisor who has experience working with military veterans and their unique needs.

CATEGORY 5: Legal and Administrative Tasks
1. Obtain certified copies of your military records, including DD-214 and medical records.
2. Register with your state's Department of Veterans Affairs to access local veteran services.
3. Update your legal documents including will, power of attorney, and beneficiary information on all accounts.
4. Research veteran preference points for federal and state government job applications.
5. Apply for a veteran identification card to access discounts and services in your community.
6. Transfer military security clearances to civilian equivalents if applicable to your career path.
7. Update your voter registration and driver's license to reflect your new civilian address.
8. Research state-specific veteran benefits including property tax exemptions and license plate options.
9. Create a filing system for important documents including medical records, service records, and benefit paperwork.
10. Understand your rights as a veteran in the workplace including reasonable accommodations and anti-discrimination protections.
