CATEGORY 1: Planning & Research
1. Measure the dimensions of the room or area you want to renovate.
2. Set a realistic budget for your home improvement project.
3. Research local building codes and permit requirements for your project.
4. Create a prioritized list of improvements based on your needs and budget.
5. Get at least three quotes from different contractors for major work.
6. Research the best materials and finishes within your price range.
7. Create a timeline with realistic deadlines for each phase of your project.
8. Check your homeowner's insurance coverage for renovation work.
9. Plan for temporary living arrangements if major work will disrupt daily life.
10. Research seasonal considerations that might affect your project timing.

CATEGORY 2: Preparation & Setup
1. Clear out furniture and belongings from the work area.
2. Set up a temporary workspace with tools and materials organized.
3. Protect adjacent areas with plastic sheeting or drop cloths.
4. Turn off utilities like electricity, water, or gas to the work area if needed.
5. Order and confirm delivery dates for all materials before starting.
6. Schedule inspections required at different phases of the project.
7. Arrange for proper disposal of debris and old materials.
8. Set up adequate lighting and ventilation in the work area.
9. Create a daily cleanup routine to maintain safety and organization.
10. Establish clear communication channels with contractors and suppliers.

CATEGORY 3: Structural & Systems
1. Check and repair any foundation issues before starting cosmetic work.
2. Upgrade electrical wiring to meet current safety codes.
3. Replace old plumbing pipes and fixtures that show signs of wear.
4. Install or upgrade insulation to improve energy efficiency.
5. Repair or replace damaged drywall and structural framing.
6. Update HVAC systems for better heating and cooling efficiency.
7. Install new windows and doors to improve security and energy savings.
8. Address any moisture or mold issues before closing up walls.
9. Upgrade your electrical panel if it's outdated or insufficient.
10. Install proper ventilation in bathrooms and kitchens.

CATEGORY 4: Interior Finishes
1. Paint walls and ceilings with quality primer and paint.
2. Install new flooring that matches your lifestyle and budget.
3. Replace cabinet hardware with updated styles and finishes.
4. Install new light fixtures to improve both function and aesthetics.
5. Update bathroom fixtures like faucets, showerheads, and toilet seats.
6. Replace interior doors and trim for a fresh, cohesive look.
7. Install backsplashes in kitchen and bathroom areas.
8. Add built-in storage solutions to maximize space efficiency.
9. Replace old countertops with durable, attractive materials.
10. Install window treatments that provide privacy and style.

CATEGORY 5: Final Touches & Maintenance
1. Deep clean all surfaces and remove construction dust thoroughly.
2. Touch up paint and caulk any gaps or imperfections.
3. Install smoke detectors and carbon monoxide detectors as needed.
4. Program and test all new electrical and smart home devices.
5. Create a maintenance schedule for new appliances and systems.
6. Document warranty information and keep receipts organized.
7. Take final photos of completed work for insurance and records.
8. Schedule a final walkthrough with contractors to address any issues.
9. Update your home's value records for insurance and tax purposes.
10. Plan and execute a final decorating phase with furniture and accessories.
