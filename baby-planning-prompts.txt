CATEGORY 1: Preparing Your Body and Health
1. Schedule a preconception appointment with your healthcare provider to discuss your plans.
2. Start taking prenatal vitamins with folic acid at least one month before trying to conceive.
3. Update your immunizations and ensure you're current on recommended vaccines.
4. Begin tracking your menstrual cycle to understand your fertility window.
5. Quit smoking, limit alcohol consumption, and avoid recreational drugs.
6. Maintain a healthy weight through balanced diet and regular exercise.
7. Discuss any current medications with your doctor for pregnancy safety.
8. Get screened for sexually transmitted infections and other health conditions.
9. Start incorporating more folate-rich foods into your daily meals.
10. Reduce caffeine intake to recommended pregnancy-safe levels.

CATEGORY 2: Financial Planning and Insurance
1. Review your health insurance coverage for maternity and newborn care benefits.
2. Calculate the estimated costs of pregnancy, delivery, and first-year baby expenses.
3. Start a dedicated baby savings account and set up automatic monthly transfers.
4. Research your employer's parental leave policies and benefits.
5. Consider purchasing or increasing life insurance coverage for both parents.
6. Create a budget that accounts for reduced income during maternity leave.
7. Investigate flexible spending accounts or health savings accounts for medical expenses.
8. Research childcare costs in your area and start saving accordingly.
9. Review and update your will and estate planning documents.
10. Set up a 529 education savings plan for your future child's college expenses.

CATEGORY 3: Home and Safety Preparation
1. Identify which room will serve as the nursery and plan the layout.
2. Install smoke detectors and carbon monoxide detectors throughout your home.
3. Research and purchase a safe crib that meets current safety standards.
4. Baby-proof your home by securing cabinets, outlets, and sharp corners.
5. Create a checklist of essential baby gear and furniture you'll need.
6. Research and select a reliable car seat appropriate for newborns.
7. Stock up on basic baby supplies like diapers, wipes, and clothing.
8. Install safety gates at the top and bottom of staircases.
9. Remove or secure any poisonous plants and household chemicals.
10. Set up a comfortable nursing or feeding station in your home.

CATEGORY 4: Healthcare and Birth Planning
1. Research and interview potential obstetricians or midwives in your area.
2. Tour different hospitals or birthing centers to find your preferred location.
3. Understand your health insurance's network requirements for providers and facilities.
4. Create a birth plan outlining your preferences for labor and delivery.
5. Research pain management options available during labor and delivery.
6. Discuss genetic screening and testing options with your healthcare provider.
7. Find and interview potential pediatricians for your baby's care.
8. Learn about breastfeeding and consider taking a breastfeeding class.
9. Sign up for childbirth education classes in your area.
10. Understand the signs of labor and when to contact your healthcare provider.

CATEGORY 5: Support System and Lifestyle Changes
1. Have an open conversation with your partner about parenting roles and expectations.
2. Build a support network of family, friends, and other new or expectant parents.
3. Research local parenting groups, classes, or online communities to join.
4. Discuss work arrangements and career adjustments with your employer.
5. Plan how household responsibilities will be divided during pregnancy and after birth.
6. Consider hiring help for cleaning, meal prep, or other tasks after the baby arrives.
7. Create a list of people who can provide meals or assistance after delivery.
8. Discuss childcare preferences and research options like daycare, nanny, or family care.
9. Plan quality time with your partner before the baby arrives.
10. Prepare older children in the family for the arrival of their new sibling.
