CATEGORY 1: Finding the Right Roommate
1. Create a detailed roommate compatibility questionnaire covering lifestyle, cleanliness, and social preferences.
2. Post roommate listings on multiple platforms including Craigslist, Facebook groups, and roommate matching apps.
3. Schedule video calls with potential roommates to assess communication style and compatibility.
4. Ask for references from previous roommates or landlords to verify character and reliability.
5. Meet potential roommates in person at a neutral location before making any commitments.
6. Discuss deal-breakers upfront including pets, smoking, overnight guests, and noise levels.
7. Verify potential roommates' income and employment status to ensure they can afford rent.
8. Check social media profiles and online presence to get a better sense of their personality.
9. Arrange a trial period or temporary living arrangement before signing a long-term lease.
10. Trust your instincts and don't settle for someone who doesn't feel like a good fit.

CATEGORY 2: Legal and Financial Arrangements
1. Research local tenant rights and roommate laws in your city and state.
2. Determine whether both names will be on the lease or if one person will be the primary tenant.
3. Decide how rent, utilities, and shared expenses will be split and collected each month.
4. Set up a shared bank account or payment app for household expenses and bills.
5. Create a written roommate agreement covering rent, chores, guests, and house rules.
6. Obtain renters insurance that covers personal belongings and liability for both roommates.
7. Establish a security deposit plan and determine who holds the deposit funds.
8. Document the condition of the apartment with photos before moving in together.
9. Exchange emergency contact information and important personal details for safety.
10. Plan for lease renewal or exit strategies if the living arrangement doesn't work out.

CATEGORY 3: Setting Up the Living Space
1. Tour potential apartments together to ensure both roommates approve of the space.
2. Decide how bedrooms will be assigned if they're different sizes or have varying amenities.
3. Create a plan for furnishing common areas including living room, kitchen, and dining space.
4. Establish which personal items each roommate will bring versus what you'll buy together.
5. Set up shared household essentials like cleaning supplies, toilet paper, and kitchen basics.
6. Install necessary utilities including internet, cable, electricity, and water service.
7. Exchange keys and determine any access codes or building entry requirements.
8. Designate storage spaces for each roommate's personal belongings and seasonal items.
9. Set up a system for sharing kitchen appliances, cookware, and food storage areas.
10. Create designated spaces for personal items in shared bathrooms and common areas.

CATEGORY 4: Establishing House Rules and Communication
1. Create a chore schedule that fairly distributes cleaning responsibilities for all areas.
2. Establish quiet hours for studying, sleeping, and respectful living practices.
3. Set boundaries for having guests over including overnight visitors and party policies.
4. Agree on temperature settings, thermostat control, and utility usage preferences.
5. Create a communication system for addressing conflicts and household issues promptly.
6. Establish rules for sharing and labeling food in refrigerator and pantry spaces.
7. Decide on policies for borrowing personal items and using each other's belongings.
8. Set expectations for cleanliness standards in shared spaces and personal areas.
9. Create a system for notifying each other about schedule changes and extended absences.
10. Establish boundaries around romantic relationships and dating within the shared living space.

CATEGORY 5: Building a Positive Relationship
1. Schedule regular roommate check-ins to discuss any issues before they become major problems.
2. Plan occasional shared activities like cooking dinner together or watching movies.
3. Respect each other's privacy and personal space needs within the shared living arrangement.
4. Address conflicts directly and respectfully rather than letting resentment build up over time.
5. Be considerate about noise levels, especially during early morning and late evening hours.
6. Share household responsibilities equally and communicate openly about expectations and preferences.
7. Give each other advance notice about schedule changes, guests, or disruptions to normal routines.
8. Celebrate important milestones and holidays together to build a sense of community.
9. Maintain open and honest communication about finances, bills, and any money-related concerns.
10. Be willing to compromise and adapt house rules as needed to maintain harmony.
