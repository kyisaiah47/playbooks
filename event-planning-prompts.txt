CATEGORY 1: Initial Planning & Vision
1. Define the main purpose and goals for your event.
2. Set a realistic budget range for all event expenses.
3. Choose your preferred event date and backup options.
4. Determine the approximate number of guests you want to invite.
5. Decide on the overall theme or style for your event.
6. Research and select 3-5 potential venues that fit your vision.
7. Create a preliminary timeline working backwards from your event date.
8. Identify key people who will help you plan and execute the event.
9. List the must-have elements that are non-negotiable for your event.
10. Open a dedicated savings account or budget tracking system for event expenses.

CATEGORY 2: Venue & Logistics
1. Visit your top venue choices and ask detailed questions about services.
2. Compare venue pricing including all hidden fees and service charges.
3. Check venue availability for your preferred dates and backup options.
4. Review venue policies for decorations, catering, and vendor restrictions.
5. Book your chosen venue and get all agreements in writing.
6. Plan the layout and floor plan for optimal guest flow and experience.
7. Arrange transportation options for guests if needed.
8. Confirm parking availability and accessibility requirements.
9. Schedule a final venue walkthrough one week before the event.
10. Create a detailed venue setup timeline for the day of the event.

CATEGORY 3: Vendors & Services
1. Research and contact at least three caterers for menu options and pricing.
2. Book a photographer or videographer to capture your event memories.
3. Arrange for appropriate entertainment such as DJ, band, or speakers.
4. Order flowers, decorations, or hire a decorator for your theme.
5. Reserve rental items like tables, chairs, linens, and equipment.
6. Book any specialty services like bartenders, servers, or event coordinators.
7. Confirm delivery times and setup requirements with all vendors.
8. Create contracts with clear expectations and cancellation policies for each vendor.
9. Schedule final confirmations with all vendors one week before the event.
10. Prepare vendor contact information and timeline sheets for the event day.

CATEGORY 4: Guest Management
1. Create and finalize your complete guest list with accurate contact information.
2. Design and send save-the-date announcements for important events.
3. Create and mail formal invitations with RSVP instructions and deadlines.
4. Set up a system to track RSVPs and dietary restrictions or special needs.
5. Send reminder messages to guests who haven't responded by the deadline.
6. Plan seating arrangements based on guest relationships and preferences.
7. Create welcome bags or favors to thank guests for attending.
8. Arrange accommodations information for out-of-town guests.
9. Send final event details and directions to all confirmed guests.
10. Prepare a day-of guest list with contact information for check-in purposes.

CATEGORY 5: Final Preparations & Day-Of Coordination
1. Confirm all vendor deliveries and setup times for the week of the event.
2. Delegate specific responsibilities to family members or friends helping you.
3. Pack an emergency kit with supplies like stain remover, tape, and pain relievers.
4. Prepare all speeches, toasts, or presentations you'll be giving at the event.
5. Set up a detailed timeline for yourself and helpers on the day of the event.
6. Confirm weather backup plans if your event has outdoor elements.
7. Charge all devices and cameras you'll be using during the event.
8. Get a good night's sleep and prepare yourself mentally for the big day.
9. Arrive early to oversee setup and address any last-minute issues.
10. Designate someone to handle vendor payments and coordinate during the event so you can enjoy it.
