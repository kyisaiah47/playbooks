CATEGORY 1: Getting Started with Investing
1. Open a high-yield savings account to establish your emergency fund before investing.
2. Calculate your net worth by listing all assets and debts to understand your financial position.
3. Set up automatic transfers from checking to savings to build consistent saving habits.
4. Research and open a brokerage account with low fees and good educational resources.
5. Learn the difference between stocks, bonds, ETFs, and mutual funds through online courses.
6. Determine your risk tolerance by completing a financial risk assessment questionnaire.
7. Set specific investment goals with target amounts and timeframes for each objective.
8. Create a monthly budget that includes a dedicated investing allocation.
9. Pay off high-interest debt before investing to maximize your overall financial returns.
10. Start with a robo-advisor to automate your first investment decisions while learning.

CATEGORY 2: Building Your Investment Portfolio
1. Invest in a low-cost broad market index fund for instant diversification.
2. Allocate your portfolio based on your age using the "100 minus your age" rule for stocks.
3. Set up automatic monthly investments to dollar-cost average into your chosen funds.
4. Rebalance your portfolio quarterly to maintain your target asset allocation.
5. Research and invest in sector-specific ETFs to diversify beyond broad market exposure.
6. Consider adding international funds to reduce dependence on domestic markets.
7. Evaluate and invest in dividend-paying stocks for potential passive income.
8. Research individual companies' financial statements before buying their stock.
9. Limit individual stock picks to no more than 5-10% of your total portfolio.
10. Keep detailed records of all purchases, sales, and dividends for tax purposes.

CATEGORY 3: Retirement Planning and Tax-Advantaged Accounts
1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full company match if available.
2. Open and fund a Roth IRA if you meet the income requirements.
3. Maximize your annual IRA contribution limit each tax year.
4. Research if a traditional or Roth IRA is better for your current tax situation.
5. Set up automatic contributions to retirement accounts to ensure consistent funding.
6. Review and adjust your 401(k) investment options annually for optimal performance.
7. Consider a backdoor Roth IRA conversion if your income exceeds direct contribution limits.
8. Calculate how much you need to save monthly to reach your retirement income goal.
9. Take advantage of catch-up contributions if you're over 50 years old.
10. Create a withdrawal strategy for retirement that minimizes taxes and maximizes longevity.

CATEGORY 4: Advanced Investment Strategies
1. Learn about options trading basics before attempting any options strategies.
2. Research REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) for real estate exposure without direct ownership.
3. Consider value investing by analyzing companies trading below their intrinsic value.
4. Explore growth investing by identifying companies with strong earnings growth potential.
5. Research dividend growth investing by finding companies that consistently increase payouts.
6. Learn about asset allocation models like the three-fund portfolio for simplicity.
7. Consider tax-loss harvesting to offset gains with strategic selling of losing positions.
8. Research socially responsible investing (ESG) funds that align with your values.
9. Explore international investing through developed and emerging market funds.
10. Learn about alternative investments like commodities, precious metals, or cryptocurrency.

CATEGORY 5: Monitoring and Managing Your Investments
1. Review your investment performance monthly but avoid making emotional decisions.
2. Track your progress toward financial goals using spreadsheets or investment apps.
3. Stay informed about market trends through reputable financial news sources.
4. Reassess your risk tolerance annually as your life circumstances change.
5. Compare your portfolio's performance to relevant benchmarks like the S&P 500.
6. Keep investment costs low by regularly reviewing expense ratios and fees.
7. Avoid checking your account balance daily to prevent emotional trading decisions.
8. Create an investment policy statement to guide your long-term decision making.
9. Schedule annual reviews with a financial advisor if you use professional management.
10. Prepare for market volatility by having a written plan for different market scenarios.
