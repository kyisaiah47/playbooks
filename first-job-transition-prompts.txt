CATEGORY 1: Self-Assessment and Career Planning
1. Take a career assessment test to identify your strengths, interests, and values.
2. Research 5-10 career paths that align with your degree or interests.
3. Write down your short-term and long-term career goals.
4. Identify the skills gap between what you have and what employers want.
5. Create a personal mission statement for your career direction.
6. List your top 5 non-negotiable job requirements (salary, location, culture, etc.).
7. Shadow a professional in your field of interest for a day.
8. Schedule informational interviews with 3 people in your target industry.
9. Assess your financial needs to determine your minimum acceptable salary.
10. Create a timeline for your job search with specific milestones.

CATEGORY 2: Skill Development and Education
1. Identify 3 technical skills most in-demand for your target role.
2. Complete an online course or certification relevant to your field.
3. Practice coding, writing, or other job-specific skills for 30 minutes daily.
4. Join a professional association in your industry.
5. Attend a workshop or webinar related to your career interests.
6. Find a mentor who can guide your professional development.
7. Create a portfolio showcasing your best academic or personal projects.
8. Learn a new software tool commonly used in your target industry.
9. Improve your public speaking skills by joining Toastmasters or similar group.
10. Take on a volunteer project that builds relevant experience.

CATEGORY 3: Resume and Application Materials
1. Write a compelling professional summary for your resume.
2. Tailor your resume for each specific job application.
3. Create a master resume with all your experiences and achievements.
4. Write 5 different cover letter templates for various job types.
5. Get your resume reviewed by a career counselor or industry professional.
6. Create an online portfolio or personal website.
7. Develop a 30-second elevator pitch about yourself.
8. List and quantify all your achievements from internships, projects, and activities.
9. Create a LinkedIn profile that matches your resume.
10. Practice filling out online job applications efficiently.

CATEGORY 4: Job Search and Networking
1. Set up job alerts on 5 major job search websites.
2. Apply to 5 jobs every week consistently.
3. Attend 2 networking events or career fairs each month.
4. Connect with 10 new professionals on LinkedIn weekly.
5. Follow up with recruiters and hiring managers after applications.
6. Research companies you want to work for and add them to your target list.
7. Reach out to your college alumni network for job referrals.
8. Ask professors, supervisors, and colleagues for job recommendations.
9. Create a spreadsheet to track all your job applications and follow-ups.
10. Practice networking conversations and prepare questions to ask contacts.

CATEGORY 5: Interview Preparation and Job Acceptance
1. Research common interview questions for your field and practice answers.
2. Prepare 5 thoughtful questions to ask interviewers.
3. Practice interviewing with friends or family members.
4. Research each company thoroughly before every interview.
5. Choose and prepare professional interview outfits.
6. Practice describing your projects and experiences using the STAR method.
7. Prepare salary negotiation talking points and research market rates.
8. Send thank-you notes within 24 hours after each interview.
9. Create a list of professional references with updated contact information.
10. Practice accepting or declining job offers professionally.
