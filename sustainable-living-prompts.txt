CATEGORY 1: Energy & Home Efficiency
1. Replace all incandescent bulbs with LED lights throughout your home.
2. Install a programmable thermostat to optimize heating and cooling schedules.
3. Seal air leaks around windows and doors with weatherstripping or caulk.
4. Unplug electronics and appliances when not in use to eliminate phantom energy draw.
5. Switch to a renewable energy provider or install solar panels if feasible.
6. Use cold water for washing clothes whenever possible to reduce energy consumption.
7. Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators to reduce hot water usage.
8. Insulate your water heater and set the temperature to 120°F or lower.
9. Use natural lighting during the day instead of turning on artificial lights.
10. Consider upgrading to energy-efficient appliances when replacements are needed.

CATEGORY 2: Waste Reduction & Recycling
1. Start composting organic kitchen scraps and yard waste in your backyard or with a countertop composter.
2. Bring reusable bags to all grocery stores and shopping trips.
3. Replace single-use water bottles with a reusable water bottle you carry daily.
4. Buy products with minimal or recyclable packaging whenever possible.
5. Donate or sell items you no longer need instead of throwing them away.
6. Learn your local recycling guidelines and properly sort recyclable materials.
7. Repurpose glass jars and containers for food storage or organization.
8. Use both sides of paper and opt for digital receipts and bills.
9. Start a neighborhood tool-sharing program to reduce individual ownership needs.
10. Choose products made from recycled materials when making new purchases.

CATEGORY 3: Sustainable Food & Diet
1. Start a small herb or vegetable garden in your yard, balcony, or windowsill.
2. Reduce meat consumption by having at least two plant-based meals per week.
3. Shop at local farmers markets to support regional agriculture and reduce transportation emissions.
4. Plan your meals weekly to minimize food waste and unnecessary purchases.
5. Store fruits and vegetables properly to extend their freshness and reduce spoilage.
6. Choose organic produce for the "Dirty Dozen" fruits and vegetables when possible.
7. Cook more meals at home instead of ordering takeout with disposable packaging.
8. Buy in bulk to reduce packaging waste and often save money on staples.
9. Learn to preserve seasonal produce through freezing, canning, or dehydrating.
10. Start eating more locally-produced and seasonal foods available in your area.

CATEGORY 4: Transportation & Mobility
1. Walk or bike for trips under 2 miles instead of driving when weather permits.
2. Use public transportation, carpool, or rideshare for longer commutes and trips.
3. Combine multiple errands into one trip to reduce total driving time and fuel consumption.
4. Maintain proper tire pressure and regular vehicle maintenance for optimal fuel efficiency.
5. Consider working from home one or more days per week if your job allows it.
6. Plan vacations closer to home or choose destinations accessible by train or bus.
7. Explore car-sharing services for occasional use instead of owning a second vehicle.
8. Walk or bike to nearby restaurants, cafes, and entertainment venues.
9. Research electric or hybrid vehicle options when your current car needs replacement.
10. Use video conferencing for meetings that would otherwise require travel.

CATEGORY 5: Conscious Consumption & Lifestyle
1. Buy quality items that will last longer rather than cheaper alternatives that need frequent replacement.
2. Research companies' environmental practices before making purchases to support sustainable businesses.
3. Borrow or rent items you'll only use occasionally instead of buying them.
4. Choose clothing made from sustainable materials and shop at thrift stores when possible.
5. Use natural cleaning products or make your own with simple ingredients like vinegar and baking soda.
6. Replace disposable items with reusable alternatives like cloth napkins and rechargeable batteries.
7. Cancel subscriptions and memberships you don't actively use to reduce waste and clutter.
8. Choose experiences over material gifts for birthdays and holidays.
9. Learn basic repair skills to fix items instead of automatically replacing them.
10. Support local businesses and services to reduce shipping emissions and strengthen your community.
