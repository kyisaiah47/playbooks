CATEGORY 1: Debt Assessment & Planning
1. List all your debts including balances, interest rates, and minimum payments.
2. Calculate your total debt amount across all accounts.
3. Determine your debt-to-income ratio by dividing total monthly debt payments by monthly income.
4. Choose between debt avalanche (highest interest first) or debt snowball (smallest balance first) method.
5. Set a realistic target date for becoming debt-free.
6. Create a monthly debt payment budget that includes all minimum payments plus extra.
7. Identify which debts are tax-deductible (like student loans or mortgages).
8. Research debt consolidation options if you have multiple high-interest debts.
9. Calculate how much interest you'll save by paying off debts early.
10. Write down your specific reasons for wanting to become debt-free for motivation.

CATEGORY 2: Budget & Expense Management
1. Track every expense for one full month to understand your spending patterns.
2. Create a zero-based budget where every dollar has a specific purpose.
3. Identify three non-essential expenses you can eliminate this month.
4. Set up automatic transfers to move extra money toward debt payments.
5. Review and negotiate monthly subscriptions to reduce recurring costs.
6. Plan meals in advance to reduce food spending and avoid impulse purchases.
7. Implement a 24-hour waiting period before making any non-essential purchases.
8. Use cash envelopes for discretionary spending categories to stay within limits.
9. Find free or low-cost alternatives for entertainment and social activities.
10. Review your budget monthly and adjust categories based on actual spending.

CATEGORY 3: Income Enhancement
1. Ask your employer for a raise or promotion within the next 90 days.
2. Start a side hustle using skills you already have (tutoring, freelancing, crafts).
3. Sell items you no longer need through online marketplaces or garage sales.
4. Look for higher-paying job opportunities in your field.
5. Offer to work overtime or pick up extra shifts when available.
6. Rent out a spare room, parking space, or storage area for extra income.
7. Participate in the gig economy (rideshare, food delivery, task services).
8. Monetize a hobby by selling products or services related to your interests.
9. Apply for temporary or seasonal work during busy periods.
10. Claim all eligible tax deductions and credits to maximize your refund.

CATEGORY 4: Debt Payment Strategy
1. Make the minimum payment on all debts to avoid late fees and penalties.
2. Apply any extra money to your target debt (highest interest or smallest balance).
3. Set up automatic payments to ensure you never miss a due date.
4. Pay more than the minimum on at least one debt every month.
5. Use windfalls like tax refunds, bonuses, or gifts entirely for debt reduction.
6. Consider making biweekly payments instead of monthly to reduce interest.
7. Round up all debt payments to the nearest $50 or $100 for faster progress.
8. Negotiate with creditors for lower interest rates or payment plans.
9. Avoid taking on any new debt while paying off existing balances.
10. Celebrate small victories when you pay off individual debts completely.

CATEGORY 5: Financial Habits & Emergency Planning
1. Build a starter emergency fund of $1,000 before aggressively paying off debt.
2. Open a separate savings account specifically for your emergency fund.
3. Track your credit score monthly to monitor improvement as you pay down debt.
4. Create a debt payoff visual tracker to maintain motivation and see progress.
5. Find an accountability partner or join a debt payoff support group.
6. Read one personal finance book or article each month to stay motivated.
7. Practice saying "no" to social spending that doesn't align with your debt goals.
8. Set up alerts on your phone or calendar for all debt payment due dates.
9. Review your debt payoff plan quarterly and adjust as needed.
10. Plan how you'll redirect debt payments to savings once you're debt-free.
