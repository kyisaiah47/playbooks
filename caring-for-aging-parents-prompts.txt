CATEGORY 1: Health & Medical Management
1. Schedule annual physical exams and wellness checkups with your parent's primary care doctor.
2. Create a comprehensive list of all current medications, dosages, and prescribing physicians.
3. Research and interview potential geriatricians who specialize in senior healthcare.
4. Organize all medical records, insurance cards, and health directives in one accessible location.
5. Accompany your parent to important medical appointments to ask questions and take notes.
6. Install pill organizers or medication reminder systems to ensure proper dosing.
7. Discuss advance directives, living wills, and healthcare proxy designations with your parent.
8. Research Medicare supplement plans and prescription drug coverage options.
9. Create an emergency medical information card with key health details and emergency contacts.
10. Establish relationships with specialists like cardiologists, ophthalmologists, or orthopedists as needed.

CATEGORY 2: Safety & Home Environment
1. Conduct a comprehensive home safety assessment to identify fall risks and hazards.
2. Install grab bars, non-slip mats, and improved lighting in bathrooms and stairways.
3. Remove or secure loose rugs, electrical cords, and clutter from walkways.
4. Set up a medical alert system or emergency response device for urgent situations.
5. Ensure smoke detectors, carbon monoxide detectors, and security systems are functioning properly.
6. Evaluate the need for mobility aids like walkers, canes, or stairlifts.
7. Consider smart home technology like automated lighting or voice-activated assistants.
8. Review and update home insurance policies to reflect any modifications or equipment.
9. Create an emergency evacuation plan and practice it with your parent.
10. Research local handyman services or contractors for ongoing home maintenance needs.

CATEGORY 3: Financial & Legal Planning
1. Gather and organize all financial documents, including bank statements, investment accounts, and tax records.
2. Meet with an elder law attorney to discuss wills, trusts, and estate planning options.
3. Establish power of attorney for financial and healthcare decisions if appropriate.
4. Review and update beneficiaries on all retirement accounts, insurance policies, and investments.
5. Create a comprehensive budget that accounts for increasing healthcare and care costs.
6. Research long-term care insurance options and coverage for potential future needs.
7. Investigate available senior discounts, benefits, and government assistance programs.
8. Set up automatic bill pay systems to ensure utilities and important payments are never missed.
9. Consult with a financial advisor about retirement income strategies and asset protection.
10. Document all financial account information, passwords, and important contact numbers in a secure location.

CATEGORY 4: Social & Emotional Support
1. Help your parent maintain regular contact with friends, family members, and social networks.
2. Research local senior centers, community programs, and social activities for engagement opportunities.
3. Consider counseling or support groups for both you and your parent to process emotions and stress.
4. Schedule regular family visits, phone calls, or video chats to maintain close connections.
5. Encourage your parent to pursue hobbies, interests, or volunteer opportunities that bring joy.
6. Join a caregiver support group to connect with others facing similar challenges.
7. Plan meaningful activities and outings that your parent can still safely enjoy.
8. Address signs of depression, anxiety, or social isolation with professional help when needed.
9. Create memory books, photo albums, or recording projects to preserve family history and stories.
10. Establish open communication about your parent's wishes, fears, and preferences for their care.

CATEGORY 5: Care Coordination & Daily Living
1. Assess your parent's ability to perform activities of daily living like bathing, dressing, and meal preparation.
2. Research and interview potential home care agencies or individual caregivers if assistance is needed.
3. Create daily, weekly, and monthly schedules for medications, appointments, and essential tasks.
4. Establish grocery delivery services, meal preparation assistance, or senior meal programs.
5. Coordinate transportation solutions for medical appointments, errands, and social activities.
6. Research adult day programs or respite care services to provide breaks for primary caregivers.
7. Evaluate different levels of care options including assisted living, memory care, or skilled nursing facilities.
8. Create a communication system among family members and caregivers to share updates and coordinate care.
9. Develop contingency plans for emergencies, hospitalizations, or sudden changes in care needs.
10. Regularly reassess your parent's changing needs and adjust care plans and resources accordingly.
