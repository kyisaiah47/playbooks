CATEGORY 1: Treatment Planning & Research
1. Research your specific medical condition using reputable sources like Mayo Clinic or WebMD.
2. Write down all questions you want to ask your doctor before your next appointment.
3. Create a list of your current symptoms and track when they occur.
4. Research different treatment options available for your condition.
5. Get a second opinion from another qualified medical professional.
6. Look into clinical trials that might be relevant to your condition.
7. Research the success rates and side effects of recommended treatments.
8. Create a timeline of your medical history to share with healthcare providers.
9. Investigate whether your insurance covers the recommended treatments.
10. Research the credentials and experience of specialists you're considering.

CATEGORY 2: Medical Team & Communication
1. Schedule an appointment with your primary care physician to discuss your concerns.
2. Ask your doctor for referrals to specialists who can help with your condition.
3. Prepare a list of all medications and supplements you're currently taking.
4. Request copies of all your medical records and test results.
5. Bring a family member or friend to important medical appointments for support.
6. Ask your doctor to explain medical terms you don't understand.
7. Request written instructions for any treatments or procedures.
8. Establish a relationship with a patient advocate at your hospital or clinic.
9. Get contact information for after-hours medical questions or emergencies.
10. Ask about the best way to communicate with your healthcare team between visits.

CATEGORY 3: Insurance & Financial Planning
1. Contact your insurance company to understand what treatments are covered.
2. Request pre-authorization for any expensive treatments or procedures.
3. Ask about payment plans or financial assistance programs at your medical facility.
4. Keep detailed records of all medical expenses for tax purposes.
5. Research whether you qualify for disability benefits during treatment.
6. Set up a separate savings account for medical expenses not covered by insurance.
7. Understand your insurance deductible and out-of-pocket maximum limits.
8. Get written estimates for all planned procedures and treatments.
9. Ask about generic medication alternatives to reduce prescription costs.
10. Look into supplemental insurance options that might help with additional costs.

CATEGORY 4: Treatment Preparation & Management
1. Fill all prescribed medications and understand how to take them properly.
2. Schedule all necessary medical appointments and procedures.
3. Arrange for transportation to and from medical appointments and treatments.
4. Prepare your home environment for recovery after treatments or procedures.
5. Stock up on comfort items like heating pads, pillows, and easy-to-prepare foods.
6. Create a medication schedule and tracking system to ensure proper dosing.
7. Arrange for help with daily tasks during your treatment period.
8. Pack a hospital bag with essential items if an overnight stay is required.
9. Complete all pre-treatment requirements like fasting or stopping certain medications.
10. Inform your employer about your treatment schedule and any time off needed.

CATEGORY 5: Recovery & Long-term Management
1. Follow all post-treatment instructions provided by your healthcare team.
2. Schedule and attend all follow-up appointments as recommended.
3. Monitor your recovery progress and report any concerning symptoms immediately.
4. Gradually return to normal activities as cleared by your medical team.
5. Join a support group for people with your condition or treatment experience.
6. Maintain a healthy lifestyle with proper nutrition and appropriate exercise.
7. Keep a journal of your recovery progress and any side effects you experience.
8. Update your emergency contacts and medical information documents.
9. Develop a long-term monitoring plan with your healthcare providers.
10. Create an action plan for managing your condition if symptoms return or worsen.
