CATEGORY 1: Business Planning & Strategy
1. Write a one-page business plan outlining your core product or service offering.
2. Research three direct competitors and analyze their pricing strategies.
3. Define your target customer demographics and create a customer persona.
4. Set three specific, measurable business goals for your first year.
5. Choose a business structure (LLC, corporation, sole proprietorship) and register it.
6. Create a simple monthly budget including startup costs and ongoing expenses.
7. Develop a unique value proposition that differentiates you from competitors.
8. Research and select a business name, then check domain availability.
9. Write down your mission statement in one clear sentence.
10. Create a basic timeline for launching your business within the next 90 days.

CATEGORY 2: Legal & Administrative Setup
1. Obtain all necessary business licenses and permits for your industry and location.
2. Register for a federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) with the IRS.
3. Open a dedicated business bank account separate from your personal finances.
4. Purchase appropriate business insurance coverage for your industry and risks.
5. Set up a basic bookkeeping system to track income and expenses.
6. Create standardized contracts or service agreements for your customers.
7. Establish a simple filing system for important business documents and receipts.
8. Register your business name and trademark if applicable to your industry.
9. Set up a payroll system if you plan to hire employees within the first year.
10. Create basic business policies for returns, refunds, and customer service.

CATEGORY 3: Marketing & Customer Acquisition
1. Create professional business cards with your contact information and value proposition.
2. Build a simple website or landing page showcasing your products or services.
3. Set up social media profiles on two platforms where your customers spend time.
4. Write and distribute a press release announcing your business launch to local media.
5. Create a referral program offering incentives for customers who bring new business.
6. Design and implement a simple email newsletter to stay connected with customers.
7. Network with five local business owners or potential partners in your community.
8. Develop a content calendar with weekly posts that provide value to your audience.
9. Create promotional materials like flyers or brochures for your target market.
10. Launch a grand opening event or special promotion to attract your first customers.

CATEGORY 4: Operations & Systems
1. Set up a workspace that allows you to efficiently serve customers and manage tasks.
2. Create standard operating procedures for your most important business processes.
3. Establish reliable supplier relationships and backup options for key materials or services.
4. Implement a customer relationship management system to track leads and sales.
5. Set up efficient inventory management if you sell physical products.
6. Create a daily routine that balances customer service with business development tasks.
7. Establish clear business hours and communication channels for customer inquiries.
8. Set up automatic backup systems for important business data and files.
9. Create quality control checklists to ensure consistent service or product delivery.
10. Develop contingency plans for common business disruptions or challenges.

CATEGORY 5: Financial Management & Growth
1. Set up monthly financial reviews to track revenue, expenses, and profit margins.
2. Establish an emergency fund covering three months of business operating expenses.
3. Create a pricing strategy that covers costs while remaining competitive in your market.
4. Monitor key performance indicators like customer acquisition cost and lifetime value.
5. Research potential funding options such as small business loans or investor opportunities.
6. Set aside money each month for business taxes and quarterly tax payments.
7. Track and analyze which marketing efforts generate the best return on investment.
8. Create a plan for reinvesting profits back into business growth and improvements.
9. Establish relationships with business mentors, advisors, or professional service providers.
10. Develop a clear exit strategy or succession plan for your long-term business goals.
