CATEGORY 1: Immediate Financial Assessment
1. Calculate your current monthly expenses and identify which are essential versus optional.
2. Review your savings account balance and determine how many months of expenses it covers.
3. List all sources of income including unemployment benefits, severance, or part-time work.
4. Contact your bank to discuss potential loan deferments or payment modifications.
5. Research your state's unemployment insurance eligibility requirements and application process.
6. Create a bare-bones emergency budget that covers only essential expenses.
7. Inventory any assets you could liquidate if necessary without major life impact.
8. Review your health insurance options including COBRA continuation coverage.
9. Contact creditors to explain your situation and negotiate temporary payment plans.
10. Set up automatic transfers to prevent overdraft fees on essential accounts.

CATEGORY 2: Job Search Strategy
1. Update your resume with your most recent position and quantifiable achievements.
2. Create or refresh your LinkedIn profile with a professional photo and current experience.
3. Identify 10-15 companies in your industry that you'd like to work for.
4. Set up job alerts on major job boards for positions matching your skills.
5. Reach out to three former colleagues or managers to inform them you're job searching.
6. Research salary ranges for your target positions in your geographic area.
7. Practice your 30-second elevator pitch explaining your background and career goals.
8. Identify any skill gaps for your desired roles and create a plan to address them.
9. Apply to at least 5 relevant job postings each week with customized cover letters.
10. Schedule time for networking activities like industry events or informational interviews.

CATEGORY 3: Mental Health and Wellbeing
1. Establish a daily routine that includes regular wake-up and sleep times.
2. Set aside 30 minutes daily for physical exercise or outdoor activity.
3. Connect with one friend or family member each day for emotional support.
4. Limit news and social media consumption to reduce anxiety and comparison.
5. Practice a stress-reduction technique like deep breathing or meditation for 10 minutes daily.
6. Join a job seekers support group either online or in your community.
7. Maintain personal hygiene and dress professionally even when working from home.
8. Engage in one hobby or creative activity that brings you joy each week.
9. Consider speaking with a counselor or therapist about job loss stress.
10. Write down three things you're grateful for each day to maintain perspective.

CATEGORY 4: Skill Development and Career Growth
1. Identify the top 3 skills most requested in job postings for your target role.
2. Enroll in one free online course related to your industry or desired career path.
3. Update your professional certifications or research which ones would benefit your career.
4. Volunteer for a nonprofit organization to gain experience and expand your network.
5. Start or contribute to a portfolio project that demonstrates your key abilities.
6. Read industry publications or blogs to stay current on trends and developments.
7. Attend virtual webinars or conferences in your field to learn and network.
8. Practice interviewing by recording yourself answering common interview questions.
9. Learn a new technology or software program commonly used in your industry.
10. Seek feedback from mentors or career counselors on your job search approach.

CATEGORY 5: Long-term Planning and Recovery
1. Reassess your career goals and determine if this is an opportunity to change direction.
2. Build an emergency fund goal of 6-8 months of expenses to prevent future financial stress.
3. Create a timeline for your job search with weekly and monthly milestones.
4. Research alternative income streams like freelancing or consulting in your field.
5. Develop a professional network maintenance plan for ongoing relationship building.
6. Consider relocating to areas with better job opportunities in your industry.
7. Plan how you'll negotiate salary and benefits when you receive job offers.
8. Reflect on lessons learned from your previous job to avoid similar situations.
9. Create a career development plan with specific goals for the next 2-3 years.
10. Establish systems to regularly update your resume and LinkedIn profile going forward.
