### Professional Credibility Tests
- Would horticulturists and master gardeners recommend this resource?
- Does this include insights known primarily to plant science professionals and experienced growers?

- Does this solve real challenges gardeners face in plant cultivation and garden management?
- Would this prevent common gardening mistakes while optimizing plant health and garden productivity?

## Content Differentiation Requirements

### Never Create Generic Content About
- Basic gardening advice available in countless gardening websites and general plant care guides
- One-size-fits-all approaches ignoring climate zone differences and local growing conditions
- Oversimplified plant care that doesn't address soil health and ecosystem considerations
- Generic plant recommendations without regional specificity and ecological awareness

### Always Create Definitive Resources For
- Evidence-based horticulture with current plant science knowledge and sustainable practices
- Region-appropriate approaches that address specific climate zones and local growing conditions
- Comprehensive cultivation guidance that ensures plant health while promoting ecological gardening
- Science-based instruction that integrates plant biology with practical growing techniques
- Sustainability-focused practices that build soil health and support beneficial ecosystems

## Success Validation for Gardening and Horticulture Articles

**Each article must pass these tests:**
1. **Horticultural Standards**: Would professional horticulturists and plant scientists endorse this guidance?
2. **Scientific Accuracy**: Does this reflect current understanding of plant biology and growing science?
3. **Ecological Responsibility**: Does this promote sustainable practices and ecosystem health?
4. **Regional Relevance**: Does this address specific growing conditions and climate considerations?
5. **Long-term Success**: Does this build gardening knowledge that ensures ongoing plant health and garden sustainability?

**The Ultimate Test**: If someone could only read one resource about this gardening and horticulture topic, would this guide them to successfully grow healthy plants while building sustainable garden ecosystems that support both their goals and environmental health through science-based, regionally-appropriate practices?
