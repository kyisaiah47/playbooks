CATEGORY 1: Research & Preparation
1. Research the company's mission, values, and recent news to understand their culture and priorities.
2. Identify 3-5 key skills mentioned in the internship job description and assess your current proficiency.
3. Create a list of 10 companies in your target industry that offer internship programs.
4. Review sample internship interview questions and prepare 2-3 specific examples for each.
5. Update your LinkedIn profile with relevant coursework, projects, and skills.
6. Request transcripts and gather any required documents for applications.
7. Find and connect with current or former interns at your target companies on LinkedIn.
8. Research the typical internship timeline and application deadlines for your industry.
9. Identify 2-3 professional references who can speak to your work ethic and abilities.
10. Create a master list of all application requirements across different companies.

CATEGORY 2: Application Materials
1. Tailor your resume to highlight relevant coursework, projects, and experience for each application.
2. Write a compelling cover letter that connects your goals with the company's mission.
3. Create a portfolio showcasing your best academic projects or personal work.
4. Develop a 30-second elevator pitch explaining why you're interested in this field.
5. Proofread all application materials for grammar, spelling, and formatting errors.
6. Ask a mentor or career counselor to review your resume and provide feedback.
7. Customize your resume keywords to match each job description.
8. Write sample answers to common application essay questions.
9. Create a professional email signature for all internship-related correspondence.
10. Save all application materials in a organized digital folder with version control.

CATEGORY 3: Networking & Outreach
1. Attend virtual or in-person career fairs to meet recruiters and learn about opportunities.
2. Join professional associations or student organizations related to your field of interest.
3. Reach out to alumni from your school who work in your target industry.
4. Schedule informational interviews with professionals in your desired field.
5. Follow your target companies on social media and engage with their content professionally.
6. Attend industry webinars, workshops, or conferences to expand your knowledge and network.
7. Send personalized connection requests to recruiters and employees at target companies.
8. Participate in online forums or communities related to your industry.
9. Ask professors if they have industry connections who might offer advice or opportunities.
10. Follow up with new connections within 24-48 hours of meeting them.

CATEGORY 4: Interview Preparation
1. Practice your responses to behavioral interview questions using the STAR method.
2. Research your interviewers on LinkedIn and company websites before the meeting.
3. Prepare thoughtful questions to ask about the role, team, and company culture.
4. Conduct mock interviews with friends, family, or career services staff.
5. Test your technology and internet connection before virtual interviews.
6. Choose professional interview attire and ensure it fits properly.
7. Practice explaining your resume and key experiences in 2-3 minutes.
8. Prepare specific examples that demonstrate leadership, problem-solving, and teamwork.
9. Research common technical questions or case studies relevant to your field.
10. Plan your route and timing if attending an in-person interview.

CATEGORY 5: Follow-up & Decision Making
1. Send a personalized thank-you email within 24 hours of each interview.
2. Follow up on application status if you haven't heard back within the stated timeframe.
3. Keep detailed notes about each interview and company for future reference.
4. Continue networking and applying while waiting for responses from other companies.
5. Negotiate start dates, compensation, or other terms if you receive an offer.
6. Ask for feedback from companies that reject your application to improve future efforts.
7. Compare multiple offers based on learning opportunities, mentorship, and career goals.
8. Notify other companies promptly once you've accepted an offer.
9. Maintain relationships with recruiters and interviewers even after the process ends.
10. Reflect on your internship search experience and document lessons learned for next time.
