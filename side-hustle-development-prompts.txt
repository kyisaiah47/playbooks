CATEGORY 1: Business Idea & Market Research
1. Brainstorm three potential side hustles based on your existing skills and interests.
2. Research your target audience by surveying 10 potential customers about their pain points.
3. Analyze three direct competitors to understand their pricing, services, and customer reviews.
4. Validate your business idea by pre-selling to at least 5 customers before full launch.
5. Create a simple one-page business model canvas outlining your value proposition.
6. Test your minimum viable product with a small group of beta users.
7. Research market size and demand using Google Trends and industry reports.
8. Identify your unique selling proposition that differentiates you from competitors.
9. Conduct interviews with 5 people in your target market to understand their needs.
10. Create a simple survey to gather feedback on your proposed pricing structure.

CATEGORY 2: Legal & Financial Setup
1. Choose and register your business structure (LLC, sole proprietorship, etc.).
2. Open a separate business bank account to keep personal and business finances separate.
3. Set up a simple bookkeeping system using tools like QuickBooks or spreadsheets.
4. Research and obtain any necessary licenses or permits for your industry.
5. Get business insurance coverage appropriate for your type of side hustle.
6. Set up a business credit card to build credit and track expenses.
7. Create a pricing strategy that covers costs and provides reasonable profit margins.
8. Establish an emergency fund with 3-6 months of business operating expenses.
9. Research tax implications and set aside money quarterly for tax payments.
10. Create invoicing templates and payment processing systems for customers.

CATEGORY 3: Brand Development & Marketing
1. Design a simple logo and choose consistent brand colors for all materials.
2. Create social media profiles on 2-3 platforms where your target audience is active.
3. Build a basic website or landing page showcasing your services and contact information.
4. Develop a content calendar with weekly posts to engage your audience.
5. Create business cards and basic marketing materials for networking events.
6. Write a compelling elevator pitch that explains your business in 30 seconds.
7. Set up Google My Business listing if you serve local customers.
8. Create a referral program to incentive existing customers to bring new business.
9. Join relevant online communities or forums where your target customers gather.
10. Plan and execute one small marketing campaign to test different messaging approaches.

CATEGORY 4: Operations & Time Management
1. Create a dedicated workspace in your home for focused side hustle work.
2. Block out specific hours each week exclusively for your side hustle activities.
3. Set up project management tools to track tasks and deadlines efficiently.
4. Create standard operating procedures for your most common business tasks.
5. Establish boundaries between your day job, side hustle, and personal time.
6. Set up automated systems for invoicing, scheduling, and customer communication.
7. Create templates for common emails, proposals, and client communications.
8. Develop a morning or evening routine that includes time for side hustle work.
9. Set up systems to track your time and productivity on different business activities.
10. Create a filing system for important business documents and client information.

CATEGORY 5: Growth & Scaling
1. Set specific monthly revenue goals and track your progress toward achieving them.
2. Identify which services or products generate the highest profit margins.
3. Create systems to handle increased demand without compromising quality.
4. Build an email list of prospects and past customers for future marketing.
5. Develop additional income streams related to your core business offering.
6. Network with other entrepreneurs in your industry for collaboration opportunities.
7. Invest profits back into tools, training, or marketing to accelerate growth.
8. Create case studies showcasing successful projects to attract similar clients.
9. Consider hiring virtual assistants or contractors for time-consuming tasks.
10. Plan your transition strategy if the side hustle becomes profitable enough to replace your day job.
