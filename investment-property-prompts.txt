CATEGORY 1: Market Research & Property Analysis
1. Research median home prices in your target investment area over the past 3 years.
2. Analyze rental rates for similar properties in 5 different neighborhoods.
3. Calculate the average days properties stay on the market in your chosen area.
4. Study local employment rates and major employers that drive rental demand.
5. Review crime statistics and school ratings for potential investment neighborhoods.
6. Investigate upcoming infrastructure projects that could affect property values.
7. Compare cap rates for different property types in your market.
8. Research local rent control laws and tenant protection regulations.
9. Analyze seasonal rental patterns and vacancy rates in your target area.
10. Study the competition by visiting 10 similar rental properties currently on the market.

CATEGORY 2: Financial Planning & Budgeting
1. Calculate your maximum down payment amount from current savings and assets.
2. Get pre-approved for an investment property mortgage with at least 3 lenders.
3. Create a detailed budget including purchase price, closing costs, and initial repairs.
4. Calculate your target monthly cash flow after all expenses and vacancy allowance.
5. Research property management fees if you plan to hire a management company.
6. Set aside 6 months of mortgage payments as an emergency fund.
7. Determine your maximum acceptable debt-to-income ratio including the new property.
8. Calculate potential tax benefits and deductions for investment property ownership.
9. Research homeowners insurance costs for investment properties in your area.
10. Create a 5-year financial projection including rent increases and major maintenance.

CATEGORY 3: Property Search & Evaluation
1. Set up automated search alerts on MLS and real estate websites with your criteria.
2. Drive through target neighborhoods at different times of day and week.
3. Schedule property viewings for at least 15 potential investment properties.
4. Create a standardized evaluation checklist to rate each property consistently.
5. Research the history of each property including previous sale prices and time on market.
6. Estimate repair and renovation costs for each property you're seriously considering.
7. Calculate the 1% rule and 2% rule for each potential investment property.
8. Investigate HOA fees and restrictions for condos or townhomes.
9. Review property tax assessments and recent increases for target properties.
10. Get professional inspections for your top 3 property choices.

CATEGORY 4: Purchase Process & Legal Considerations
1. Find a real estate agent experienced with investment property transactions.
2. Make your first offer at least 10% below asking price to test seller motivation.
3. Include inspection and financing contingencies in all purchase offers.
4. Negotiate seller credits for necessary repairs identified during inspection.
5. Review all HOA documents and bylaws if purchasing a condo or townhome.
6. Order a professional appraisal to confirm the property's market value.
7. Secure hazard insurance and consider additional umbrella liability coverage.
8. Conduct a final walk-through 24 hours before closing.
9. Review all closing documents with your attorney or experienced agent.
10. Transfer utilities into your name and set up accounts for the rental property.

CATEGORY 5: Property Management & Operations
1. Set competitive rental rates based on current market comparables.
2. Create professional listing photos and descriptions for rental marketing.
3. Screen potential tenants with credit checks, employment verification, and references.
4. Draft a comprehensive lease agreement that complies with local rental laws.
5. Collect first month's rent and security deposit before tenant move-in.
6. Create a detailed move-in inspection checklist with photos for documentation.
7. Establish reliable relationships with contractors for maintenance and repairs.
8. Set up a separate bank account for all rental income and expenses.
9. Create a system for tracking rental payments, late fees, and maintenance requests.
10. Develop a long-term maintenance schedule for major systems and appliances.
