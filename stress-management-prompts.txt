CATEGORY 1: Mindfulness & Awareness
1. Take three deep breaths and notice how your body feels right now.
2. Set a timer for 5 minutes and sit quietly, focusing only on your breathing.
3. Write down three things that are causing you stress today.
4. Notice and name one emotion you're feeling without trying to change it.
5. Spend 2 minutes observing your thoughts without judgment.
6. Identify one physical sign of stress in your body right now.
7. Practice the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding technique (5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you touch, 2 you smell, 1 you taste).
8. Take a mindful walk for 10 minutes, paying attention to each step.
9. Check in with yourself hourly and rate your stress level from 1-10.
10. Write down one thought that's been replaying in your mind and question if it's helpful.

CATEGORY 2: Physical Wellness
1. Do 10 jumping jacks or push-ups to release physical tension.
2. Drink a full glass of water and notice how it feels going down.
3. Take a hot shower or bath and focus on the warm water on your skin.
4. Go to bed 30 minutes earlier than usual tonight.
5. Eat one nutritious meal today without distractions like TV or phone.
6. Stretch your neck, shoulders, and back for 5 minutes.
7. Take a 15-minute walk outside, regardless of the weather.
8. Replace one caffeinated drink today with herbal tea or water.
9. Do 5 minutes of yoga poses or gentle stretching.
10. Schedule and attend one medical or dental appointment you've been postponing.

CATEGORY 3: Emotional Regulation
1. Write about your feelings for 10 minutes without stopping or editing.
2. Call or text someone you trust and share what's bothering you.
3. Listen to one song that makes you feel calm or happy.
4. Practice saying "no" to one request that would add unnecessary stress.
5. Forgive yourself for one mistake you made today.
6. Write down three things you're grateful for right now.
7. Cry if you need to, and remind yourself that it's okay to feel sad.
8. Create a playlist of 5 songs that help you feel better.
9. Write a letter to yourself as if you were comforting your best friend.
10. Practice self-compassion by speaking to yourself as kindly as you would to a loved one.

CATEGORY 4: Environment & Organization
1. Clean and organize one small space in your home for 15 minutes.
2. Remove one source of clutter from your immediate workspace.
3. Create a designated "calm space" in your home with comfortable seating.
4. Unsubscribe from three email lists that create unnecessary mental clutter.
5. Set boundaries around one technology use (like phone-free meals or no screens before bed).
6. Organize your schedule for tomorrow to reduce decision fatigue.
7. Create a simple morning routine that includes one stress-reducing activity.
8. Remove or minimize exposure to one news source or social media account that increases your anxiety.
9. Prepare everything you need for tomorrow before going to bed.
10. Designate specific times for checking emails and messages instead of constantly monitoring them.

CATEGORY 5: Connection & Support
1. Reach out to one friend or family member you haven't talked to in a while.
2. Join one group, class, or community activity related to your interests.
3. Ask for help with one specific task you've been struggling with.
4. Schedule regular check-ins with someone who supports your well-being.
5. Practice active listening in your next conversation without planning your response.
6. Express appreciation to someone who has helped you recently.
7. Set boundaries with one person who consistently adds stress to your life.
8. Share one accomplishment or positive moment from your day with someone you care about.
9. Offer support or help to someone else who might be going through a difficult time.
10. Research and contact one professional counselor, therapist, or support group if you need additional help.
