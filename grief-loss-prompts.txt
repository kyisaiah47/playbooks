CATEGORY 1: Immediate Support & Self-Care
1. Call a trusted friend or family member to let them know how you're feeling today.
2. Set up a simple daily routine that includes one meal, one shower, and one walk outside.
3. Create a list of three people you can contact when you need immediate emotional support.
4. Schedule a check-in with your doctor to discuss how grief is affecting your physical health.
5. Remove or temporarily store items that trigger intense grief reactions from your immediate living space.
6. Ask a friend to help you with grocery shopping or meal preparation for the next week.
7. Set gentle boundaries with people who offer advice or try to rush your grieving process.
8. Create a comfort kit with tissues, tea, a soft blanket, and photos that bring you peace.
9. Write down three small acts of self-compassion you can do when the pain feels overwhelming.
10. Establish a simple bedtime routine to help improve your sleep during this difficult time.

CATEGORY 2: Emotional Processing & Expression
1. Write a letter to your loved one expressing everything you wish you could still tell them.
2. Create a photo album or scrapbook celebrating your favorite memories together.
3. Record yourself talking about your loved one so you can preserve your voice sharing their story.
4. Draw, paint, or create art that represents your feelings about the loss.
5. Join a grief support group either in person or online to connect with others who understand.
6. Schedule regular sessions with a grief counselor or therapist who specializes in loss.
7. Write in a journal every morning for ten minutes about how you're feeling that day.
8. Create a playlist of songs that remind you of your loved one or help you process emotions.
9. Practice saying "I miss you" out loud to your loved one's photo each day.
10. Set aside time each week to cry without trying to stop or judge yourself for the tears.

CATEGORY 3: Memorial & Remembrance Activities
1. Plant a tree or garden in memory of your loved one in your yard or a special location.
2. Donate to a charity or cause that was important to your loved one in their honor.
3. Create an annual tradition like visiting their favorite place on their birthday or anniversary.
4. Organize a celebration of life gathering with friends and family to share stories and memories.
5. Make a memory jar where family and friends can add written memories throughout the year.
6. Commission or create a piece of artwork, jewelry, or keepsake using something that belonged to them.
7. Volunteer for an organization that aligns with values your loved one held dear.
8. Write and share your loved one's life story or eulogy on a memorial website or blog.
9. Create a special ritual for holidays and anniversaries that honors their memory.
10. Establish a scholarship, fund, or memorial in their name that will help others.

CATEGORY 4: Practical Life Management
1. Meet with an attorney to understand any legal matters that need attention after the loss.
2. Contact banks, insurance companies, and financial institutions to update account information.
3. Create a filing system for important documents like death certificates, insurance papers, and legal forms.
4. Ask a trusted friend to help you sort through and decide what to do with your loved one's belongings.
5. Update your own will, beneficiaries, and emergency contacts on important accounts and documents.
6. Research and connect with professionals who can help with estate planning or financial planning.
7. Create a list of your loved one's accounts, subscriptions, and memberships that need to be cancelled.
8. Take inventory of shared responsibilities and create a plan for managing them going forward.
9. Consider whether you need to make changes to your living situation, job, or daily commitments.
10. Set up automatic bill payments and simplify your financial management during this overwhelming time.

CATEGORY 5: Rebuilding & Moving Forward
1. Identify one small hobby or interest you can pursue that brings you a sense of purpose or joy.
2. Plan a small trip or outing to somewhere that holds positive memories or offers new experiences.
3. Reconnect with old friends or make plans to meet new people when you feel ready for social interaction.
4. Set one small personal goal for the next month that gives you something positive to work toward.
5. Consider taking a class, learning a skill, or pursuing education that your loved one would have supported.
6. Create new traditions or routines that honor your loved one while also building your new life.
7. Explore what giving back to your community might look like as you heal and grow stronger.
8. Practice gratitude by writing down three things you're thankful for each week, no matter how small.
9. Consider adopting a pet or caring for something living that can provide companionship and purpose.
10. Write a letter to yourself about what you hope your life will look like one year from now.
