CATEGORY 1: Financial Assessment & Goal Setting
1. Calculate your current net worth by listing all assets and debts.
2. Estimate your annual retirement expenses based on your current lifestyle.
3. Determine your desired retirement age and lifestyle goals.
4. Review all existing retirement accounts and their current balances.
5. Calculate how much monthly income you'll need in retirement.
6. Assess your current savings rate and monthly budget.
7. Identify any major expenses you expect in retirement (healthcare, travel, hobbies).
8. Review your current debt obligations and create a payoff timeline.
9. Evaluate your risk tolerance for retirement investments.
10. Set specific savings milestones for the next 5, 10, and 20 years.

CATEGORY 2: Investment Strategy & Retirement Accounts
1. Maximize your 401(k) contributions, especially if your employer offers matching.
2. Open and contribute to an IRA if you don't have one already.
3. Review and rebalance your investment portfolio based on your age and risk tolerance.
4. Consider Roth IRA conversions if you're in a lower tax bracket now.
5. Diversify your investments across different asset classes and sectors.
6. Set up automatic contributions to your retirement accounts.
7. Review investment fees and switch to lower-cost options if available.
8. Consider target-date funds if you prefer hands-off investing.
9. Evaluate whether you need a financial advisor or investment management service.
10. Research and understand your 401(k) plan's investment options and features.

CATEGORY 3: Healthcare & Insurance Planning
1. Research Medicare options and enrollment requirements for your situation.
2. Consider long-term care insurance while you're still healthy and employed.
3. Maximize contributions to a Health Savings Account (HSA) if eligible.
4. Review your current health insurance and prescription drug needs.
5. Budget for healthcare costs that Medicare doesn't cover.
6. Understand COBRA options if you plan to retire before Medicare eligibility.
7. Research supplemental Medicare insurance (Medigap) policies.
8. Create a healthcare directive and discuss medical preferences with family.
9. Evaluate disability insurance to protect your income before retirement.
10. Research healthcare costs in areas where you might want to retire.

CATEGORY 4: Social Security & Pension Optimization
1. Create a Social Security account online to review your earnings history.
2. Understand how your Social Security benefits are calculated.
3. Determine the optimal age to start claiming Social Security benefits.
4. Review any pension benefits you're entitled to from current or former employers.
5. Understand the tax implications of your Social Security and pension income.
6. Consider spousal claiming strategies if you're married.
7. Verify that your Social Security earnings record is accurate and complete.
8. Plan how Social Security and pension income will fit into your overall retirement budget.
9. Understand survivor benefits available to your spouse.
10. Research how working in retirement might affect your Social Security benefits.

CATEGORY 5: Estate Planning & Legacy Preparation
1. Create or update your will to reflect your current wishes and assets.
2. Establish power of attorney documents for financial and healthcare decisions.
3. Review and update beneficiaries on all retirement accounts and insurance policies.
4. Consider setting up a trust if your estate is complex or valuable.
5. Organize all important financial documents in one accessible location.
6. Discuss your retirement and estate plans with your spouse and adult children.
7. Research tax-efficient ways to transfer wealth to heirs or charities.
8. Create a list of all accounts, passwords, and important contacts for your family.
9. Consider life insurance needs for your surviving spouse or dependents.
10. Plan for potential incapacity with clear instructions for managing your affairs.
