CATEGORY 1: Foundation & Purpose
1. Write a clear mission statement that defines the specific social problem you want to solve.
2. Research existing organizations addressing similar issues to understand the current landscape.
3. Identify your target beneficiaries and conduct interviews to understand their real needs.
4. Define measurable social impact goals you want to achieve in the next 2-3 years.
5. Create a simple theory of change that maps your activities to desired outcomes.
6. List your core values and how they will guide your decision-making process.
7. Develop a compelling elevator pitch that explains your social venture in 60 seconds.
8. Create a stakeholder map identifying all parties affected by or involved in your mission.
9. Write down your personal "why" for starting this social venture.
10. Establish key performance indicators to track both social impact and business metrics.

CATEGORY 2: Business Model & Strategy
1. Choose a sustainable business model that balances profit with social impact.
2. Create a lean canvas outlining your value proposition, key activities, and revenue streams.
3. Calculate the unit economics of your solution to ensure financial viability.
4. Identify potential revenue diversification strategies beyond your primary income source.
5. Develop a competitive analysis comparing your approach to existing solutions.
6. Create a timeline with milestones for your first 12 months of operation.
7. Determine your minimum viable product and plan for iterative development.
8. Research legal structures suitable for social enterprises in your jurisdiction.
9. Establish pricing strategies that remain accessible while ensuring sustainability.
10. Create partnerships with complementary organizations to strengthen your model.

CATEGORY 3: Funding & Resources
1. Research grant opportunities specifically available for social enterprises.
2. Develop a comprehensive budget covering startup costs and first-year operations.
3. Create a compelling pitch deck for potential investors or donors.
4. Identify potential impact investors who align with your mission.
5. Explore crowdfunding platforms suitable for social causes.
6. Apply for social enterprise competitions and accelerator programs.
7. Build relationships with foundations that support your cause area.
8. Create a resource mobilization plan including in-kind donations and volunteer support.
9. Develop earned revenue strategies to reduce dependence on external funding.
10. Establish financial management systems for tracking funds and reporting to stakeholders.

CATEGORY 4: Team Building & Leadership
1. Identify key roles needed for your venture and create job descriptions.
2. Recruit advisors with relevant expertise in your sector and social impact.
3. Build a diverse team that reflects the communities you aim to serve.
4. Establish a strong organizational culture aligned with your social mission.
5. Create systems for regular team feedback and professional development.
6. Develop leadership skills through training, mentorship, or coaching programs.
7. Form a board of directors with complementary skills and networks.
8. Implement fair compensation structures that attract talent while managing costs.
9. Create volunteer engagement strategies for community supporters.
10. Establish succession planning to ensure long-term sustainability.

CATEGORY 5: Impact Measurement & Growth
1. Implement data collection systems to track social outcomes from day one.
2. Create regular reporting schedules for stakeholders including beneficiaries.
3. Develop case studies and stories that demonstrate your impact.
4. Establish feedback loops with beneficiaries to continuously improve your services.
5. Plan for external evaluation or impact assessment within your first two years.
6. Create systems for collecting and analyzing both quantitative and qualitative data.
7. Develop strategies for scaling your impact while maintaining quality.
8. Build partnerships for knowledge sharing and sector-wide collaboration.
9. Create transparent impact reports to build trust with funders and supporters.
10. Plan exit strategies or transition plans if your mission becomes obsolete.
