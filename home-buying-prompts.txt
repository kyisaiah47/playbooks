CATEGORY 1: Financial Preparation
1. Check your credit score and obtain a free credit report from all three bureaus.
2. Calculate how much you can afford for a monthly mortgage payment based on your income.
3. Save for a down payment of at least 3-20% of your target home price.
4. Gather financial documents including pay stubs, tax returns, and bank statements.
5. Get pre-approved for a mortgage to understand your borrowing power.
6. Research different mortgage types including conventional, FHA, VA, and USDA loans.
7. Shop around with multiple lenders to compare interest rates and terms.
8. Set aside funds for closing costs, which typically range from 2-5% of the home price.
9. Build an emergency fund to cover 3-6 months of expenses after buying.
10. Pay down existing debt to improve your debt-to-income ratio.

CATEGORY 2: Market Research and Location
1. Research different neighborhoods and their average home prices.
2. Visit potential areas at different times of day and days of the week.
3. Check local school ratings if you have or plan to have children.
4. Investigate crime statistics and safety ratings for target neighborhoods.
5. Research property tax rates and how they might affect your budget.
6. Look into local amenities like parks, shopping, and entertainment options.
7. Check commute times to your workplace during peak hours.
8. Research future development plans that might affect property values.
9. Compare home prices to rental costs in your target areas.
10. Talk to current residents about their experiences living in the neighborhood.

CATEGORY 3: Home Search and Selection
1. Create a list of must-have features versus nice-to-have features for your home.
2. Start browsing online listings to get familiar with the market.
3. Find a qualified real estate agent who knows your target area well.
4. Schedule home tours for properties that meet your criteria.
5. Take notes and photos during each home visit to help you remember details.
6. Research the history of properties you're interested in, including past sales.
7. Drive by potential homes at different times to assess the neighborhood feel.
8. Consider the home's potential for future resale value.
9. Evaluate the condition of major systems like HVAC, plumbing, and electrical.
10. Don't get emotionally attached to any single property during your search.

CATEGORY 4: Making an Offer and Negotiation
1. Research recent comparable sales in the area to determine a fair offer price.
2. Decide on your maximum budget and stick to it during negotiations.
3. Work with your agent to craft a competitive offer with appropriate contingencies.
4. Include inspection, appraisal, and financing contingencies in your offer.
5. Consider what you can offer beyond price, such as flexible closing dates.
6. Be prepared to respond quickly to counteroffers in competitive markets.
7. Don't waive important contingencies just to make your offer more attractive.
8. Get everything in writing and review all contract terms carefully.
9. Submit required earnest money deposit to show you're a serious buyer.
10. Stay flexible but know when to walk away if negotiations aren't working.

CATEGORY 5: Closing Process and Final Steps
1. Schedule a professional home inspection within your contingency period.
2. Review the inspection report and negotiate repairs with the seller if needed.
3. Ensure the home appraises for at least your loan amount.
4. Finalize your mortgage application and provide any additional documentation requested.
5. Purchase homeowner's insurance and provide proof to your lender.
6. Conduct a final walk-through 24-48 hours before closing.
7. Review all closing documents carefully before signing.
8. Bring a certified check for closing costs and down payment to the closing meeting.
9. Get copies of all important documents including the deed and title insurance.
10. Plan your move-in timeline and arrange for utilities to be transferred to your name.
