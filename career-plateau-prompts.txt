CATEGORY 1: Self-Assessment and Reflection
1. Write down your top 5 professional strengths and how you currently use them at work.
2. List 3 career achievements you're most proud of from the past 2 years.
3. Identify which daily work tasks energize you versus drain your motivation.
4. Rate your current job satisfaction on a scale of 1-10 and explain your reasoning.
5. Document what you enjoyed most about your career when you first started versus now.
6. Ask yourself what you would do professionally if money wasn't a factor.
7. Write a brief description of where you want to be in your career 5 years from now.
8. Identify 3 skills you've always wanted to develop but haven't prioritized.
9. List the work environments and company cultures where you thrive most.
10. Reflect on whether your current role aligns with your core personal values.

CATEGORY 2: Skill Development and Learning
1. Enroll in one online course related to your field within the next month.
2. Identify a colleague who has a skill you want to learn and ask them to mentor you.
3. Set aside 30 minutes daily for reading industry publications or blogs.
4. Attend at least one professional conference or webinar this quarter.
5. Practice a new technical skill for 15 minutes every day for 30 days.
6. Join a professional association or online community in your field.
7. Complete a certification program that would enhance your current role.
8. Start a side project that lets you experiment with new skills or tools.
9. Schedule monthly lunch meetings with experts in different departments.
10. Create a personal learning plan with specific milestones and deadlines.

CATEGORY 3: Network Building and Relationships
1. Reach out to one former colleague or classmate each week to reconnect.
2. Schedule coffee meetings with 3 people in roles you find interesting.
3. Join a local professional meetup group and attend at least 2 events.
4. Update your LinkedIn profile and start sharing industry insights weekly.
5. Offer to help a colleague with a project outside your usual responsibilities.
6. Find a mentor in your desired career direction and request a meeting.
7. Attend company social events and introduce yourself to people from other teams.
8. Connect with alumni from your school who work in your target industry.
9. Volunteer for a cross-functional project to work with new people.
10. Join online forums or Slack communities where your industry professionals gather.

CATEGORY 4: Career Exploration and Opportunities
1. Research 5 companies you'd like to work for and follow their job postings.
2. Schedule informational interviews with people in 3 different roles that interest you.
3. Apply for at least one stretch assignment or special project at your current company.
4. Explore internal job postings and have conversations about lateral moves.
5. Shadow someone in a different department for half a day to learn their role.
6. Research salary ranges for roles you're interested in using sites like Glassdoor.
7. Create a list of 10 companies in growing industries that align with your interests.
8. Attend industry job fairs to learn about different career paths and opportunities.
9. Consider whether freelance or consulting work might offer new experiences.
10. Investigate whether additional education or certifications would open new doors.

CATEGORY 5: Action Planning and Goal Setting
1. Write down 3 specific career goals you want to achieve in the next 12 months.
2. Create a 90-day action plan with weekly milestones toward your biggest career goal.
3. Schedule monthly check-ins with yourself to review progress on career objectives.
4. Set up job alerts for positions that match your target role and location.
5. Build a simple spreadsheet to track your networking activities and follow-ups.
6. Block 2 hours each week on your calendar dedicated to career development activities.
7. Create a personal board of directors with 3-5 people who can advise your career decisions.
8. Write a one-page career vision statement that guides your decision-making.
9. Set a timeline for making your next career move and work backward from that date.
10. Establish accountability by sharing your career goals with a trusted friend or mentor.
