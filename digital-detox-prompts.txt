CATEGORY 1: Screen Time Awareness
1. Track your daily screen time for one week without making any changes.
2. Set specific hours when you will check your phone, like 9am, 1pm, and 6pm only.
3. Move your phone to another room while you sleep.
4. Use a physical alarm clock instead of your phone's alarm.
5. Turn off all non-essential push notifications on your devices.
6. Create phone-free zones in your bedroom and dining area.
7. Set a timer for 30 minutes and put your phone away during that time.
8. Replace your morning phone scroll with 10 minutes of stretching or breathing.
9. Use grayscale mode on your phone to make it less visually appealing.
10. Write down how you feel before and after each major phone session.

CATEGORY 2: Social Media Boundaries
1. Unfollow accounts that make you feel anxious, inadequate, or negative.
2. Delete one social media app from your phone for 24 hours.
3. Set a daily time limit of 30 minutes for all social media combined.
4. Turn off social media notifications completely for one week.
5. Post only positive or meaningful content for the next seven days.
6. Replace mindless scrolling with reading one article or book chapter.
7. Use social media only on your computer, not your phone.
8. Take a complete 48-hour break from all social platforms.
9. Unsubscribe from email newsletters that don't add real value to your life.
10. Share one genuine compliment or encouraging message with a friend instead of passive scrolling.

CATEGORY 3: Mindful Technology Use
1. Ask yourself "What am I hoping to accomplish?" before picking up any device.
2. Use the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.
3. Complete one task fully before switching to check messages or browse online.
4. Choose one specific purpose each time you open a web browser.
5. Turn off your phone completely during meals with others.
6. Practice single-tasking by closing all browser tabs except the one you're actively using.
7. Set your devices to "Do Not Disturb" mode for two hours of focused work time.
8. Replace background TV or music with silence during daily activities like cooking or cleaning.
9. Use voice-to-text instead of typing when possible to reduce screen interaction.
10. Take five deep breaths before responding to any message that triggers strong emotions.

CATEGORY 4: Offline Activities and Hobbies
1. Spend 30 minutes outside without bringing any electronic devices.
2. Learn a new skill that requires your hands, like drawing, knitting, or cooking.
3. Read a physical book for at least 20 minutes before bed.
4. Have a face-to-face conversation with someone for at least 15 minutes.
5. Exercise or move your body for 20 minutes without listening to anything.
6. Write in a physical journal about your thoughts and feelings for 10 minutes.
7. Try a creative activity like painting, crafts, or playing a musical instrument.
8. Go for a walk and pay attention to five things you see, hear, or smell.
9. Play a board game or do a puzzle with family or friends.
10. Prepare and eat one meal without any screens or background entertainment.

CATEGORY 5: Digital Environment Cleanup
1. Delete apps you haven't used in the past month from your phone.
2. Organize your desktop by creating folders and removing unnecessary files.
3. Unsubscribe from three email lists that clutter your inbox.
4. Clear your browser history and saved passwords for sites you no longer use.
5. Log out of social media accounts on all devices to create intentional friction.
6. Clean up your photo gallery by deleting blurry or duplicate pictures.
7. Remove shortcuts to time-wasting websites from your browser bookmarks.
8. Set up automatic email filters to organize incoming messages.
9. Delete contacts from your phone that you no longer communicate with.
10. Review and delete old files, downloads, and documents taking up storage space.
