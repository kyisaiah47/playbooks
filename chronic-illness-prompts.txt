CATEGORY 1: Medical Management & Healthcare
1. Schedule your annual physical exam with your primary care physician.
2. Request copies of all your medical records from each healthcare provider.
3. Create a medication list with dosages, frequencies, and prescribing doctors.
4. Research specialists in your area who treat your specific condition.
5. Prepare a list of symptoms and questions before each doctor appointment.
6. Set up a pill organizer system to track daily medications.
7. Ask your doctor about potential drug interactions with new prescriptions.
8. Schedule regular lab work as recommended by your healthcare team.
9. Get a second opinion from another specialist about your treatment plan.
10. Update your emergency contact information with all healthcare providers.

CATEGORY 2: Daily Life Adaptation & Self-Care
1. Establish a consistent sleep schedule that works with your energy levels.
2. Create a morning routine that accommodates your physical limitations.
3. Modify your workspace to reduce strain and improve comfort.
4. Plan your weekly activities around your best and worst days.
5. Learn basic meal prep techniques for low-energy cooking days.
6. Set up your living space to minimize unnecessary physical effort.
7. Develop a gentle exercise routine approved by your healthcare team.
8. Practice stress reduction techniques like deep breathing or meditation.
9. Create a comfortable rest area where you can recharge during the day.
10. Establish boundaries around your energy and time commitments.

CATEGORY 3: Support Systems & Relationships
1. Tell close family members about your diagnosis and how it affects you.
2. Join an online support group for people with your condition.
3. Find a local chronic illness support group in your community.
4. Educate your friends about your condition using reliable resources.
5. Ask family members to accompany you to important medical appointments.
6. Build a network of people who can help during flare-ups or emergencies.
7. Communicate your needs clearly to your spouse or romantic partner.
8. Connect with others who have similar experiences through patient advocacy groups.
9. Train trusted friends or family on how to help during medical emergencies.
10. Seek professional counseling to process the emotional impact of your diagnosis.

CATEGORY 4: Financial & Legal Planning
1. Review your health insurance coverage and understand your benefits.
2. Research financial assistance programs for your medications or treatments.
3. Create a budget that accounts for increased medical expenses.
4. Apply for disability benefits if your condition qualifies.
5. Set up a health savings account to help manage medical costs.
6. Update your will and healthcare directives to reflect your current wishes.
7. Research prescription assistance programs offered by drug manufacturers.
8. Keep detailed records of all medical expenses for tax purposes.
9. Investigate whether your condition qualifies for FMLA protection at work.
10. Consider long-term care insurance options while still eligible.

CATEGORY 5: Knowledge & Advocacy
1. Research your condition using reputable medical websites and journals.
2. Keep a symptom diary to track patterns and triggers.
3. Learn the medical terminology related to your condition.
4. Subscribe to newsletters from relevant patient advocacy organizations.
5. Attend educational workshops or webinars about your condition.
6. Read patient stories and memoirs from others with your diagnosis.
7. Stay informed about new treatments and clinical trials for your condition.
8. Learn to advocate for yourself during medical appointments.
9. Understand your rights as a patient and person with a chronic illness.
10. Share your knowledge and experience to help newly diagnosed patients.
