## Industry-Specific Content Requirements

### Financial Expertise
**Demonstrate Understanding Of:**
- Current mortgage rates and market trends (conventional, FHA, VA, USDA loans)
- Down payment requirements and assistance programs
- Closing costs breakdown (2-5% of purchase price)
- Property tax calculations and escrow requirements
- PMI requirements and removal strategies
- Debt-to-income ratios and qualification standards
- Pre-approval vs. pre-qualification differences

### Market Knowledge
**Show Expertise In:**
- Local market conditions and seasonal patterns
- Pricing strategies and comparative market analysis (CMA)
- Days on market and absorption rates
- Inventory levels and buyer/seller market dynamics
- Interest rate impact on affordability and competition
- Regional price variations and gentrification patterns

### Process Mastery
**Cover Step-by-Step:**
- Pre-approval process and timing (4-6 weeks before house hunting)
- House hunting strategies and criteria prioritization
- Offer preparation and negotiation tactics
- Inspection periods and contingency management
- Appraisal process and dispute resolution
- Final walkthrough and closing day procedures
- Post-closing responsibilities and timelines

### Risk Management
**Address Common Pitfalls:**
- Waiving contingencies in competitive markets
- Overextending budget with bidding wars
- Skipping inspections or rushing due diligence
- Not understanding actual monthly costs beyond mortgage
- Title issues and survey problems
- Last-minute financing changes or delays

## Quality Benchmarks for Home Buying Content

### Authority Indicators
- Reference specific loan programs by name (FHA 203(b), VA IRRRL, etc.)
- Include current market data and rate ranges
- Demonstrate knowledge of regional market variations
- Show understanding of seasonal buying patterns
- Reference industry tools and professional practices

### Practical Value Metrics
- Provide specific cost ranges and calculations
- Include actionable timelines and checklists
- Offer negotiation scripts and conversation starters
- Give red flags and warning signs to watch for
- Include contingency planning for common problems

### Professional Credibility Tests
- Would a real estate agent share this with clients?
- Does this include information typically known only to professionals?
- Are the cost estimates and timelines realistic and current?
- Does this solve real problems buyers face in today's market?
- Would this prevent costly mistakes or missed opportunities?

## Content Differentiation Requirements

### Never Create Generic Content About
- Basic "how to buy a home" overviews available everywhere
- Generic advice without market-specific context
- Outdated information about rates, programs, or processes
- One-size-fits-all recommendations ignoring market variations

### Always Create Definitive Resources For
- Market-specific strategies and insider knowledge
- Current financing options and qualification strategies
- Professional-level evaluation criteria and red flags
- Advanced negotiation tactics and market timing
- Comprehensive cost analysis and financial planning

## Success Validation for Home Buying Articles

**Each article must pass these tests:**
1. **Professional Endorsement**: Would industry professionals recommend this to clients?
2. **Market Relevance**: Does this address current market conditions and challenges?
3. **Financial Accuracy**: Are all cost estimates, rates, and calculations realistic?
4. **Actionable Guidance**: Can readers immediately implement this advice?
5. **Comprehensive Coverage**: Does this serve as the definitive resource on the topic?

**The Ultimate Test**: If someone could only read one resource about this home buying topic, would this guide them successfully through their purchase while avoiding costly mistakes?
