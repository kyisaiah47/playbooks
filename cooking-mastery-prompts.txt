CATEGORY 1: Kitchen Fundamentals & Equipment
1. Organize your spice rack alphabetically and check expiration dates on all seasonings.
2. Sharpen all your knives using a whetstone or honing steel.
3. Deep clean your oven, stovetop, and refrigerator thoroughly.
4. Inventory your pantry and create a master list of staple ingredients you always keep stocked.
5. Learn to properly hold and use a chef's knife with correct grip and cutting technique.
6. Calibrate your oven temperature with an oven thermometer to ensure accuracy.
7. Practice basic knife cuts: julienne, dice, chiffonade, and brunoise on vegetables.
8. Set up designated prep stations in your kitchen for maximum efficiency.
9. Learn to properly season and maintain your cast iron cookware.
10. Create a mise en place system and practice it with every meal you cook.

CATEGORY 2: Fundamental Cooking Techniques
1. Master the five French mother sauces: béchamel, velouté, espagnole, hollandaise, and tomato.
2. Practice sautéing vegetables until you can achieve perfect doneness and color every time.
3. Learn to roast a whole chicken with crispy skin and juicy meat.
4. Perfect your scrambled eggs using three different techniques: low and slow, French, and American style.
5. Master the art of making a proper roux for thickening sauces and soups.
6. Learn to braise tough cuts of meat until they become fork-tender.
7. Practice making fresh pasta from scratch including rolling and cutting techniques.
8. Master temperature control for pan-searing steaks to achieve perfect doneness.
9. Learn to make a classic vinaigrette and emulsify it properly by hand.
10. Perfect your rice cooking technique for consistently fluffy, non-sticky results.

CATEGORY 3: Flavor Development & Seasoning
1. Create your own spice blends and document the ratios for future reference.
2. Learn to taste and adjust seasoning throughout the cooking process, not just at the end.
3. Practice balancing the five basic tastes: sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and umami in one dish.
4. Master the timing of when to add different herbs and spices during cooking.
5. Learn to build layers of flavor through proper browning, deglazing, and reduction techniques.
6. Experiment with acid (lemon, vinegar, wine) to brighten and balance heavy dishes.
7. Practice seasoning proteins at different stages: before, during, and after cooking.
8. Learn to use aromatics (onions, garlic, celery, carrots) as flavor foundations.
9. Master the art of finishing dishes with fresh herbs, citrus zest, or flavored oils.
10. Practice tasting ingredients raw to understand how cooking transforms their flavors.

CATEGORY 4: Recipe Mastery & Adaptation
1. Choose five classic recipes from different cuisines and cook them until you can make them from memory.
2. Learn to scale recipes up and down mathematically for different serving sizes.
3. Practice substituting ingredients based on availability while maintaining dish integrity.
4. Master reading and following recipes precisely before attempting modifications.
5. Learn to convert between measurement systems (metric, imperial, weight, volume) quickly.
6. Practice timing multiple components of a meal to finish cooking simultaneously.
7. Document your successful recipe modifications and adaptations in a cooking journal.
8. Learn to troubleshoot common cooking problems and adjust recipes mid-cooking.
9. Practice cooking the same dish using different techniques to understand their effects.
10. Master plating and presentation techniques to make your dishes visually appealing.

CATEGORY 5: Advanced Skills & Specialization
1. Learn to break down a whole fish or chicken into individual portions.
2. Master bread making including understanding gluten development and fermentation.
3. Practice advanced pastry techniques like making laminated doughs for croissants.
4. Learn to make stock from scratch and understand how to extract maximum flavor.
5. Master the art of food preservation through pickling, canning, or curing.
6. Learn to cook sous vide and understand precision temperature cooking.
7. Practice making fresh cheese, yogurt, or other fermented dairy products.
8. Master smoking or grilling techniques for different proteins and vegetables.
9. Learn to make complex sauces that require multiple steps and precise timing.
10. Practice menu planning and batch cooking for efficient meal preparation throughout the week.
