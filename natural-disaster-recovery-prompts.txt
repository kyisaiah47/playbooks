CATEGORY 1: Immediate Safety and Assessment
1. Ensure all family members and pets are accounted for and safe.
2. Check for immediate hazards like gas leaks, electrical damage, or structural instability.
3. Take photos of all property damage for insurance documentation.
4. Contact emergency services if there are any injuries or immediate dangers.
5. Secure your property by boarding up broken windows or covering roof damage.
6. Document all damaged belongings with photos and written descriptions.
7. Move salvageable items to a safe, dry location away from further damage.
8. Check on neighbors and offer assistance if you're able to help safely.
9. Locate important documents like insurance policies, ID, and financial records.
10. Create a temporary inventory list of damaged and undamaged possessions.

CATEGORY 2: Emergency Services and Insurance
1. Contact your insurance company immediately to report the disaster and start a claim.
2. Keep detailed records of all communication with insurance representatives and claim numbers.
3. Request an insurance adjuster visit to assess property damage as soon as possible.
4. File claims for all types of coverage you have including homeowner's, auto, and flood insurance.
5. Contact FEMA to register for federal disaster assistance if your area is declared a disaster zone.
6. Apply for Small Business Administration disaster loans for property repair funding.
7. Research and apply for any available state or local disaster relief programs.
8. Keep all receipts for emergency expenses and temporary living costs for reimbursement.
9. Schedule inspections with qualified contractors to get repair estimates for insurance.
10. Review your insurance settlement offers carefully and negotiate if amounts seem insufficient.

CATEGORY 3: Temporary Housing and Basic Needs
1. Arrange temporary housing with family, friends, or through disaster relief organizations.
2. Contact the Red Cross or Salvation Army for emergency shelter and basic supplies.
3. Register with local emergency management for temporary housing assistance programs.
4. Set up mail forwarding to your temporary address to ensure you receive important correspondence.
5. Locate nearby resources for food, clean water, and essential supplies.
6. Find temporary childcare or school arrangements if your children's schools are closed.
7. Arrange temporary care for pets if your housing situation cannot accommodate them.
8. Identify local medical facilities and transfer prescriptions to accessible pharmacies.
9. Establish a temporary budget for increased living expenses during the recovery period.
10. Maintain communication with family and friends about your temporary living situation and needs.

CATEGORY 4: Financial Recovery and Planning
1. Contact your bank to report any damage to financial documents and request account statements.
2. Apply for expedited replacement of lost credit cards, debit cards, and checks.
3. Create a detailed budget that accounts for disaster-related expenses and reduced income.
4. Research disaster-related tax deductions and credits you may be eligible to claim.
5. Contact creditors to explain your situation and request payment deferrals or modifications.
6. Set up a separate checking account specifically for tracking disaster-related expenses and reimbursements.
7. Apply for disaster unemployment assistance if your job was affected by the disaster.
8. Research and apply for grants from charitable organizations and disaster relief funds.
9. Meet with a financial advisor or counselor to create a long-term recovery financial plan.
10. Review and update your insurance coverage to better protect against future disasters.

CATEGORY 5: Rebuilding and Long-term Recovery
1. Hire licensed and insured contractors after checking references and Better Business Bureau ratings.
2. Obtain all necessary building permits before beginning any major repair or reconstruction work.
3. Create a prioritized timeline for repairs, focusing on essential systems first like plumbing and electrical.
4. Consider disaster-resistant improvements like hurricane straps, flood vents, or fire-resistant materials.
5. Keep detailed records of all contractor agreements, permits, and receipts for warranty and tax purposes.
6. Schedule regular inspections throughout the rebuilding process to ensure work meets code requirements.
7. Research and implement mitigation measures to reduce vulnerability to future disasters.
8. Update your home inventory and insurance coverage to reflect rebuilt or replaced items.
9. Create an updated emergency preparedness plan based on lessons learned from this disaster.
10. Connect with support groups or counseling services to address emotional recovery needs.
