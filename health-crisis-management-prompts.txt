CATEGORY 1: Emergency Response & Immediate Care
1. Call 911 or emergency services if experiencing life-threatening symptoms.
2. Gather all current medications and bring them to the emergency room.
3. Contact your primary care physician or on-call doctor immediately.
4. Notify your emergency contact person about the health crisis.
5. Locate your insurance cards and identification documents.
6. Write down all symptoms and when they started for medical professionals.
7. Arrange immediate transportation to the nearest hospital or urgent care.
8. Ask a trusted person to accompany you to medical appointments.
9. Request copies of all emergency room records and test results.
10. Follow up with prescribed emergency medications or treatments exactly as directed.

CATEGORY 2: Medical Team Coordination
1. Schedule an appointment with your primary care doctor within 48 hours.
2. Request referrals to specialists relevant to your health condition.
3. Create a list of all healthcare providers involved in your care.
4. Ask each doctor for their preferred method of communication.
5. Request that all test results be shared with your primary care physician.
6. Schedule follow-up appointments with each specialist as recommended.
7. Ask for written summaries of each medical visit and treatment plan.
8. Verify that all your doctors are aware of medications prescribed by others.
9. Request a case manager or patient advocate if available at your hospital.
10. Create a shared medical file that all providers can access if possible.

CATEGORY 3: Insurance & Financial Management
1. Call your insurance company to understand your coverage limits and benefits.
2. Request pre-authorization for any expensive treatments or procedures.
3. Keep detailed records of all medical expenses and receipts.
4. Apply for financial assistance programs offered by hospitals or clinics.
5. Contact a hospital financial counselor to discuss payment plans.
6. Review all medical bills carefully for errors or duplicate charges.
7. Ask about generic medication alternatives to reduce prescription costs.
8. Inquire about patient assistance programs from pharmaceutical companies.
9. Consider opening a Health Savings Account (HSA) for future medical expenses.
10. Contact your employer's HR department about Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA) benefits.

CATEGORY 4: Personal Care & Daily Life Management
1. Arrange for someone to help with grocery shopping and meal preparation.
2. Set up a medication management system with pill organizers or reminders.
3. Create a comfortable recovery space in your home with necessary supplies.
4. Ask family or friends to help with household chores and cleaning.
5. Arrange transportation to medical appointments if you cannot drive.
6. Modify your work schedule or request time off as needed.
7. Set up a support system for childcare or pet care during appointments.
8. Stock up on healthy, easy-to-prepare foods and snacks.
9. Create a daily routine that includes rest periods and gentle activities.
10. Ask someone to help manage your calendar and reschedule non-essential commitments.

CATEGORY 5: Communication & Support Network
1. Inform close family members and friends about your health situation.
2. Designate one person to coordinate communication updates to others.
3. Join a support group for people with similar health conditions.
4. Create a group text or email list to share updates with loved ones.
5. Ask specific people for specific types of help rather than general requests.
6. Connect with a counselor or therapist to process emotions about your health crisis.
7. Research online communities and forums related to your health condition.
8. Schedule regular check-ins with close friends or family members.
9. Ask your healthcare team about patient education resources and support groups.
10. Consider using a health journal app to track symptoms and share with your medical team.
