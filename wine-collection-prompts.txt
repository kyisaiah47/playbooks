CATEGORY 1: Getting Started with Wine Collection
1. Research different wine regions and their signature grape varieties to understand what appeals to your taste.
2. Set a realistic monthly budget for wine purchases to avoid overspending.
3. Identify your preferred wine styles by tasting reds, whites, rosés, and sparkling wines.
4. Find a local wine shop with knowledgeable staff who can guide your selections.
5. Start a wine journal to track what you taste and what you like or dislike.
6. Attend a beginner's wine tasting class to learn basic tasting techniques.
7. Choose a dedicated storage space in your home away from heat and light.
8. Purchase basic wine accessories like a corkscrew, decanter, and proper glasses.
9. Join a wine club or subscription service to discover new bottles regularly.
10. Set collection goals like focusing on specific regions, vintages, or price ranges.

CATEGORY 2: Building Your Collection
1. Start with versatile, food-friendly wines that pair well with your regular meals.
2. Buy wines from different price points to learn how quality affects taste.
3. Purchase bottles from various regions to understand terroir differences.
4. Collect both wines for immediate drinking and those meant for aging.
5. Buy multiples of wines you love so you can track their evolution over time.
6. Focus on building a core collection before branching into rare or expensive bottles.
7. Document each wine's purchase date, price, and intended drinking window.
8. Network with other collectors to learn about good deals and trading opportunities.
9. Visit wineries during harvest season to buy wines directly from producers.
10. Balance your collection between everyday drinking wines and special occasion bottles.

CATEGORY 3: Proper Storage and Organization
1. Maintain consistent temperature between 55-65°F in your storage area.
2. Keep humidity levels around 70% to prevent corks from drying out.
3. Store bottles on their sides to keep corks moist and prevent oxidation.
4. Organize wines by region, vintage, or drinking timeline for easy access.
5. Create an inventory system using apps or spreadsheets to track your collection.
6. Label storage areas clearly so you can find specific bottles quickly.
7. Install proper lighting that won't damage wine through UV exposure.
8. Check stored wines periodically for signs of spoilage or cork failure.
9. Rotate your stock by drinking older vintages before they pass their prime.
10. Consider investing in a wine refrigerator as your collection grows.

CATEGORY 4: Tasting and Evaluation
1. Taste wines systematically by examining appearance, aroma, and flavor.
2. Compare wines side by side to better understand differences in style and quality.
3. Take detailed tasting notes including date, occasion, and food pairings.
4. Rate wines using a consistent scoring system to track your preferences.
5. Host tasting parties with friends to share opinions and discover new favorites.
6. Practice identifying common wine faults like cork taint or oxidation.
7. Experiment with decanting older red wines to improve their flavor.
8. Learn to pair wines with different cuisines and cooking styles.
9. Attend professional tastings to expand your palate and knowledge.
10. Track how wines change over time by retasting bottles from the same case.

CATEGORY 5: Advanced Collecting and Investment
1. Research vintage charts to identify exceptional years worth collecting.
2. Build relationships with wine merchants who can access rare and limited releases.
3. Consider wines with strong track records for appreciation in value.
4. Diversify your collection across regions, producers, and price points.
5. Learn about wine auctions and how to buy and sell collectible bottles.
6. Focus on wines from prestigious producers known for aging potential.
7. Keep detailed provenance records for valuable bottles to maintain their worth.
8. Store investment-grade wines in professional facilities if needed.
9. Stay informed about market trends and critic scores that affect wine values.
10. Plan your collection's future by considering inheritance or sale strategies.
