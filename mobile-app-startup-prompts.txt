CATEGORY 1: Idea Validation & Market Research
1. Conduct interviews with 20 potential users to validate your app concept.
2. Analyze your top 5 competitors and identify their strengths and weaknesses.
3. Create a detailed user persona document for your target audience.
4. Survey 100 people to determine willingness to pay for your app solution.
5. Research app store categories to find the best fit for your product.
6. Test your app idea with a simple landing page and collect email signups.
7. Join relevant online communities where your target users gather.
8. Calculate the total addressable market size for your app category.
9. Create a problem-solution fit statement in one clear sentence.
10. Validate your app's core feature through a simple prototype or mockup.

CATEGORY 2: Product Development & Design
1. Create detailed wireframes for all key screens in your mobile app.
2. Design your app's user interface following platform-specific guidelines.
3. Build a minimum viable product (MVP) with core functionality only.
4. Set up version control and establish a development workflow.
5. Choose your technology stack based on target platforms and team skills.
6. Create a comprehensive technical architecture document for your app.
7. Implement user authentication and basic security measures.
8. Design and test your app's onboarding flow for new users.
9. Develop a content management system or data structure for your app.
10. Create a testing plan that covers functionality, usability, and performance.

CATEGORY 3: Business Strategy & Planning
1. Write a comprehensive business plan outlining your app's strategy.
2. Determine your app's monetization model and pricing strategy.
3. Create financial projections for the first 3 years of operation.
4. Establish your startup as a legal business entity.
5. Develop a go-to-market strategy with specific launch tactics.
6. Create a detailed project timeline with development milestones.
7. Research and apply for relevant startup grants or competitions.
8. Build relationships with potential strategic partners or integrations.
9. Develop a customer acquisition strategy with measurable goals.
10. Create a risk assessment document identifying potential business threats.

CATEGORY 4: Funding & Investment
1. Calculate exactly how much funding you need for the first 18 months.
2. Create a compelling pitch deck that tells your startup's story.
3. Research and compile a list of 50 relevant investors for your industry.
4. Prepare detailed financial statements and business metrics for investors.
5. Practice your pitch presentation until you can deliver it flawlessly.
6. Network with other entrepreneurs to get introductions to investors.
7. Apply to startup accelerators or incubator programs in your area.
8. Consider bootstrapping options and calculate how long your savings will last.
9. Prepare for due diligence by organizing all legal and financial documents.
10. Negotiate term sheets and understand equity implications for your startup.

CATEGORY 5: Launch & Growth
1. Submit your app to app stores following all guidelines and requirements.
2. Implement analytics tools to track user behavior and app performance.
3. Create a content marketing strategy to build awareness before launch.
4. Set up customer support systems to handle user inquiries and feedback.
5. Launch a beta testing program with 50-100 early adopters.
6. Develop partnerships with influencers or brands in your target market.
7. Create a referral program to encourage organic user growth.
8. Monitor app store reviews and respond to user feedback promptly.
9. Implement A/B testing for key features to optimize user conversion.
10. Scale your marketing efforts based on which channels drive quality users.
