CATEGORY 1: Loan Assessment and Understanding
1. List all your federal and private student loans with current balances and interest rates.
2. Identify which loans are subsidized versus unsubsidized federal loans.
3. Review your loan servicer contact information and online account access.
4. Calculate your total student loan debt across all loans and servicers.
5. Determine your current monthly minimum payment obligations.
6. Research the specific terms and conditions of each loan type you have.
7. Check if any of your loans qualify for Public Service Loan Forgiveness.
8. Identify which loans have variable versus fixed interest rates.
9. Review your loan origination dates and remaining repayment terms.
10. Gather all loan documents and store them in an organized filing system.

CATEGORY 2: Repayment Plan Selection
1. Compare standard repayment versus income-driven repayment plan options.
2. Calculate potential monthly payments under each available repayment plan.
3. Apply for an income-driven repayment plan if it reduces your monthly burden.
4. Evaluate whether consolidating federal loans would benefit your situation.
5. Consider switching repayment plans if your income has changed significantly.
6. Research graduated repayment plans if you expect income growth over time.
7. Calculate the total interest you'll pay under different repayment scenarios.
8. Set up automatic payments to receive interest rate reductions.
9. Review repayment plan options annually during your loan servicer's renewal process.
10. Document your chosen repayment strategy and reasoning for future reference.

CATEGORY 3: Interest Rate Optimization
1. Research current refinancing rates from multiple private lenders.
2. Calculate potential savings from refinancing high-interest private loans.
3. Evaluate the trade-offs of refinancing federal loans versus keeping federal protections.
4. Apply for student loan refinancing if it significantly reduces your interest costs.
5. Consider making extra payments toward your highest interest rate loans first.
6. Set up bi-weekly payments instead of monthly to reduce interest accumulation.
7. Apply any windfalls like tax refunds directly to loan principal.
8. Negotiate with private loan servicers for interest rate reductions.
9. Monitor interest rate trends to time refinancing applications strategically.
10. Calculate how much extra principal payments would save in total interest costs.

CATEGORY 4: Financial Planning and Budgeting
1. Create a detailed monthly budget that includes student loan payments.
2. Identify areas in your spending where you can redirect money toward loan payments.
3. Build an emergency fund before aggressively paying down low-interest student loans.
4. Balance student loan payments with retirement savings contributions.
5. Track your net worth monthly to see progress on debt reduction.
6. Set up a separate savings account specifically for extra loan payments.
7. Calculate how much you need to earn monthly to cover all loan obligations.
8. Plan for potential income changes and their impact on loan affordability.
9. Create a debt avalanche or debt snowball strategy for multiple loans.
10. Automate your loan payments to ensure you never miss a due date.

CATEGORY 5: Long-term Strategy and Goal Setting
1. Set a target date for becoming completely debt-free from student loans.
2. Calculate how extra monthly payments would accelerate your payoff timeline.
3. Create milestone celebrations for paying off individual loans or reaching balance targets.
4. Research tax deductions available for student loan interest payments.
5. Plan how eliminating student loans will impact your other financial goals.
6. Consider the opportunity cost of aggressive loan payoff versus investing.
7. Evaluate whether pursuing additional education would improve your debt-to-income ratio.
8. Create a backup plan for loan payments if you lose your job or income.
9. Document your progress monthly to stay motivated toward your payoff goals.
10. Plan how you'll redirect loan payments toward other financial priorities once debt-free.
