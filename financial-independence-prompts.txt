CATEGORY 1: Financial Assessment & Planning
1. Calculate your current net worth by listing all assets and subtracting all debts.
2. Track your monthly income and expenses for three months to understand your spending patterns.
3. Determine your FIRE number by multiplying your annual expenses by 25.
4. Set a specific target date for achieving financial independence.
5. Calculate how much you need to save monthly to reach your FIRE goal.
6. Review and optimize your current budget to identify areas for increased savings.
7. Assess your current investment accounts and their performance over the past year.
8. Research and compare different FIRE strategies (Lean FIRE, Fat FIRE, Barista FIRE).
9. Create a detailed breakdown of your essential versus discretionary expenses.
10. Establish specific milestone markers to track your progress toward financial independence.

CATEGORY 2: Income Optimization
1. Negotiate a salary raise or promotion at your current job within the next six months.
2. Develop a marketable skill that could increase your earning potential by 20%.
3. Start a side hustle or freelance work to generate additional monthly income.
4. Create multiple streams of passive income through dividends, royalties, or rental properties.
5. Switch to a higher-paying job or career field that aligns with your skills.
6. Build a professional network that could lead to better job opportunities.
7. Obtain relevant certifications or education that qualify you for higher-paying positions.
8. Launch an online business or digital product that can generate recurring revenue.
9. Monetize a hobby or passion project to create supplemental income.
10. Explore geographic arbitrage by working remotely from a lower cost-of-living area.

CATEGORY 3: Expense Management & Frugality
1. Cancel all unused subscriptions and memberships you haven't used in three months.
2. Negotiate lower rates for your phone, internet, and insurance bills.
3. Switch to generic brands for groceries and household items to reduce monthly spending.
4. Meal plan and cook at home instead of eating out more than twice per week.
5. Find free or low-cost entertainment alternatives to replace expensive hobbies.
6. Downsize your housing or find a roommate to reduce your largest monthly expense.
7. Use public transportation, bike, or walk instead of driving when possible.
8. Buy quality used items instead of new ones for non-essential purchases.
9. Implement a 24-hour waiting period before making any non-essential purchase over $50.
10. Set up automatic transfers to savings so you pay yourself first before spending.

CATEGORY 4: Investment & Wealth Building
1. Open and maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k) and IRA.
2. Invest in low-cost index funds with expense ratios under 0.1%.
3. Automate your investments by setting up monthly transfers to your investment accounts.
4. Diversify your portfolio across different asset classes and geographic regions.
5. Avoid individual stock picking and focus on broad market index investing.
6. Rebalance your investment portfolio quarterly to maintain your target allocation.
7. Take advantage of employer 401(k) matching by contributing at least the full match amount.
8. Consider investing in real estate through REITs or rental properties for diversification.
9. Minimize taxes by utilizing tax-loss harvesting and holding investments long-term.
10. Educate yourself on investment principles by reading one finance book per month.

CATEGORY 5: Financial Protection & Risk Management
1. Build an emergency fund covering 6-12 months of living expenses in a high-yield savings account.
2. Obtain adequate health insurance coverage to protect against medical bankruptcy.
3. Purchase term life insurance if you have dependents who rely on your income.
4. Review and update your beneficiaries on all investment and retirement accounts annually.
5. Create or update your will and consider establishing a trust if you have significant assets.
6. Protect your earning capacity with disability insurance through your employer or independently.
7. Keep important financial documents organized and accessible in case of emergency.
8. Monitor your credit report regularly and maintain a credit score above 750.
9. Avoid lifestyle inflation by maintaining your current standard of living as income increases.
10. Plan for major life changes like marriage, children, or career transitions that could impact your FIRE timeline.
