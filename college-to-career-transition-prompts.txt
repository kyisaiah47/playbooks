CATEGORY 1: Academic & Skill Development
1. Research industry-specific certifications that complement your major and boost employability.
2. Identify three technical skills employers seek in your field and create a learning plan.
3. Join or form a study group with classmates in your target career field.
4. Schedule regular office hours with professors who have industry connections.
5. Complete at least one project that demonstrates practical application of your coursework.
6. Enroll in a public speaking or presentation skills workshop to improve communication.
7. Take an elective course outside your major that adds valuable cross-functional skills.
8. Create a portfolio showcasing your best academic work and personal projects.
9. Apply for academic competitions or case study challenges in your industry.
10. Seek out undergraduate research opportunities that align with career interests.

CATEGORY 2: Professional Experience & Internships
1. Apply to at least five internship opportunities each semester in your target field.
2. Reach out to local companies for informational interviews about their industry.
3. Volunteer for organizations that operate in your field of interest.
4. Join professional student organizations related to your career path.
5. Attend career fairs and networking events hosted by your school or local businesses.
6. Create profiles on professional networking platforms like LinkedIn.
7. Shadow professionals in your field for a day to understand their daily responsibilities.
8. Apply for part-time jobs that provide relevant experience, even if entry-level.
9. Seek leadership roles in clubs, organizations, or group projects.
10. Document all work experiences and quantify your achievements with specific metrics.

CATEGORY 3: Career Exploration & Planning
1. Complete career assessment tests to identify strengths and compatible career paths.
2. Research salary ranges and growth prospects for roles in your target industry.
3. Identify three companies you'd love to work for and study their culture and values.
4. Create a list of your transferable skills and how they apply to different industries.
5. Explore alternative career paths that utilize your major in unexpected ways.
6. Schedule informational interviews with professionals in roles that interest you.
7. Attend industry conferences, webinars, or virtual events to learn about trends.
8. Research graduate school options if advanced education is required for your goals.
9. Create a five-year career plan with specific milestones and checkpoints.
10. Identify potential mentors in your field and reach out for guidance.

CATEGORY 4: Job Search Preparation
1. Craft a compelling resume tailored to your target industry and roles.
2. Write a professional cover letter template that you can customize for applications.
3. Practice answering common interview questions and prepare specific examples.
4. Develop a 30-second elevator pitch that clearly communicates your value proposition.
5. Research and compile a list of companies currently hiring in your field.
6. Set up job alerts on multiple job search platforms for relevant positions.
7. Create a professional email address and voicemail message for job communications.
8. Prepare a list of professional references and obtain their permission to use them.
9. Practice your interview skills through mock interviews with career services or friends.
10. Research typical interview processes and requirements for your target companies.

CATEGORY 5: Financial & Life Transition Planning
1. Create a post-graduation budget including student loan payments and living expenses.
2. Research the cost of living in cities where you want to work.
3. Start building an emergency fund to cover job search and transition periods.
4. Understand your student loan repayment options and create a payment strategy.
5. Research health insurance options for after you lose student coverage.
6. Learn about 401(k) plans, retirement savings, and employee benefits packages.
7. Develop a timeline for major life transitions like moving or finding housing.
8. Create a professional wardrobe on a budget for interviews and your first job.
9. Research salary negotiation strategies and practice discussing compensation.
10. Plan for potential relocation costs if your ideal job requires moving to a new city.
