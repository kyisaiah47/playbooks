CATEGORY 1: Organizational Setup & Legal Foundation
1. Research your state's requirements for nonprofit incorporation and tax-exempt status.
2. Draft your organization's mission statement in one clear, compelling sentence.
3. Assemble a founding board of directors with diverse skills and expertise.
4. File Articles of Incorporation with your state's Secretary of State office.
5. Apply for an Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS.
6. Submit Form 1023 or 1023-EZ to apply for 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status.
7. Create organizational bylaws that outline governance structure and procedures.
8. Register for state and local tax exemptions where applicable.
9. Obtain necessary business licenses and permits for your operations.
10. Set up a dedicated business bank account using your EIN and incorporation documents.

CATEGORY 2: Financial Management & Fundraising
1. Develop a detailed annual budget with projected income and expenses.
2. Choose and implement accounting software designed for nonprofits.
3. Create a donor database to track contributions and donor relationships.
4. Write your first grant proposal to a local foundation or government agency.
5. Establish financial policies for expense reimbursement and purchasing approvals.
6. Plan and execute a signature fundraising event for your organization.
7. Set up online donation capabilities through your website or donation platform.
8. Recruit a treasurer or hire a bookkeeper to manage daily financial tasks.
9. Research and apply for corporate sponsorship opportunities in your community.
10. Schedule quarterly financial reviews with your board to track budget performance.

CATEGORY 3: Program Development & Implementation
1. Conduct a community needs assessment to identify gaps your organization can fill.
2. Design your first program with clear objectives, activities, and success metrics.
3. Create a program evaluation framework to measure impact and outcomes.
4. Develop partnerships with other organizations serving similar populations.
5. Recruit and train volunteers to support your program delivery.
6. Write standard operating procedures for all major program activities.
7. Establish a client intake and data collection system for program participants.
8. Create marketing materials to promote your programs to the target audience.
9. Pilot test your program with a small group before full-scale implementation.
10. Schedule regular program review meetings to assess effectiveness and make improvements.

CATEGORY 4: Governance & Leadership
1. Recruit additional board members to reach optimal board size for your organization.
2. Establish board committees for finance, fundraising, and program oversight.
3. Create a board orientation packet with roles, responsibilities, and expectations.
4. Develop conflict of interest and whistleblower policies for ethical governance.
5. Schedule monthly board meetings with structured agendas and documentation.
6. Hire your first executive director or designate key leadership roles.
7. Create job descriptions and performance evaluation processes for all staff positions.
8. Establish an advisory council of community leaders and subject matter experts.
9. Develop a strategic plan with 3-5 year goals and annual objectives.
10. Implement board term limits and succession planning for leadership continuity.

CATEGORY 5: Operations & Compliance
1. Set up basic insurance coverage including general liability and directors' officers insurance.
2. Create an employee handbook with policies on harassment, safety, and benefits.
3. File annual reports and maintain good standing with state regulatory agencies.
4. Establish data security protocols to protect donor and client information.
5. Develop a communications strategy including website, social media, and newsletters.
6. Create volunteer management systems for recruitment, training, and recognition.
7. Implement document retention policies for financial records and organizational documents.
8. Establish vendor relationships for essential services like payroll, IT support, and legal counsel.
9. Conduct annual board retreats to review performance and plan future directions.
10. Prepare and file annual IRS Form 990 reporting on finances and activities.
