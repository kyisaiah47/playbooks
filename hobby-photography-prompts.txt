CATEGORY 1: Equipment & Setup
1. Research different camera types to determine if you need a DSLR, mirrorless, or can start with your smartphone.
2. Set a realistic budget for your first camera and essential accessories like memory cards and batteries.
3. Learn the basic functions of your camera by reading the manual or watching beginner tutorials.
4. Practice holding your camera properly to avoid camera shake and improve stability.
5. Understand the exposure triangle (aperture, shutter speed, ISO) and how each affects your photos.
6. Experiment with your camera's automatic modes before diving into manual settings.
7. Invest in a basic tripod to help with stability and long exposure shots.
8. Learn how to properly clean your camera lens to maintain image quality.
9. Test different shooting angles and heights to find what feels comfortable for you.
10. Create a simple camera bag setup with essentials like extra batteries and lens cloths.

CATEGORY 2: Photography Fundamentals
1. Practice the rule of thirds by placing subjects along the grid lines in your viewfinder.
2. Learn to identify and use natural light sources like golden hour and overcast skies.
3. Experiment with depth of field by adjusting your aperture settings for different effects.
4. Study composition techniques like leading lines, symmetry, and framing.
5. Practice focusing techniques including single-point autofocus and manual focus.
6. Learn to recognize and work with different types of lighting conditions.
7. Experiment with different perspectives by shooting from high, low, and eye-level angles.
8. Practice controlling camera shake through proper breathing and stance techniques.
9. Learn to use your camera's histogram to check for proper exposure.
10. Study the work of photographers you admire to understand their composition choices.

CATEGORY 3: Shooting Practice & Subjects
1. Start with stationary subjects like flowers, architecture, or still life objects to practice basics.
2. Take daily photos for a week to build a consistent shooting habit.
3. Challenge yourself to photograph the same subject at different times of day.
4. Practice street photography by capturing candid moments in public spaces.
5. Try portrait photography with friends or family members in natural light.
6. Experiment with macro photography using close-up shots of small objects.
7. Capture moving subjects like pets, children, or vehicles to practice action shots.
8. Take landscape photos during different weather conditions to see how they affect mood.
9. Practice indoor photography using available light from windows and lamps.
10. Document a local event or gathering to practice photojournalism-style shooting.

CATEGORY 4: Post-Processing & Editing
1. Choose a photo editing software that fits your budget and skill level.
2. Learn basic adjustments like exposure, contrast, highlights, and shadows.
3. Practice color correction to ensure your photos look natural and appealing.
4. Experiment with cropping to improve composition and remove distracting elements.
5. Learn to sharpen images appropriately without creating unnatural effects.
6. Practice batch editing to efficiently process multiple similar photos.
7. Create a simple filing system to organize your photos by date or subject.
8. Learn to export photos in different sizes and formats for various uses.
9. Experiment with black and white conversions to create dramatic effects.
10. Practice subtle editing that enhances rather than completely transforms your original image.

CATEGORY 5: Community & Growth
1. Join a local photography club or online community to connect with other photographers.
2. Share your work on social media or photography platforms to get feedback.
3. Participate in photo challenges or contests to push your creative boundaries.
4. Find a photography mentor or take a workshop to accelerate your learning.
5. Create a simple portfolio of your best 10-20 images to track your progress.
6. Attend photography exhibitions or galleries to see professional work in person.
7. Start a photography project with a specific theme or timeframe to stay motivated.
8. Volunteer to photograph local events to gain experience and give back to your community.
9. Set specific photography goals like learning one new technique each month.
10. Consider selling prints or offering services once you've developed confidence in your skills.
