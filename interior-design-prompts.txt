CATEGORY 1: Planning & Assessment
1. Take photos of each room from multiple angles to document current state.
2. Measure room dimensions including ceiling height and window placements.
3. Create a realistic budget breakdown for your interior design project.
4. Identify which rooms are highest priority for redesign.
5. Research your preferred design styles using Pinterest or design magazines.
6. List current furniture and decor items you want to keep or replace.
7. Note any structural limitations like load-bearing walls or plumbing locations.
8. Assess natural light patterns throughout the day in each space.
9. Document any specific functional needs for each room.
10. Set a realistic timeline with milestones for project completion.

CATEGORY 2: Design Development
1. Create a mood board combining colors, textures, and inspiration images.
2. Choose a cohesive color palette that flows between connected spaces.
3. Draft basic floor plans showing furniture placement and traffic flow.
4. Select a focal point or statement piece for each room.
5. Plan lighting layers including ambient, task, and accent lighting.
6. Choose window treatments that complement your design style.
7. Design storage solutions that blend seamlessly with your aesthetic.
8. Plan how artwork and decor will be arranged on walls.
9. Select flooring materials that suit each room's function and style.
10. Create a shopping list organized by room and priority level.

CATEGORY 3: Shopping & Sourcing
1. Research furniture retailers that match your budget and style preferences.
2. Compare prices across multiple vendors for major furniture pieces.
3. Order fabric samples and paint swatches to test in your actual lighting.
4. Visit showrooms to see and feel furniture pieces in person.
5. Source vintage or secondhand pieces that add character to your design.
6. Find local artisans or craftspeople for custom built-in solutions.
7. Order key furniture pieces allowing for delivery lead times.
8. Shop for decorative accessories and artwork that complete your vision.
9. Purchase quality basics like pillows, throws, and plants for layering.
10. Create a backup list of alternative options for out-of-stock items.

CATEGORY 4: Installation & Setup
1. Prepare rooms by clearing out old furniture and cleaning thoroughly.
2. Complete any painting or wallpaper installation before furniture arrives.
3. Install new lighting fixtures and ensure electrical work is completed.
4. Arrange furniture according to your planned layout and test traffic flow.
5. Hang artwork at appropriate heights and create gallery wall arrangements.
6. Install window treatments ensuring proper measurements and hardware.
7. Set up storage systems and organize belongings in their designated spaces.
8. Layer textiles like rugs, pillows, and throws for warmth and texture.
9. Place plants and decorative objects to add life and personality.
10. Adjust lighting levels and lamp placement for optimal ambiance.

CATEGORY 5: Finishing & Maintenance
1. Style surfaces like coffee tables, shelves, and nightstands with curated objects.
2. Fine-tune furniture placement based on how you actually use the space.
3. Add final decorative touches like candles, books, and personal mementos.
4. Take after photos to document your completed transformation.
5. Create a maintenance schedule for cleaning and caring for new furnishings.
6. Organize warranty information and care instructions for major purchases.
7. Establish systems to keep spaces organized and clutter-free.
8. Plan for seasonal updates with easily swappable accessories and textiles.
9. Evaluate what worked well and what you'd do differently for future projects.
10. Share your completed space with friends and family to celebrate your achievement.
