CATEGORY 1: Skills & Learning
1. Identify three key technical skills most valuable in your industry and create a learning plan for each.
2. Sign up for an online course or certification program relevant to your career goals.
3. Read one industry publication or blog weekly to stay current with trends and developments.
4. Practice a new software tool or technology for 30 minutes daily for two weeks.
5. Join a professional development workshop or webinar in your field this month.
6. Find a mentor who can guide your skill development and career progression.
7. Create a personal learning portfolio documenting your completed courses and certifications.
8. Attend a local meetup or conference related to your industry or interests.
9. Start a side project that allows you to practice and showcase new skills.
10. Schedule monthly check-ins with yourself to assess progress on your learning goals.

CATEGORY 2: Professional Networking
1. Connect with five new professionals in your industry on LinkedIn each week.
2. Attend one networking event, conference, or industry meetup per month.
3. Schedule informational interviews with three people in roles you aspire to have.
4. Join a professional association or organization relevant to your career field.
5. Reach out to alumni from your school who work in companies you're interested in.
6. Offer to help colleagues with projects outside your immediate responsibilities.
7. Start engaging meaningfully with industry content on social media platforms.
8. Introduce two people in your network who could benefit from knowing each other.
9. Follow up with new connections within 48 hours of meeting them with a personalized message.
10. Create and share valuable content related to your expertise or industry insights.

CATEGORY 3: Career Planning & Goal Setting
1. Write down your 1-year, 3-year, and 5-year career goals with specific milestones.
2. Research salary ranges and growth trajectories for your desired career path.
3. Identify companies you'd like to work for and research their culture and requirements.
4. Create a personal mission statement that defines your professional values and purpose.
5. Schedule quarterly career planning sessions to review and adjust your goals.
6. Map out the skills and experiences needed to reach your next career level.
7. Set up job alerts for positions that align with your career advancement goals.
8. Create a timeline for when you want to achieve each major career milestone.
9. Research alternative career paths within your industry that might interest you.
10. Document your career wins and lessons learned in a professional journal.

CATEGORY 4: Personal Branding & Visibility
1. Update your LinkedIn profile with a professional photo and compelling headline.
2. Create a personal website or portfolio showcasing your work and achievements.
3. Write and publish one article or blog post about your industry expertise.
4. Volunteer to present at a team meeting or industry event to build speaking experience.
5. Participate actively in online industry forums and discussion groups.
6. Document and share your professional accomplishments on social media regularly.
7. Request recommendations from supervisors and colleagues on LinkedIn.
8. Create a consistent professional image across all your online platforms.
9. Develop an elevator pitch that clearly communicates your value proposition.
10. Seek opportunities to be quoted or featured in industry publications or podcasts.

CATEGORY 5: Performance & Leadership
1. Set specific, measurable performance goals with your manager for the next quarter.
2. Volunteer to lead a small project or initiative to demonstrate leadership potential.
3. Ask for regular feedback from your supervisor and act on their suggestions.
4. Identify ways to improve processes or solve problems in your current role.
5. Mentor a newer employee or intern to develop your leadership skills.
6. Take on additional responsibilities that align with your career advancement goals.
7. Track and document your key achievements and their impact on the organization.
8. Seek stretch assignments that challenge you beyond your current skill level.
9. Practice giving constructive feedback to colleagues and team members.
10. Create and present a proposal for a new project or improvement initiative.
