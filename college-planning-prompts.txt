CATEGORY 1: Academic Preparation
1. Research graduation requirements for your current high school to ensure you're on track.
2. Meet with your guidance counselor to discuss your current GPA and course trajectory.
3. Register for challenging courses like AP, IB, or dual enrollment classes in subjects you enjoy.
4. Create a study schedule that allocates time for homework, test prep, and extracurriculars.
5. Take practice SAT or ACT tests to identify areas needing improvement.
6. Sign up for official SAT or ACT test dates that align with college application deadlines.
7. Consider retaking standardized tests if your scores don't meet your target college requirements.
8. Build relationships with teachers who can write strong letters of recommendation.
9. Start a portfolio or documentation of your academic achievements and projects.
10. Research summer academic programs or courses that could strengthen your college applications.

CATEGORY 2: College Research and Selection
1. Create a list of 15-20 colleges that offer programs matching your intended major.
2. Research admission requirements, average GPAs, and test scores for your target schools.
3. Visit college campuses either in person or through virtual tours to get a feel for each environment.
4. Attend college fairs and information sessions to speak directly with admissions representatives.
5. Categorize your college list into reach, match, and safety schools based on your academic profile.
6. Research the campus culture, student life, and extracurricular opportunities at each college.
7. Look into specific programs, professors, or research opportunities that align with your interests.
8. Consider factors like location, size, cost, and distance from home in your decision-making.
9. Read student reviews and testimonials to understand the real college experience.
10. Create a spreadsheet to track application deadlines, requirements, and your progress for each school.

CATEGORY 3: Financial Planning
1. Complete the FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) as early as possible after October 1st.
2. Research and apply for scholarships through your school, community organizations, and online databases.
3. Calculate the total cost of attendance including tuition, room, board, and personal expenses for each college.
4. Meet with a financial aid counselor to understand your aid eligibility and options.
5. Compare financial aid packages from different colleges to understand your out-of-pocket costs.
6. Research state-specific financial aid programs and grants available in your area.
7. Consider work-study programs and part-time job opportunities to help cover college expenses.
8. Open a dedicated savings account for college expenses and set up automatic contributions.
9. Research student loan options and understand the long-term implications of borrowing money.
10. Apply for local scholarships offered by businesses, foundations, and civic organizations in your community.

CATEGORY 4: Application Process
1. Create accounts on college application platforms like Common App, Coalition App, or individual school portals.
2. Write a compelling personal statement that showcases your unique experiences and aspirations.
3. Request official transcripts from your high school to be sent to your chosen colleges.
4. Ask teachers, counselors, or mentors to write letters of recommendation well in advance of deadlines.
5. Complete all required essays and supplemental questions for each college application.
6. Submit your standardized test scores directly to colleges through the testing agencies.
7. Proofread all application materials multiple times and have others review them for errors.
8. Pay application fees or request fee waivers if you qualify for financial assistance.
9. Submit applications before deadlines, aiming for at least one week early to avoid technical issues.
10. Follow up with colleges to ensure all required materials have been received and your application is complete.

CATEGORY 5: Life Skills and Transition Preparation
1. Learn basic life skills like doing laundry, managing a budget, and cooking simple meals.
2. Practice time management by balancing schoolwork, extracurriculars, and personal responsibilities.
3. Research career paths related to your intended major and potential job outcomes.
4. Develop strong communication skills through writing, public speaking, and interpersonal interactions.
5. Build independence by handling your own appointments, deadlines, and personal organization.
6. Create a resume highlighting your academic achievements, work experience, and volunteer activities.
7. Practice interview skills for college admissions interviews and future job opportunities.
8. Research housing options like dorms, apartments, or living at home during your college years.
9. Learn about campus resources like tutoring centers, career services, and mental health support.
10. Connect with current college students or recent graduates to learn about the college experience and get advice.
