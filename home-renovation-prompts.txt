CATEGORY 1: Planning & Design
1. Create a detailed list of all rooms and areas you want to renovate.
2. Set a realistic budget that includes a 20% contingency for unexpected costs.
3. Research local building codes and permit requirements for your planned renovations.
4. Collect inspiration photos and create a mood board for each space.
5. Measure all rooms and create basic floor plans or sketches.
6. Prioritize renovations based on necessity, budget, and timeline.
7. Decide whether each project requires professional help or can be DIY.
8. Research different materials, finishes, and fixtures within your budget range.
9. Create a realistic timeline that accounts for permit approval and contractor availability.
10. Establish temporary living arrangements if major areas will be unusable.

CATEGORY 2: Permits & Legal Requirements
1. Contact your local building department to understand permit requirements.
2. Submit permit applications with required drawings and documentation.
3. Schedule required inspections at appropriate project milestones.
4. Verify that all contractors are properly licensed and insured.
5. Check with your HOA about any renovation restrictions or approval processes.
6. Review your homeowner's insurance policy for renovation coverage.
7. Notify neighbors about upcoming construction that might affect them.
8. Obtain copies of your home's original blueprints from city records.
9. Research utility line locations before digging or major structural work.
10. Keep all permit documents and inspection certificates organized and accessible.

CATEGORY 3: Contractor Selection & Management
1. Get at least three detailed quotes from licensed contractors for major work.
2. Check references and visit previous job sites if possible.
3. Verify contractor licenses, insurance, and Better Business Bureau ratings.
4. Create detailed contracts that specify materials, timeline, and payment schedule.
5. Never pay large amounts upfront; tie payments to completed milestones.
6. Establish clear communication protocols and regular check-in schedules.
7. Document all changes to the original scope in writing with cost adjustments.
8. Take before, during, and after photos to track progress and quality.
9. Hold final payment until all work is completed and inspected.
10. Address any issues immediately rather than letting problems accumulate.

CATEGORY 4: Material Selection & Procurement
1. Create a comprehensive materials list with quantities and specifications.
2. Research suppliers and compare prices for major items like flooring and fixtures.
3. Order materials with lead times in mind to avoid project delays.
4. Choose durable, low-maintenance materials appropriate for each space's use.
5. Consider energy-efficient options that may qualify for rebates or tax credits.
6. Buy extra materials for future repairs, especially for custom or discontinued items.
7. Coordinate delivery schedules to avoid storage issues or damage.
8. Inspect all deliveries immediately and document any damage or defects.
9. Keep all receipts and warranty information organized in one location.
10. Return unused materials promptly to recover costs and free up storage space.

CATEGORY 5: Project Execution & Quality Control
1. Establish a safe, organized workspace with proper storage for tools and materials.
2. Follow the planned sequence of work to avoid having to redo completed tasks.
3. Conduct daily walkthroughs to monitor progress and address issues early.
4. Test all systems (plumbing, electrical, HVAC) before closing up walls or finishing.
5. Take detailed photos of all work before covering with drywall or finishes.
6. Keep a daily log of work completed, issues encountered, and decisions made.
7. Maintain clear communication with family members about safety and access.
8. Schedule regular cleanup to maintain a safe and organized work environment.
9. Perform quality checks at each milestone before proceeding to the next phase.
10. Plan and execute a thorough final walkthrough before considering the project complete.
