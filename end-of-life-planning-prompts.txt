CATEGORY 1: Legal Documents & Estate Planning
1. Create or update your will with the help of an attorney or online service.
2. Establish a living trust to avoid probate and protect your assets.
3. Designate power of attorney for financial decisions if you become incapacitated.
4. Create a healthcare power of attorney to make medical decisions on your behalf.
5. Draft an advance directive or living will outlining your end-of-life care preferences.
6. Review and update beneficiaries on all accounts, insurance policies, and retirement plans.
7. Create a comprehensive list of all assets, debts, and account information.
8. Store important documents in a secure location and inform trusted people where they are.
9. Consider establishing guardianship plans if you have minor children or dependents.
10. Review and update your estate plan every 3-5 years or after major life changes.

CATEGORY 2: Financial Planning & Organization
1. Calculate your net worth by listing all assets and liabilities.
2. Organize all financial accounts and create a master list with account numbers and contacts.
3. Review your life insurance coverage to ensure it meets your family's needs.
4. Set up automatic bill payments to ensure ongoing expenses are covered.
5. Create a budget for end-of-life care expenses and final arrangements.
6. Research long-term care insurance options and associated costs.
7. Consolidate retirement accounts and optimize investment allocations.
8. Pay off high-interest debt to reduce financial burden on your estate.
9. Set aside funds in an easily accessible account for immediate final expenses.
10. Work with a financial advisor to create a comprehensive financial transition plan.

CATEGORY 3: Healthcare & Medical Decisions
1. Choose a primary care physician and establish regular health checkups.
2. Create a comprehensive medical history document including medications and allergies.
3. Research and choose healthcare facilities for potential long-term care needs.
4. Discuss end-of-life care preferences with family members and healthcare providers.
5. Complete a POLST (Physician Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment) form if appropriate.
6. Organize all medical records and insurance information in one accessible location.
7. Research palliative care and hospice options in your area.
8. Choose a healthcare proxy who understands and will honor your wishes.
9. Create a medication management system and inform caregivers of your routine.
10. Consider registering as an organ donor and communicate your decision to family.

CATEGORY 4: Personal & Family Communication
1. Have honest conversations with family members about your end-of-life wishes.
2. Write letters to loved ones to be opened after your passing.
3. Create a list of important people to contact and their relationship to you.
4. Record family history, stories, and memories for future generations.
5. Plan and communicate your funeral or memorial service preferences.
6. Discuss caregiving roles and responsibilities with family members.
7. Share passwords and access information for digital accounts with trusted individuals.
8. Create a plan for the care of pets or dependents after your passing.
9. Organize and label family photos and heirlooms with their stories and significance.
10. Schedule regular family meetings to update everyone on your planning progress.

CATEGORY 5: Legacy & Final Arrangements
1. Choose between burial, cremation, or other final disposition options.
2. Pre-plan and potentially pre-pay for funeral or memorial services.
3. Select a cemetery plot or decide on cremation ash placement.
4. Write your own obituary or provide key information for others to use.
5. Create a digital legacy plan for social media accounts and online presence.
6. Donate meaningful items to charity or distribute them to loved ones.
7. Plan a meaningful memorial service or celebration of life event.
8. Create a memory box or time capsule for future family members.
9. Consider establishing a charitable foundation or making planned giving arrangements.
10. Prepare instructions for handling your personal belongings and possessions.
