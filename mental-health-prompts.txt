CATEGORY 1: Self-Awareness & Assessment
1. Take 5 minutes to write down three emotions you felt today and what triggered them.
2. Complete a mood tracking app entry or journal page about your current mental state.
3. Identify one negative thought pattern you've noticed recurring this week.
4. Rate your stress level on a scale of 1-10 and write one reason why.
5. List three physical symptoms you experience when feeling anxious or overwhelmed.
6. Reflect on one relationship that significantly impacts your mental wellbeing.
7. Identify your biggest mental health challenge right now in one sentence.
8. Write down three personal strengths that help you cope with difficult times.
9. Notice and record one environmental factor that affects your mood today.
10. Ask yourself what you need most right now for your mental health.

CATEGORY 2: Professional Support & Resources
1. Research and write down contact information for three local therapists who accept your insurance.
2. Schedule an appointment with your primary care doctor to discuss mental health concerns.
3. Call your insurance company to understand your mental health coverage benefits.
4. Download and explore one evidence-based mental health app recommended by professionals.
5. Find and save the contact information for a mental health crisis hotline.
6. Research one type of therapy (CBT, DBT, EMDR) that might help your specific concerns.
7. Ask your doctor for a referral to a psychiatrist if you're considering medication.
8. Look into whether your workplace offers Employee Assistance Program (EAP) services.
9. Research local support groups for your specific mental health condition or challenge.
10. Create a list of questions to ask a potential therapist during your first consultation.

CATEGORY 3: Daily Coping Strategies
1. Practice one 5-minute breathing exercise when you feel stressed today.
2. Write down three things you're grateful for before going to bed tonight.
3. Take a 10-minute walk outside to clear your mind and get fresh air.
4. Set a boundary with one person or situation that drains your energy.
5. Create a simple morning routine that includes one activity that brings you joy.
6. Practice saying "no" to one commitment that feels overwhelming right now.
7. Do one small act of self-care that takes less than 15 minutes today.
8. Challenge one negative thought by writing down evidence for and against it.
9. Reach out to one trusted friend or family member when you're feeling isolated.
10. Use a grounding technique (5-4-3-2-1 senses) when feeling anxious or overwhelmed.

CATEGORY 4: Lifestyle & Wellness
1. Establish a consistent sleep schedule by going to bed at the same time for one week.
2. Limit social media use to 30 minutes per day for better mental clarity.
3. Add one serving of brain-healthy food (like omega-3 rich fish or berries) to your meals today.
4. Engage in 20 minutes of physical activity that you actually enjoy.
5. Create a phone-free zone in your bedroom to improve sleep quality.
6. Reduce caffeine intake after 2 PM to see if it improves your anxiety levels.
7. Stay hydrated by drinking one extra glass of water every few hours today.
8. Spend 10 minutes in natural sunlight to boost your vitamin D and mood.
9. Declutter one small area of your living space to create a calmer environment.
10. Plan and prepare one nutritious meal that makes you feel energized and satisfied.

CATEGORY 5: Building Support Systems
1. Reach out to one person you haven't talked to in a while who usually lifts your spirits.
2. Join one online community or forum related to your mental health journey.
3. Tell one trusted person about your mental health struggles and ask for their support.
4. Plan a low-key social activity with a friend who makes you feel comfortable and accepted.
5. Write a letter (you don't have to send) to someone who has hurt you to process your feelings.
6. Identify one person in your life who you can call during a mental health crisis.
7. Practice expressing your needs clearly to one person in your support network.
8. Set up regular check-ins (weekly text or monthly coffee) with a supportive friend or family member.
9. Research and attend one local mental health awareness event or support group meeting.
10. Create a "support team" list with names, phone numbers, and when to contact each person.
