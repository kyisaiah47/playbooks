CATEGORY 1: Planning & Setup
1. Research which vegetables and herbs grow best in your local climate and growing season.
2. Measure your available growing space including balconies, windowsills, and rooftop areas.
3. Test your soil pH and nutrient levels using a simple home testing kit.
4. Calculate how many hours of direct sunlight your growing areas receive each day.
5. Create a budget for initial setup costs including containers, soil, seeds, and basic tools.
6. Design a layout plan showing where each type of plant will be positioned for optimal growth.
7. Research local gardening regulations and HOA restrictions for your building or neighborhood.
8. Choose between starting from seeds versus buying seedlings based on your experience level.
9. Plan your planting schedule around frost dates and seasonal growing windows.
10. Identify nearby sources for organic soil, compost, and gardening supplies.

CATEGORY 2: Container & Space Preparation
1. Select appropriate sized containers with proper drainage holes for each type of plant.
2. Install wall-mounted planters or vertical growing systems to maximize limited space.
3. Set up a potting station with all necessary tools and materials organized and accessible.
4. Fill containers with high-quality potting mix appropriate for vegetables and herbs.
5. Create or purchase plant labels to keep track of what you've planted where.
6. Install hooks, shelves, or stands to position plants at optimal heights for sunlight.
7. Set up a water collection system to catch and reuse rainwater for irrigation.
8. Apply mulch or ground cover to retain moisture and suppress weeds in larger containers.
9. Install windbreaks or protective barriers for exposed rooftop or balcony gardens.
10. Organize your tools and supplies in a compact storage solution near your growing area.

CATEGORY 3: Planting & Growing
1. Start your first seeds indoors in small pots 6-8 weeks before the last frost date.
2. Plant quick-growing crops like lettuce and radishes for fast early harvests.
3. Transplant seedlings to their final containers once they develop their second set of leaves.
4. Establish a companion planting system pairing plants that benefit each other's growth.
5. Install tomato cages or trellises for climbing plants before they become too large.
6. Begin a succession planting schedule to ensure continuous harvests throughout the season.
7. Plant perennial herbs like rosemary and thyme that will return year after year.
8. Start a small compost bin to create nutrient-rich soil amendments from kitchen scraps.
9. Sow cover crops in unused containers to improve soil health during off-seasons.
10. Document your planting dates and varieties in a garden journal for future reference.

CATEGORY 4: Maintenance & Care
1. Establish a daily watering routine checking soil moisture with your finger before watering.
2. Remove weeds weekly before they compete with your plants for nutrients and space.
3. Prune tomato suckers and pinch herb flowers to encourage continued leaf production.
4. Apply organic fertilizer every 2-4 weeks according to each plant's specific needs.
5. Monitor plants daily for early signs of pests or disease problems.
6. Rotate your crops each season to prevent soil depletion and disease buildup.
7. Thin overcrowded seedlings to give remaining plants adequate space to grow.
8. Install row covers or shade cloth during extreme weather to protect vulnerable plants.
9. Stake tall plants and provide support for heavy fruiting branches before they break.
10. Clean and sanitize your tools regularly to prevent spreading diseases between plants.

CATEGORY 5: Harvesting & Problem-Solving
1. Harvest leafy greens in the morning when they're crisp and full of moisture.
2. Pick tomatoes when they show the first blush of color and let them ripen indoors.
3. Cut herbs frequently to encourage bushier growth and prevent them from flowering.
4. Collect seeds from your best-performing plants to save money on next year's garden.
5. Identify common pests like aphids and learn organic methods to control them naturally.
6. Troubleshoot yellowing leaves by checking for overwatering, underwatering, or nutrient deficiencies.
7. Preserve your harvest by learning basic techniques like freezing, drying, or pickling.
8. Share excess produce with neighbors to build community connections and reduce waste.
9. Research solutions for specific problems like blossom end rot in tomatoes or bolting lettuce.
10. Plan improvements for next season based on what worked well and what challenges you faced.
