CATEGORY 1: Habit Identification & Goal Setting
1. Write down three specific habits you want to build or break this month.
2. Define exactly what success looks like for each habit using measurable terms.
3. Choose one keystone habit that will positively impact other areas of your life.
4. Set a realistic frequency for each habit (daily, 3x per week, etc.).
5. Identify the specific time of day you'll perform each new habit.
6. Write down why each habit matters to you personally.
7. Choose habits that take less than 5 minutes to start with.
8. Link each new habit to an existing routine you already do consistently.
9. Set a 30-day trial period for testing new habits before committing long-term.
10. Create a simple "if this, then that" rule for each habit trigger.

CATEGORY 2: Tracking Systems & Tools
1. Choose one tracking method (app, journal, calendar) and stick with it for 30 days.
2. Set up a simple daily checklist with checkboxes for each habit.
3. Take a photo of your completed habit tracker each evening.
4. Use a habit tracking app that sends you daily reminder notifications.
5. Create a visual habit calendar and mark successful days with an X.
6. Set up automatic tracking where possible (step counter, sleep tracker, etc.).
7. Keep your tracking tool in a visible location where you'll see it daily.
8. Backup your tracking data weekly to prevent losing progress.
9. Review your tracking data every Sunday to identify patterns.
10. Share your tracking system with an accountability partner.

CATEGORY 3: Building Consistency & Momentum
1. Start with just one habit and master it before adding others.
2. Never miss the same habit two days in a row.
3. Focus on showing up consistently rather than perfect performance.
4. Create a minimum viable version of each habit for difficult days.
5. Prepare for obstacles by planning what you'll do when motivation is low.
6. Use the "2-minute rule" - scale habits down until they take less than 2 minutes.
7. Stack new habits onto existing strong routines you already do.
8. Create environmental cues that make good habits easier to remember.
9. Remove friction by preparing everything you need the night before.
10. Celebrate small wins immediately after completing each habit.

CATEGORY 4: Overcoming Challenges & Setbacks
1. Plan specific strategies for your three most likely habit obstacles.
2. Create a comeback plan for getting back on track after missing days.
3. Identify your habit triggers and replace bad ones with good alternatives.
4. Track your excuses to recognize patterns in your resistance.
5. Prepare a list of micro-habits you can do when feeling unmotivated.
6. Set up accountability by telling someone about your habit goals.
7. Remove temptations from your environment that derail good habits.
8. Create if-then scenarios for handling stress, travel, and busy periods.
9. Practice self-compassion when you slip up instead of giving up entirely.
10. Adjust your habits based on what you learn from failed attempts.

CATEGORY 5: Long-term Success & Habit Evolution
1. Review and adjust your habits monthly based on what's working.
2. Gradually increase the difficulty or duration of established habits.
3. Replace completed habits with new ones to continue growing.
4. Track leading indicators that predict your habit success.
5. Create rewards for hitting habit milestones (weekly, monthly, quarterly).
6. Build habits that compound and reinforce each other over time.
7. Document lessons learned from both successful and failed habit attempts.
8. Schedule quarterly reviews to assess which habits still serve your goals.
9. Share your habit success stories to inspire and teach others.
10. Design your ideal daily routine incorporating all your established habits.
