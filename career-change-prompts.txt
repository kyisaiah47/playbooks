CATEGORY 1: Self-Discovery & Assessment
1. Write down three values that are most important to you in your work life.
2. List ten skills you've developed in your current or previous roles.
3. Identify three work activities that energize you and three that drain you.
4. Take a personality assessment like Myers-Briggs or StrengthsFinder to understand your work style.
5. Ask five trusted colleagues or friends what they see as your greatest strengths.
6. Write a one-page reflection on what success means to you personally.
7. Identify three industries or roles you've always been curious about.
8. List your non-negotiable requirements for your next job (salary, location, hours, etc.).
9. Reflect on a time when you felt most fulfilled at work and analyze what made it special.
10. Create a visual representation (mind map, collage) of your ideal work environment.

CATEGORY 2: Research & Exploration
1. Interview three people currently working in your target field or role.
2. Research salary ranges for positions you're interested in using sites like Glassdoor or PayScale.
3. Attend one industry conference, meetup, or networking event related to your target field.
4. Read five recent articles about trends and challenges in your industry of interest.
5. Follow ten thought leaders or companies in your target field on LinkedIn.
6. Shadow someone for a day or volunteer in your area of interest to gain firsthand experience.
7. Join two professional associations or online communities related to your target career.
8. Research the educational requirements and certifications needed for your desired role.
9. Create a list of twenty companies you'd love to work for and research their culture and values.
10. Set up Google alerts for keywords related to your target industry to stay informed.

CATEGORY 3: Skill Development & Education
1. Identify the top three skills gap between your current abilities and your target role requirements.
2. Enroll in one online course or certification program relevant to your desired career path.
3. Start a side project or volunteer opportunity that lets you practice new skills.
4. Find a mentor who can guide your skill development in your target field.
5. Join a professional development group or study circle related to your career goals.
6. Dedicate thirty minutes daily to learning something new about your target industry.
7. Practice explaining your transferable skills in terms relevant to your new field.
8. Create a portfolio or case study showcasing your relevant work and projects.
9. Attend workshops or seminars to develop both technical and soft skills needed for your transition.
10. Seek feedback on your skill development progress from mentors or industry contacts.

CATEGORY 4: Personal Branding & Networking
1. Update your LinkedIn profile to reflect your career transition goals and aspirations.
2. Write a compelling elevator pitch that explains your career change in 30 seconds.
3. Reach out to one new professional contact each week through LinkedIn or email.
4. Start sharing industry-relevant content on social media to demonstrate your knowledge and interest.
5. Create a personal website or portfolio that showcases your skills and career journey.
6. Attend networking events and introduce yourself to at least three new people each time.
7. Offer to help others in your network with their projects or challenges.
8. Write thoughtful comments on industry leaders' LinkedIn posts to increase your visibility.
9. Ask for informational interviews with professionals in companies you're targeting.
10. Join alumni networks, professional groups, or community organizations to expand your connections.

CATEGORY 5: Job Search & Transition Planning
1. Create a realistic timeline for your career transition with specific milestones and deadlines.
2. Build an emergency fund to cover 3-6 months of expenses during your transition period.
3. Tailor your resume for each application to highlight relevant skills and experiences.
4. Practice interviewing by conducting mock interviews with friends or career counselors.
5. Apply to 2-3 positions per week that align with your career goals and qualifications.
6. Develop a strategy for explaining your career change positively to potential employers.
7. Consider taking on freelance or contract work in your target field to gain experience.
8. Create a job search tracking system to monitor applications, interviews, and follow-ups.
9. Negotiate your exit strategy from your current role to maintain positive relationships.
10. Plan your first 90 days in your new role to ensure a successful transition and quick wins.
