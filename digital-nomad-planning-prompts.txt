CATEGORY 1: Destination Research & Selection
1. Research visa requirements and length of stay allowed for your nationality in potential destinations.
2. Compare cost of living between your top 5 destination choices using online calculators.
3. Check internet speed and reliability reports for your target cities on nomad websites.
4. Read recent blog posts and forum discussions from nomads who lived in your shortlisted locations.
5. Evaluate time zone differences and how they'll affect client meetings or remote work schedules.
6. Research local co-working spaces, cafes, and digital nomad communities in each destination.
7. Check seasonal weather patterns and identify the best months to visit each location.
8. Look up safety ratings and current political stability for your potential destinations.
9. Research local transportation options and monthly costs for getting around the city.
10. Create a shortlist of 3 destinations based on your research and personal priorities.

CATEGORY 2: Legal & Administrative Setup
1. Apply for or renew your passport ensuring it has at least 6 months validity remaining.
2. Research and apply for appropriate visas or digital nomad visas for your chosen destinations.
3. Set up a virtual mailing address service to receive important documents while traveling.
4. Notify your bank and credit card companies about your travel plans to avoid account freezes.
5. Register for Global Entry or similar programs to expedite border crossings.
6. Obtain international health insurance that covers you in all planned destinations.
7. Digitize all important documents and store encrypted copies in cloud storage.
8. Research tax obligations as a digital nomad and consult with a tax professional if needed.
9. Set up power of attorney with a trusted person for handling urgent matters at home.
10. Create a document checklist and ensure you have backup copies of everything important.

CATEGORY 3: Remote Work & Income Preparation
1. Negotiate a remote work arrangement with your current employer or transition to freelancing.
2. Set up reliable communication tools and test video calling quality from different locations.
3. Create multiple income streams to reduce dependence on a single source of revenue.
4. Build an emergency fund covering at least 3-6 months of expenses in your target destinations.
5. Research and set up international payment methods like Wise or Payoneer for receiving payments.
6. Optimize your online presence and portfolio to attract remote work opportunities.
7. Test your work setup with portable equipment to ensure productivity while traveling.
8. Establish clear boundaries and communication schedules with clients across different time zones.
9. Research local business licensing requirements if you plan to work legally in certain countries.
10. Create backup plans for internet outages including mobile hotspots and alternative work locations.

CATEGORY 4: Accommodation & Living Arrangements
1. Research different accommodation options from short-term rentals to co-living spaces in your target cities.
2. Book your first month's accommodation before arrival to ensure a smooth landing.
3. Join local Facebook groups and nomad communities to find housing recommendations and roommates.
4. Create a list of must-have amenities for your accommodations including reliable wifi and workspace.
5. Research neighborhoods that balance safety, convenience, and proximity to co-working spaces.
6. Set a realistic monthly budget for accommodation based on local market rates.
7. Learn about local rental customs, deposit requirements, and typical lease terms.
8. Identify backup accommodation options in case your initial plans fall through.
9. Research furnished vs unfurnished options and what basic items you might need to purchase.
10. Plan your accommodation booking strategy to balance cost savings with flexibility.

CATEGORY 5: Health, Safety & Lifestyle Management
1. Schedule a travel health consultation and get required vaccinations for your destinations.
2. Research local healthcare systems and identify quality hospitals or clinics in your target cities.
3. Create a comprehensive first aid kit and learn about medication availability in different countries.
4. Research local customs, cultural norms, and basic language phrases to show respect and integrate better.
5. Plan how you'll maintain your fitness routine and mental health while constantly changing locations.
6. Identify local emergency contacts and register with your embassy or consulate where applicable.
7. Research local food safety practices and common health risks in your planned destinations.
8. Create a support network of fellow nomads and locals through online communities and meetups.
9. Plan how you'll handle homesickness, loneliness, and the challenges of constant travel.
10. Develop strategies for maintaining work-life balance and avoiding digital nomad burnout.
