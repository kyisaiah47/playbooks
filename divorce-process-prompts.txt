CATEGORY 1: Legal and Documentation Preparation
1. Gather all financial documents including bank statements, tax returns, and investment accounts from the past three years.
2. Locate and organize marriage certificate, prenuptial agreements, and any existing legal documents.
3. Research and compile a list of qualified divorce attorneys in your area with good reviews.
4. Schedule initial consultations with at least three different divorce lawyers to compare approaches.
5. Create a detailed inventory of all marital assets including real estate, vehicles, and personal property.
6. Obtain copies of all insurance policies including life, health, auto, and homeowner's coverage.
7. Document all debts and liabilities including credit cards, loans, and mortgages with current balances.
8. Collect employment records, pay stubs, and benefits information for both spouses.
9. Photograph valuable items, artwork, and jewelry for documentation purposes.
10. Open a separate bank account in your name only for future financial independence.

CATEGORY 2: Child-Related Matters
1. Research different custody arrangement options to understand what works best for your family situation.
2. Create a detailed schedule of your children's current activities, school, and healthcare needs.
3. Document your daily involvement in childcare, school activities, and decision-making responsibilities.
4. Research child support guidelines and calculators for your state to understand potential obligations.
5. Consider your children's emotional needs and research age-appropriate ways to discuss the divorce.
6. Identify family counselors or child therapists who specialize in divorce transitions.
7. Plan how to maintain stability in your children's routines during the divorce process.
8. Research schools and childcare options if relocation becomes necessary.
9. Create a communication plan for co-parenting discussions and decisions.
10. Document any concerns about the other parent's fitness or ability to provide proper care.

CATEGORY 3: Financial Planning and Security
1. Calculate your post-divorce budget including housing, utilities, food, and childcare expenses.
2. Research your eligibility for spousal support or alimony based on your state's laws.
3. Understand how retirement accounts, pensions, and 401(k)s will be divided in your divorce.
4. Review and update all insurance beneficiaries on life, health, and retirement accounts.
5. Establish credit in your own name if you don't already have individual credit cards or loans.
6. Create an emergency fund to cover unexpected expenses during and after the divorce process.
7. Research the tax implications of divorce including filing status and dependency exemptions.
8. Consider whether you'll need to return to work or increase your income after divorce.
9. Evaluate whether keeping the marital home is financially feasible or if selling is better.
10. Consult with a financial planner who specializes in divorce to understand long-term implications.

CATEGORY 4: Emotional and Personal Support
1. Find a qualified therapist who specializes in divorce and relationship transitions.
2. Join a divorce support group in your community or online to connect with others in similar situations.
3. Identify trusted friends and family members who can provide emotional support throughout the process.
4. Develop healthy coping strategies for managing stress, anger, and sadness during difficult times.
5. Create new routines and hobbies to help establish your individual identity outside of marriage.
6. Practice self-care activities like exercise, meditation, or activities that bring you joy.
7. Set boundaries with your spouse about communication and interactions during the divorce process.
8. Consider whether couples counseling or mediation might be helpful before proceeding with litigation.
9. Prepare yourself emotionally for the practical challenges of single parenting if you have children.
10. Focus on building a positive vision for your future life after divorce is finalized.

CATEGORY 5: Practical Life Arrangements
1. Research housing options including whether to stay in the marital home, rent, or purchase new property.
2. Update your will, power of attorney, and other estate planning documents to reflect your new status.
3. Change passwords on all personal accounts including email, social media, and online banking.
4. Research health insurance options since you may lose coverage through your spouse's employer plan.
5. Update your address and emergency contacts with employers, schools, banks, and service providers.
6. Consider whether you need to change your name back or keep your married name for professional reasons.
7. Plan the logistics of separating household items and personal belongings fairly and efficiently.
8. Arrange temporary living situations if one spouse needs to move out during the divorce process.
9. Update your resume and consider career development if you've been out of the workforce.
10. Create a timeline for completing all necessary tasks and legal requirements for your divorce.
