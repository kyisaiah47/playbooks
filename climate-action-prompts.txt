CATEGORY 1: Home Energy & Efficiency
1. Switch all incandescent bulbs to LED lights to reduce energy consumption by up to 80%.
2. Unplug electronics and chargers when not in use to eliminate phantom energy drain.
3. Set your thermostat 2 degrees lower in winter and 2 degrees higher in summer.
4. Seal air leaks around windows and doors with weatherstripping or caulk.
5. Replace your old water heater with an energy-efficient heat pump model.
6. Install a programmable or smart thermostat to optimize heating and cooling schedules.
7. Upgrade to ENERGY STAR certified appliances when replacements are needed.
8. Add insulation to your attic, basement, or crawl spaces to reduce heating costs.
9. Use cold water for washing clothes and air-dry when possible.
10. Install solar panels or sign up for a community solar program in your area.

CATEGORY 2: Transportation & Travel
1. Walk, bike, or use public transit for trips under 3 miles instead of driving.
2. Carpool or use rideshare services to reduce the number of vehicles on the road.
3. Combine multiple errands into one trip to minimize total driving time.
4. Work from home at least one day per week if your job allows it.
5. Choose direct flights and pack light to reduce aviation fuel consumption.
6. Consider purchasing a hybrid or electric vehicle for your next car.
7. Keep your car well-maintained with proper tire pressure and regular tune-ups.
8. Plan staycations or choose destinations within driving distance instead of flying.
9. Use video conferencing for meetings instead of traveling for business when possible.
10. Offset unavoidable travel emissions through verified carbon offset programs.

CATEGORY 3: Food & Consumption
1. Eat one plant-based meal per day to reduce your dietary carbon footprint.
2. Buy local, seasonal produce from farmers markets or community-supported agriculture.
3. Reduce food waste by meal planning and using leftovers creatively.
4. Start composting food scraps to create nutrient-rich soil and reduce methane emissions.
5. Choose products with minimal packaging or packaging made from recycled materials.
6. Buy only what you need and avoid impulse purchases that create unnecessary waste.
7. Support companies with strong environmental commitments and sustainable practices.
8. Grow herbs, vegetables, or fruits in your yard or containers if space allows.
9. Repair items instead of replacing them whenever possible to extend their lifespan.
10. Shop secondhand for clothing, furniture, and household items before buying new.

CATEGORY 4: Water Conservation & Waste Reduction
1. Fix leaky faucets, toilets, and pipes immediately to prevent water waste.
2. Take shorter showers and install low-flow showerheads to reduce water usage.
3. Collect rainwater in barrels to use for watering plants and gardens.
4. Replace your lawn with native plants that require less water and maintenance.
5. Use a dishwasher instead of hand-washing dishes as it typically uses less water.
6. Install dual-flush toilets or place water-saving devices in existing toilet tanks.
7. Recycle paper, plastic, glass, and metal according to your local recycling guidelines.
8. Switch to reusable bags, water bottles, and food containers to reduce single-use plastics.
9. Donate or sell items you no longer need instead of throwing them away.
10. Choose products made from recycled materials to support the circular economy.

CATEGORY 5: Community Engagement & Advocacy
1. Vote for candidates who prioritize environmental protection and climate action policies.
2. Join or support local environmental organizations working on climate initiatives.
3. Attend town hall meetings to advocate for renewable energy and sustainability programs.
4. Organize or participate in community tree-planting events in your neighborhood.
5. Share climate action tips and resources with friends and family on social media.
6. Contact your elected representatives to express support for climate legislation.
7. Participate in local clean-up events for parks, beaches, or waterways.
8. Start or join a neighborhood sustainability group to coordinate collective action.
9. Support businesses that demonstrate genuine commitment to environmental responsibility.
10. Educate yourself about climate science and policy to become a more effective advocate.
