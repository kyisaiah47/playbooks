CATEGORY 1: Business Foundation & Planning
1. Define your core business idea and write a one-sentence value proposition.
2. Research your target market and identify your ideal customer persona.
3. Analyze 3-5 direct competitors and document their strengths and weaknesses.
4. Choose your business structure (LLC, corporation, sole proprietorship) and register it.
5. Create a simple business plan outlining your goals, strategy, and financial projections.
6. Determine your pricing strategy by researching competitor prices and calculating costs.
7. Secure necessary business licenses and permits for your industry and location.
8. Set up a separate business bank account and accounting system.
9. Define your unique selling proposition that differentiates you from competitors.
10. Create a launch timeline with specific milestones and deadlines.

CATEGORY 2: Digital Presence & Branding
1. Purchase a domain name that matches your business name or core service.
2. Design a professional logo and establish brand colors and fonts.
3. Build a simple website with essential pages (home, about, services, contact).
4. Write compelling website copy that speaks directly to your target audience.
5. Set up Google My Business listing with accurate contact information and photos.
6. Create professional social media profiles on platforms where your customers spend time.
7. Design business cards, letterhead, and other essential marketing materials.
8. Write a clear brand message and elevator pitch for networking opportunities.
9. Take professional headshots and product photos for your marketing materials.
10. Implement basic SEO on your website with relevant keywords and meta descriptions.

CATEGORY 3: Product & Service Development
1. Create your minimum viable product or define your core service offering.
2. Develop clear service packages or product descriptions with specific benefits.
3. Set up your fulfillment process for delivering products or services to customers.
4. Create templates, workflows, or systems to streamline your service delivery.
5. Test your product or service with a small group of beta customers for feedback.
6. Establish quality control standards and customer satisfaction metrics.
7. Develop a customer onboarding process that sets clear expectations.
8. Create FAQ documents addressing common customer questions and concerns.
9. Set up inventory management systems if selling physical products.
10. Design a process for collecting and implementing customer feedback.

CATEGORY 4: Marketing & Customer Acquisition
1. Create a content marketing strategy with blog posts, videos, or social media content.
2. Set up email marketing software and create a lead magnet to build your list.
3. Launch your website and announce your business to friends, family, and networks.
4. Join relevant online communities and forums where your target customers gather.
5. Create and implement a social media posting schedule with valuable content.
6. Develop partnerships or collaboration opportunities with complementary businesses.
7. Set up Google Analytics and other tracking tools to monitor website performance.
8. Create customer referral incentives to encourage word-of-mouth marketing.
9. Plan and execute a soft launch campaign to generate initial buzz and customers.
10. Establish a system for following up with leads and converting them to customers.

CATEGORY 5: Operations & Growth Management
1. Set up project management tools to track tasks, deadlines, and client work.
2. Create standard operating procedures for all recurring business tasks.
3. Establish regular financial review processes to track revenue and expenses.
4. Set up customer support systems including help desk software or chat tools.
5. Create contracts, terms of service, and other legal documents to protect your business.
6. Develop a system for managing customer relationships and communication.
7. Set quarterly business goals and key performance indicators to measure success.
8. Create a backup plan for critical business data and important documents.
9. Establish boundaries for work hours and develop systems for work-life balance.
10. Plan your first business review to analyze what's working and what needs improvement.
