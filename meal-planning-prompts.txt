CATEGORY 1: Planning & Preparation
1. Set aside 30 minutes each week to plan your meals for the upcoming week.
2. Create a master list of your family's favorite meals to reference during planning.
3. Check your pantry and refrigerator before making your grocery list.
4. Plan meals around your weekly schedule, choosing quick options for busy nights.
5. Batch prep ingredients like washing vegetables and cooking grains on weekends.
6. Choose one new recipe to try each week to expand your meal repertoire.
7. Plan for leftovers by cooking larger portions of meals that reheat well.
8. Keep a running grocery list throughout the week as you notice items running low.
9. Schedule your grocery shopping for the same day each week to build consistency.
10. Prepare a backup list of 5-ingredient meals for when plans fall through.

CATEGORY 2: Smart Shopping & Budgeting
1. Shop with a detailed grocery list organized by store sections to save time.
2. Compare unit prices rather than package prices to find the best deals.
3. Buy seasonal produce when it's at peak flavor and lowest cost.
4. Stock up on non-perishable staples when they're on sale.
5. Use store loyalty programs and apps to access digital coupons and discounts.
6. Shop the perimeter of the store first for fresh, whole foods.
7. Set a weekly grocery budget and track your spending to stay on target.
8. Buy generic or store brands for basic ingredients to reduce costs.
9. Avoid shopping when hungry to prevent impulse purchases.
10. Consider buying in bulk for frequently used items with long shelf lives.

CATEGORY 3: Meal Prep & Cooking
1. Dedicate 2-3 hours on Sunday to prepare components for the week's meals.
2. Cook proteins in bulk and use them in different meals throughout the week.
3. Chop all vegetables at once and store them properly for easy weeknight cooking.
4. Prepare mason jar salads or grain bowls for grab-and-go lunches.
5. Use a slow cooker or pressure cooker for hands-off meal preparation.
6. Double recipes and freeze half for future quick meals.
7. Pre-portion snacks into individual containers to avoid overeating.
8. Prepare overnight oats, chia puddings, or smoothie packs for easy breakfasts.
9. Cook grains and legumes in large batches to use throughout the week.
10. Set up a mise en place system with prepped ingredients ready for quick assembly.

CATEGORY 4: Nutrition & Balance
1. Aim to fill half your plate with vegetables at lunch and dinner.
2. Include a protein source in every meal to help maintain steady energy levels.
3. Choose whole grains over refined grains whenever possible.
4. Plan meals that incorporate all food groups throughout the day.
5. Keep healthy snacks like nuts, fruits, and vegetables easily accessible.
6. Drink water with meals and keep a water bottle nearby throughout the day.
7. Plan for one meatless meal per week to increase vegetable and legume intake.
8. Read nutrition labels to make informed choices about packaged foods.
9. Practice portion control by using smaller plates and measuring serving sizes initially.
10. Include calcium-rich foods like dairy, leafy greens, or fortified alternatives daily.

CATEGORY 5: Organization & Sustainability
1. Use clear containers and labels to organize your pantry and refrigerator.
2. Implement a first-in, first-out system to use older ingredients before newer ones.
3. Keep a well-stocked spice rack to add variety to simple ingredients.
4. Create designated spaces for meal prep containers and cooking tools.
5. Use up produce that's starting to wilt in soups, smoothies, or stir-fries.
6. Compost food scraps or use them to make homemade vegetable broth.
7. Store leftovers in clear, dated containers so they don't get forgotten.
8. Keep a running inventory of freezer contents to avoid food waste.
9. Repurpose dinner leftovers into new meals like grain bowls or wraps.
10. Clean as you cook to maintain an organized and efficient kitchen workspace.
