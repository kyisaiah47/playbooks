CATEGORY 1: Business Foundation & Planning
1. Research your target market and identify your ideal customer demographics.
2. Write a clear mission statement that explains your business purpose in one sentence.
3. Choose a memorable business name and check domain availability.
4. Create a simple business plan outlining your products, services, and revenue model.
5. Determine your startup costs and create a basic financial projection.
6. Research your competition and identify what makes your business unique.
7. Define your core products or services with specific pricing.
8. Set realistic revenue goals for your first year of operation.
9. Choose your business structure (LLC, corporation, sole proprietorship).
10. Create a timeline for launching your business with key milestones.

CATEGORY 2: Legal & Administrative Setup
1. Register your business name with the appropriate state or local authorities.
2. Obtain your federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS.
3. Apply for necessary business licenses and permits for your industry.
4. Open a dedicated business bank account separate from personal finances.
5. Get business insurance coverage appropriate for your industry and risks.
6. Set up a simple bookkeeping system to track income and expenses.
7. Register for state and local taxes if required in your area.
8. Create basic contracts or terms of service for your customers.
9. Set up a system for collecting and remitting sales tax if applicable.
10. Establish a business address and consider mail forwarding if working from home.

CATEGORY 3: Marketing & Brand Development
1. Design a simple logo that represents your business professionally.
2. Create business cards with your contact information and services.
3. Build a basic website with your services, contact info, and pricing.
4. Set up social media accounts on platforms where your customers spend time.
5. Write compelling descriptions of your products or services for marketing materials.
6. Take professional photos of your products, services, or workspace.
7. Create a Google My Business listing to appear in local search results.
8. Develop a consistent brand voice and messaging across all communications.
9. Design basic marketing materials like flyers or brochures.
10. Plan your launch announcement strategy for friends, family, and potential customers.

CATEGORY 4: Operations & Systems
1. Set up a workspace that allows you to work efficiently and professionally.
2. Choose and implement tools for managing customers and orders.
3. Create standard operating procedures for delivering your product or service.
4. Set up a reliable system for invoicing and collecting payments.
5. Establish inventory management if you're selling physical products.
6. Create customer onboarding processes that ensure smooth project starts.
7. Set up appointment scheduling if you provide services by appointment.
8. Develop quality control checklists to maintain consistent service delivery.
9. Create templates for common communications with customers.
10. Establish backup plans for key business operations and data.

CATEGORY 5: Sales & Customer Acquisition
1. Identify and list 50 potential customers or businesses that might need your services.
2. Practice your elevator pitch until you can deliver it confidently in 30 seconds.
3. Reach out to friends and family to let them know about your new business.
4. Offer a special discount or promotion to your first 10 customers.
5. Ask satisfied customers to refer others and provide incentives for referrals.
6. Join local networking groups or business associations in your area.
7. Create valuable content that demonstrates your expertise to potential customers.
8. Follow up with all leads within 24 hours of initial contact.
9. Develop partnerships with complementary businesses that serve similar customers.
10. Track which marketing activities generate the most leads and focus on those.
