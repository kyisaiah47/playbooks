CATEGORY 1: Team Formation and Planning
1. Research local sports leagues and their registration requirements for your age group.
2. Determine the sport you want to play and the level of competition you're seeking.
3. Set a budget for team fees, equipment, and travel expenses.
4. Find other interested players through community boards, social media, or local networks.
5. Schedule a meeting with potential teammates to discuss goals and commitment levels.
6. Choose team colors, name, and basic branding elements.
7. Identify potential practice locations and check availability and costs.
8. Research and contact qualified coaches or volunteer to coach yourself.
9. Create a team communication channel using apps like GroupMe or Slack.
10. Establish team rules, expectations, and a basic code of conduct.

CATEGORY 2: Registration and Legal Requirements
1. Complete league registration forms and submit required documentation.
2. Gather medical clearance forms and insurance information for all players.
3. Pay league registration fees and secure your spot in the division.
4. Obtain necessary permits for practice facilities if using public spaces.
5. Set up team insurance coverage through the league or independently.
6. Create player contracts outlining responsibilities and commitments.
7. Register your team name officially with the league to avoid conflicts.
8. Submit roster information with player contact details to league officials.
9. Review and sign liability waivers for all team activities.
10. Ensure all players meet age, residency, or skill requirements for the league.

CATEGORY 3: Equipment and Uniforms
1. Create a comprehensive list of required equipment for your sport.
2. Research suppliers and compare prices for bulk equipment purchases.
3. Order team uniforms with consistent sizing for all players.
4. Purchase essential safety equipment like helmets, pads, or protective gear.
5. Set up a team equipment storage system at practice or game locations.
6. Establish rules for who provides personal vs. team equipment.
7. Create a maintenance schedule for shared team equipment.
8. Order backup equipment for practices and emergency replacements.
9. Design and order team warm-up gear or practice uniforms.
10. Set up an equipment check-out system to track team property.

CATEGORY 4: Training and Practice Management
1. Create a season-long practice schedule with consistent days and times.
2. Develop basic drills and exercises appropriate for your team's skill level.
3. Set individual and team fitness goals for the season.
4. Schedule scrimmage games against other teams for practice experience.
5. Organize position-specific training sessions to develop specialized skills.
6. Plan team-building activities to improve chemistry and communication.
7. Track attendance and establish policies for missed practices.
8. Create a system for evaluating player progress throughout the season.
9. Schedule guest coaches or clinics to introduce new techniques.
10. Develop game strategies and practice executing them in controlled settings.

CATEGORY 5: Game Day and Season Operations
1. Create a game day checklist covering equipment, transportation, and timing.
2. Establish pre-game warm-up routines and team rituals.
3. Develop a player rotation system to ensure everyone gets playing time.
4. Set up a communication system for weather delays or schedule changes.
5. Organize team transportation to away games or arrange carpools.
6. Plan post-game activities or team meals to celebrate efforts.
7. Track game statistics and individual player performance metrics.
8. Schedule regular team meetings to discuss strategy and address concerns.
9. Coordinate with league officials regarding game schedules and rule clarifications.
10. Plan an end-of-season celebration or awards ceremony for the team.
