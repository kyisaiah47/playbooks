CATEGORY 1: Research and Planning
1. Determine your budget including down payment, monthly payments, and insurance costs.
2. Research reliable car models that fit your lifestyle and needs.
3. Check safety ratings and reviews for your top vehicle choices.
4. Compare fuel efficiency ratings between different models you're considering.
5. Research the resale value and depreciation rates of potential vehicles.
6. Identify must-have features versus nice-to-have options for your new car.
7. Research common problems and maintenance costs for specific makes and models.
8. Determine whether buying new, certified pre-owned, or used makes most sense for your situation.
9. Check your credit score and understand how it affects your financing options.
10. Set a realistic timeline for your car buying process.

CATEGORY 2: Financing and Insurance
1. Get pre-approved for an auto loan from your bank or credit union.
2. Compare interest rates from multiple lenders including dealership financing.
3. Calculate the total cost of ownership including loan interest over the life of the loan.
4. Research current auto loan rates and terms in your area.
5. Get insurance quotes for the specific vehicles you're considering buying.
6. Understand the difference between financing and leasing options.
7. Calculate how much trade-in value you can get for your current vehicle.
8. Review your current auto insurance policy and coverage needs.
9. Factor in gap insurance costs if you're financing or leasing.
10. Understand all fees associated with your financing options.

CATEGORY 3: Shopping and Inspection
1. Visit multiple dealerships to compare prices and inventory.
2. Test drive each vehicle you're seriously considering purchasing.
3. Inspect the exterior and interior condition of any used vehicle thoroughly.
4. Check the vehicle history report using the VIN number.
5. Have a qualified mechanic inspect any used car before buying.
6. Compare prices from different dealers for the same make and model.
7. Research the vehicle's maintenance records if buying used.
8. Test all electronic systems, lights, and safety features during your inspection.
9. Check tire condition and ask about recent maintenance or repairs.
10. Verify that all recalls have been addressed on the vehicle.

CATEGORY 4: Negotiation and Purchase
1. Research the fair market value of the specific vehicle using pricing guides.
2. Negotiate the total price of the vehicle separate from trade-in value.
3. Review and understand all charges on the sales contract before signing.
4. Negotiate the interest rate and terms if using dealer financing.
5. Decline unnecessary add-ons and extended warranties unless truly needed.
6. Get all verbal agreements and promises in writing on the contract.
7. Verify the vehicle identification number matches all paperwork.
8. Understand the return policy and any cooling-off period laws in your state.
9. Review warranty coverage and understand what is and isn't included.
10. Arrange final payment method and confirm all financing details.

CATEGORY 5: Completion and Ownership
1. Complete the title transfer and registration process in your state.
2. Update your auto insurance policy before driving the vehicle home.
3. Ensure you have proper documentation including title, registration, and insurance cards.
4. Schedule your first maintenance appointment according to the owner's manual.
5. Register for any manufacturer warranties or service programs.
6. Add the vehicle to your insurance policy and cancel coverage on your trade-in.
7. Create a maintenance schedule and keep detailed records of all services.
8. Familiarize yourself with all vehicle features and controls before regular driving.
9. Store important vehicle documents in a safe and accessible location.
10. Review your financing statements and set up automatic payments if desired.
