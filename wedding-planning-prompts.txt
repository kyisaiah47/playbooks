CATEGORY 1: Budget & Finance Planning
1. Set your overall wedding budget based on what you can realistically afford.
2. Research average costs for weddings in your area to establish realistic expectations.
3. Create a detailed spreadsheet breaking down costs by category (venue, food, flowers, etc.).
4. Decide what percentage of your budget to allocate to each major expense category.
5. Open a dedicated wedding savings account to track your funds separately.
6. Determine which family members or friends might contribute financially and discuss amounts.
7. Research and compare wedding loan options if additional funding is needed.
8. Set up automatic monthly transfers to your wedding savings account.
9. Track all wedding-related expenses as you make them to stay on budget.
10. Build in a 10-15% buffer for unexpected costs and last-minute additions.

CATEGORY 2: Venue & Date Selection
1. Choose your preferred wedding season and identify 3-5 potential dates.
2. Create a list of must-have venue features (indoor/outdoor, capacity, location, etc.).
3. Research and visit at least 5 different venue options in your preferred area.
4. Ask each venue about their availability for your preferred dates.
5. Compare venue pricing packages and what's included versus additional costs.
6. Check if your preferred venues have preferred vendor lists or restrictions.
7. Consider backup indoor options if you're planning an outdoor wedding.
8. Book your venue at least 12-18 months in advance for popular dates.
9. Read the venue contract carefully and understand cancellation policies.
10. Confirm the venue's setup and cleanup policies and any additional fees.

CATEGORY 3: Guest List & Invitations
1. Create a master guest list with both partners contributing names and contact information.
2. Categorize guests into tiers (must invite, would like to invite, if space allows).
3. Confirm your guest count fits within your venue capacity and budget per person.
4. Collect current mailing addresses for all guests you plan to invite.
5. Send save-the-date cards 6-8 months before your wedding date.
6. Choose your invitation design and order them at least 3 months in advance.
7. Mail invitations 6-8 weeks before the wedding with RSVP deadline 3-4 weeks prior.
8. Set up a wedding website with details about accommodations, registry, and schedule.
9. Follow up with guests who haven't responded by your RSVP deadline.
10. Create a final headcount for catering and seating arrangements based on confirmed RSVPs.

CATEGORY 4: Vendors & Services
1. Research and book a photographer whose style matches your vision.
2. Find and hire a caterer or confirm catering options with your venue.
3. Interview and select a wedding officiant who aligns with your ceremony preferences.
4. Book musicians, DJ, or band for your ceremony and reception entertainment.
5. Choose a florist and discuss your vision for bouquets, centerpieces, and decorations.
6. Research hair and makeup artists and schedule a trial run before the wedding.
7. Arrange transportation for the wedding party and guests if needed.
8. Book accommodations for out-of-town guests and reserve room blocks.
9. Hire a wedding coordinator or day-of coordinator to manage logistics.
10. Confirm all vendor contracts include specific dates, times, services, and cancellation policies.

CATEGORY 5: Personal Preparations & Details
1. Shop for and purchase your wedding dress, suit, or outfit with enough time for alterations.
2. Choose wedding rings and allow 4-6 weeks for sizing and engraving.
3. Plan your ceremony script, including vows, readings, and any special traditions.
4. Create a timeline for your wedding day from getting ready through the reception.
5. Arrange for someone to handle gifts and cards received at the wedding.
6. Plan your honeymoon destination and make travel arrangements.
7. Apply for marriage license according to your local requirements and timeline.
8. Write personalized vows and practice delivering them aloud.
9. Plan a rehearsal dinner for the wedding party and close family members.
10. Create an emergency kit with items like stain remover, safety pins, and pain relievers for wedding day.
