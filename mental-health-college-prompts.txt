CATEGORY 1: Academic Support & Accommodations
1. Schedule a meeting with your disability services office to discuss available mental health accommodations.
2. Register for reduced course loads if you're struggling to manage a full academic schedule.
3. Request extended deadlines for assignments during particularly difficult mental health periods.
4. Arrange for a quiet testing environment to reduce anxiety during exams.
5. Connect with academic advisors to create a flexible degree plan that accounts for mental health needs.
6. Explore pass/fail grading options for courses during challenging semesters.
7. Identify professors who are understanding about mental health challenges and prioritize their classes.
8. Set up regular check-ins with academic support staff to monitor your progress.
9. Create a backup plan for missed classes due to mental health days.
10. Research withdrawal and re-enrollment policies in case you need to take a mental health break.

CATEGORY 2: Campus Mental Health Resources
1. Schedule an intake appointment at your college counseling center.
2. Join a support group specifically for students dealing with similar mental health challenges.
3. Download your campus mental health app or familiarize yourself with online resources.
4. Locate the nearest crisis intervention services and save emergency contact numbers.
5. Attend mental health workshops or seminars offered by your wellness center.
6. Connect with peer counselors or mental health ambassadors on campus.
7. Explore group therapy options available through your student health services.
8. Research whether your campus offers specialized programs for anxiety, depression, or other conditions.
9. Find out about psychiatric services available through your college health center.
10. Create a mental health crisis plan with campus counselors before you need it.

CATEGORY 3: Daily Routine & Self-Care
1. Establish a consistent sleep schedule that allows for 7-9 hours of rest each night.
2. Schedule regular meals and snacks to maintain stable blood sugar and energy levels.
3. Build in daily movement, whether it's walking to class, gym time, or stretching breaks.
4. Create a morning routine that helps you start each day with intention and calm.
5. Set boundaries around social media and news consumption to protect your mental space.
6. Practice a daily mindfulness or meditation routine, even if just for 5 minutes.
7. Schedule weekly self-care activities that bring you joy and relaxation.
8. Maintain personal hygiene routines even when depression makes them feel difficult.
9. Create a comfortable and organized living space that supports your mental well-being.
10. Establish an evening wind-down routine to help transition from day stress to rest.

CATEGORY 4: Social Connections & Relationships
1. Join clubs or organizations that align with your interests to meet like-minded peers.
2. Maintain regular contact with supportive family members and friends from home.
3. Be open with close friends about your mental health needs and boundaries.
4. Attend campus events and activities to expand your social network beyond academics.
5. Find a study group or academic partner who understands your mental health challenges.
6. Practice saying no to social commitments when you need time for mental health care.
7. Seek out mentorship opportunities with upperclassmen who have similar experiences.
8. Join online communities or forums for college students with mental health challenges.
9. Develop healthy conflict resolution skills for roommate and peer relationships.
10. Consider getting involved in mental health advocacy groups on campus.

CATEGORY 5: Professional Development & Future Planning
1. Connect with career counselors who understand how mental health impacts professional goals.
2. Research careers and industries that are known for supporting employee mental health.
3. Develop a portfolio of skills and experiences that demonstrate your resilience and growth.
4. Practice disclosing your mental health needs appropriately in internship and job interviews.
5. Build relationships with professors who can serve as references and understand your journey.
6. Explore graduate school options and their mental health support systems if applicable.
7. Develop professional skills like time management and communication that support mental wellness.
8. Research workplace accommodations you might need and how to advocate for them.
9. Build a professional network that includes mental health awareness and advocacy.
10. Create a post-graduation mental health transition plan before your final semester.
