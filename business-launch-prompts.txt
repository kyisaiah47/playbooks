CATEGORY 1: Business Planning & Strategy
1. Write a one-page business idea summary describing what you'll sell and to whom.
2. Research three direct competitors and analyze their pricing, services, and customer reviews.
3. Define your target customer by creating a detailed profile including demographics and pain points.
4. Choose your business structure (LLC, corporation, etc.) and research the requirements in your state.
5. Create a simple financial projection showing expected income and expenses for the first year.
6. Write your unique value proposition in one clear sentence that differentiates you from competitors.
7. Decide on your business name and check if the domain name and social media handles are available.
8. Research the licensing and permit requirements for your specific business type and location.
9. Create a basic business model outlining how you'll make money and serve customers.
10. Set three specific, measurable goals you want to achieve in your first six months.

CATEGORY 2: Legal & Administrative Setup
1. Register your business name with your state or local government office.
2. Obtain your Federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS website.
3. Open a dedicated business bank account to keep personal and business finances separate.
4. Research and purchase appropriate business insurance coverage for your industry.
5. Apply for all required business licenses and permits specific to your location and industry.
6. Set up a simple bookkeeping system to track income, expenses, and receipts.
7. Choose and register with a business credit card to start building business credit history.
8. Create basic legal documents like terms of service, privacy policy, or client contracts.
9. Register for state and local tax requirements and understand your tax obligations.
10. Set up a system for organizing and storing all important business documents.

CATEGORY 3: Financial Management & Funding
1. Calculate your startup costs including equipment, inventory, marketing, and operating expenses.
2. Determine how much personal savings you can invest in your business launch.
3. Research funding options like small business loans, grants, or investor opportunities.
4. Create a simple monthly budget showing projected income versus expenses.
5. Set up a business emergency fund with at least three months of operating expenses.
6. Choose accounting software or tools to track your business finances automatically.
7. Establish a pricing strategy that covers costs and provides reasonable profit margins.
8. Open a high-yield business savings account to earn interest on excess funds.
9. Research and apply for business credit cards that offer rewards or cashback benefits.
10. Create a plan for paying yourself a salary once the business becomes profitable.

CATEGORY 4: Marketing & Customer Acquisition
1. Create a simple website or landing page that clearly explains what you offer.
2. Set up business profiles on relevant social media platforms where your customers spend time.
3. Design a basic logo and choose consistent colors and fonts for your brand identity.
4. Write a list of 20 potential customers and plan how you'll reach out to them.
5. Create business cards and basic marketing materials with your contact information.
6. Develop a content plan for sharing helpful information related to your business expertise.
7. Research and join local business networks, chambers of commerce, or industry associations.
8. Set up Google My Business listing to help local customers find your business online.
9. Create a simple email signup form to collect contact information from interested prospects.
10. Plan your launch announcement strategy including who you'll tell and how you'll spread the word.

CATEGORY 5: Operations & Growth Planning
1. Create a daily routine and schedule for managing your business tasks and priorities.
2. Set up systems for delivering your product or service consistently to customers.
3. Establish quality control processes to ensure customer satisfaction with every interaction.
4. Create templates for common business communications like emails, proposals, and invoices.
5. Develop a simple customer onboarding process that creates a great first impression.
6. Set up systems for collecting and responding to customer feedback and reviews.
7. Create a basic employee handbook if you plan to hire staff in the future.
8. Research and choose the technology tools you'll need to run your business efficiently.
9. Plan how you'll measure business success and track key performance indicators.
10. Develop a growth strategy for scaling your business once you've proven the concept works.
