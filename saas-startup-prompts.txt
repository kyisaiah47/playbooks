CATEGORY 1: Idea Validation & Market Research
1. Identify a specific problem that frustrates you or people you know on a daily basis.
2. Survey 50 potential customers to understand their biggest pain points in your target area.
3. Research your top 5 competitors to understand their pricing, features, and customer complaints.
4. Create a simple landing page describing your solution and collect email signups for validation.
5. Conduct 10 one-on-one interviews with people in your target market about their current solutions.
6. Calculate the total addressable market size for your proposed solution.
7. Define your ideal customer persona including demographics, behaviors, and motivations.
8. Test your problem-solution fit by presenting your idea to 20 potential customers.
9. Research industry trends and growth projections for your target market.
10. Validate that people are willing to pay for your solution by running a pre-order campaign.

CATEGORY 2: Product Development & MVP
1. Write a detailed product requirements document outlining your core features.
2. Create wireframes or mockups for your minimum viable product's key user flows.
3. Choose your technology stack based on your team's skills and product requirements.
4. Build a basic prototype that demonstrates your core value proposition.
5. Set up your development environment with version control and deployment pipelines.
6. Implement user authentication and basic account management functionality.
7. Develop your most critical feature that solves the primary customer problem.
8. Create a simple onboarding flow to help new users understand your product.
9. Build basic analytics to track user behavior and product usage.
10. Launch a beta version to a small group of early adopters for feedback.

CATEGORY 3: Business Model & Operations
1. Choose your revenue model (subscription, freemium, one-time payment, or usage-based).
2. Set up your business entity (LLC, corporation) and obtain necessary licenses.
3. Open a business bank account and implement basic accounting systems.
4. Determine your pricing strategy based on competitor analysis and value provided.
5. Create standard operating procedures for customer support and issue resolution.
6. Set up payment processing with Stripe, PayPal, or similar payment gateway.
7. Implement basic customer relationship management (CRM) system.
8. Establish key performance indicators (KPIs) to measure business success.
9. Create a basic financial model projecting revenue, costs, and profitability.
10. Set up legal protections including terms of service, privacy policy, and user agreements.

CATEGORY 4: Marketing & Customer Acquisition
1. Create valuable content (blog posts, tutorials, guides) that attracts your target audience.
2. Build an email list by offering a free resource relevant to your target market.
3. Launch a social media presence on platforms where your customers spend time.
4. Implement search engine optimization (SEO) to improve organic discovery.
5. Set up Google Ads or Facebook Ads campaigns targeting your ideal customers.
6. Partner with complementary businesses or influencers for cross-promotion opportunities.
7. Create customer case studies and testimonials to build social proof.
8. Attend industry events, conferences, or networking meetups to build relationships.
9. Launch a referral program to incentivize existing customers to bring in new users.
10. Track and optimize your customer acquisition cost (CAC) and lifetime value (LTV).

CATEGORY 5: Growth & Scaling
1. Hire your first employee or contractor to handle tasks outside your core expertise.
2. Implement customer success processes to reduce churn and increase retention.
3. Add new features based on customer feedback and usage data analysis.
4. Expand into adjacent market segments or use cases for your product.
5. Raise funding from investors if needed to accelerate growth and expansion.
6. Build integrations with popular tools your customers already use.
7. Create an affiliate or partner program to expand your distribution channels.
8. Implement advanced analytics to better understand user behavior and optimize conversion.
9. Scale your customer support team and implement help desk software.
10. Plan international expansion or new product lines based on market opportunities.
