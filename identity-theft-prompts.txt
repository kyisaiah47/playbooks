CATEGORY 1: Immediate Response & Documentation
1. Contact your bank and credit card companies immediately to report potential fraudulent activity.
2. File a report with your local police department to create an official record of the identity theft.
3. Take screenshots or photos of any suspicious accounts, charges, or communications you've received.
4. Create a dedicated folder (physical or digital) to store all identity theft related documents and correspondence.
5. Write down a detailed timeline of when you first noticed signs of identity theft and any suspicious activity.
6. Contact the fraud departments of the three major credit bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion).
7. Change passwords immediately for all your online accounts, starting with financial institutions.
8. File a complaint with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at IdentityTheft.gov.
9. Contact your employer's HR department if your Social Security number or work information may have been compromised.
10. Notify your insurance companies (health, auto, homeowners) about the potential identity theft.

CATEGORY 2: Credit Protection & Monitoring
1. Place a fraud alert on your credit reports with all three major credit bureaus.
2. Request free copies of your credit reports from all three bureaus to review for unauthorized accounts.
3. Consider placing a credit freeze on your credit reports to prevent new accounts from being opened.
4. Dispute any unauthorized accounts or charges you find on your credit reports in writing.
5. Set up credit monitoring services to alert you of any new activity on your credit reports.
6. Check your credit scores regularly through free services or your bank's app.
7. Review and update your credit report contact information to ensure you receive important notices.
8. Keep detailed records of all credit-related correspondence and dispute letters you send.
9. Follow up on all credit disputes within 30 days if you don't receive a response.
10. Consider enrolling in identity theft protection services for ongoing monitoring.

CATEGORY 3: Account Security & Recovery
1. Close any accounts that you know or suspect have been tampered with or opened fraudulently.
2. Open new checking and savings accounts at your bank with new account numbers.
3. Update your direct deposit information with your employer for your new bank accounts.
4. Change your Social Security number if the theft is severe and ongoing (contact Social Security Administration).
5. Replace your driver's license and state ID if they were stolen or compromised.
6. Update automatic bill payments and recurring charges with your new account information.
7. Contact subscription services and online retailers to update your payment methods.
8. Review and secure your email accounts, as they're often gateways to other accounts.
9. Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts that offer it, especially financial and email accounts.
10. Create a new PIN for your debit cards and update security questions for online accounts.

CATEGORY 4: Legal & Administrative Actions
1. Obtain an Identity Theft Report by combining your FTC complaint with your police report.
2. Send certified letters to creditors disputing fraudulent accounts and requesting debt removal.
3. Contact the IRS if your Social Security number was used for tax-related fraud.
4. File a complaint with your state's attorney general's office about the identity theft.
5. Contact the Social Security Administration if someone is using your SSN for employment.
6. Reach out to your state's Department of Motor Vehicles if your license was used fraudulently.
7. Contact your health insurance provider if medical identity theft is suspected.
8. File complaints with relevant regulatory agencies for any industry-specific fraud (banking, healthcare, etc.).
9. Keep detailed logs of all phone calls, including dates, times, and representative names.
10. Consider consulting with an identity theft attorney if the case is complex or damages are significant.

CATEGORY 5: Prevention & Long-term Recovery
1. Sign up for free annual credit reports and review them thoroughly each year.
2. Shred all documents containing personal information before disposing of them.
3. Use secure, unique passwords for all online accounts and consider a password manager.
4. Be cautious about sharing personal information over phone, email, or text unless you initiated the contact.
5. Regularly review bank statements and credit card statements for unauthorized charges.
6. Opt out of pre-approved credit offers to reduce mail-based identity theft risks.
7. Secure your mailbox and consider a P.O. Box for important financial correspondence.
8. Keep your Social Security card in a safe place and avoid carrying it in your wallet.
9. Be selective about what personal information you share on social media platforms.
10. Create a personal identity theft prevention checklist and review it annually to maintain good security habits.
