CATEGORY 1: Health and Medical Management
1. Schedule an annual comprehensive physical exam with your loved one's primary care physician.
2. Create a detailed list of all current medications including dosages and schedules.
3. Research and contact specialists recommended by the primary doctor for specific health conditions.
4. Set up a pill organizer system to ensure medications are taken correctly and on time.
5. Obtain copies of all medical records and keep them organized in a secure file.
6. Research Medicare coverage options and supplemental insurance plans available in your area.
7. Find and interview potential home health aides or nursing services if needed.
8. Install safety equipment like grab bars, shower seats, and non-slip mats in the bathroom.
9. Create an emergency medical information card with key health details and emergency contacts.
10. Schedule regular eye exams and hearing tests to monitor sensory health changes.

CATEGORY 2: Legal and Financial Planning
1. Meet with an elder law attorney to discuss wills, trusts, and estate planning options.
2. Set up power of attorney documents for both healthcare and financial decisions.
3. Review and update all insurance policies including life, health, and long-term care coverage.
4. Create a comprehensive budget that accounts for current and anticipated future care costs.
5. Research Social Security benefits and determine the optimal time to begin claiming.
6. Organize all important financial documents in a secure but accessible location.
7. Set up automatic bill payments for recurring expenses like utilities and insurance.
8. Research veteran benefits if applicable and apply for any available programs.
9. Consider setting up a special needs trust if long-term care funding will be needed.
10. Review and update beneficiaries on all retirement accounts and life insurance policies.

CATEGORY 3: Living Arrangements and Safety
1. Conduct a home safety assessment to identify and address potential fall hazards.
2. Research assisted living facilities in your area and schedule tours of top choices.
3. Interview and hire reliable housekeeping or home maintenance services as needed.
4. Install medical alert systems or emergency response devices for peace of mind.
5. Evaluate transportation options including senior shuttle services and ride-sharing programs.
6. Modify the home environment with better lighting, ramps, and accessible bathroom features.
7. Research aging-in-place services and contractors who specialize in home modifications.
8. Create a detailed emergency evacuation plan and practice it with your loved one.
9. Set up regular check-ins with neighbors or community members for social safety.
10. Evaluate whether current housing is sustainable long-term or if relocation is needed.

CATEGORY 4: Daily Care and Support Services
1. Establish routines for personal hygiene, meals, and medication management.
2. Research and arrange for meal delivery services or senior nutrition programs.
3. Find reliable transportation services for medical appointments and errands.
4. Hire trusted caregivers or companions for daily assistance and social interaction.
5. Set up grocery delivery or online shopping services to ensure adequate nutrition.
6. Arrange for regular housekeeping and laundry services to maintain a clean environment.
7. Create a daily schedule that includes physical activity appropriate for mobility level.
8. Research adult day programs that provide socialization and supervised activities.
9. Set up technology solutions like medication reminders or video calling for family contact.
10. Establish relationships with local pharmacies that offer delivery and consultation services.

CATEGORY 5: Emotional and Social Well-being
1. Help your loved one maintain connections with longtime friends through regular visits or calls.
2. Research senior centers and community programs that offer social activities and classes.
3. Find support groups for both the elder and family members navigating care challenges.
4. Encourage participation in religious or spiritual activities that have been meaningful.
5. Create memory books or photo albums to help preserve and share important life stories.
6. Schedule regular family gatherings or video calls to maintain strong relationships.
7. Research volunteer opportunities that match your loved one's interests and abilities.
8. Find mental health counselors who specialize in geriatric care and adjustment issues.
9. Encourage hobbies and creative activities that bring joy and maintain cognitive function.
10. Plan regular outings to places that hold special meaning or provide enjoyable experiences.
