CATEGORY 1: Research and Discovery
1. Create a list of your academic interests and potential majors you want to explore.
2. Research colleges that offer strong programs in your areas of interest.
3. Use college search websites to filter schools by location, size, and cost preferences.
4. Ask teachers, counselors, and family members for college recommendations based on your goals.
5. Attend virtual or in-person college fairs to discover new schools and gather information.
6. Read college guidebooks and rankings to understand different institutions' reputations.
7. Join online forums and social media groups for prospective college students.
8. Schedule informational interviews with current students or alumni from schools you're considering.
9. Research the job placement rates and career outcomes for graduates from target schools.
10. Create a spreadsheet to track and compare the colleges you're researching.

CATEGORY 2: Campus Visits and Exploration
1. Schedule official campus tours for your top choice colleges.
2. Plan overnight stays in dormitories to experience campus life firsthand.
3. Attend classes in your potential major to observe the teaching style and environment.
4. Meet with professors or department heads in your areas of academic interest.
5. Explore the campus independently to get a feel for the layout and atmosphere.
6. Visit the library, student center, and recreational facilities during your tour.
7. Talk to current students about their experiences and honest opinions of the school.
8. Check out the surrounding town or city to see if you'd enjoy living there.
9. Attend a sporting event, concert, or campus activity to experience school spirit.
10. Take photos and notes during each visit to help you remember and compare later.

CATEGORY 3: Academic and Career Planning
1. Meet with your high school guidance counselor to review graduation requirements and college prep courses.
2. Research specific degree programs and curriculum requirements at each target college.
3. Identify colleges with strong alumni networks in your desired career field.
4. Look into study abroad opportunities and international programs offered by each school.
5. Research internship and co-op programs available through each college's career services.
6. Compare the student-to-faculty ratios and average class sizes at different institutions.
7. Investigate research opportunities for undergraduates in your field of interest.
8. Review the academic support services like tutoring centers and writing labs available.
9. Look into honors programs or special academic tracks that might interest you.
10. Research graduate school acceptance rates and professional school partnerships.

CATEGORY 4: Financial Planning and Aid
1. Complete the FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) as early as possible.
2. Research and apply for merit-based scholarships offered by each college.
3. Look into external scholarship opportunities through local organizations and foundations.
4. Calculate the total cost of attendance including tuition, room, board, and personal expenses.
5. Compare financial aid packages and net costs between different colleges.
6. Research work-study programs and on-campus employment opportunities.
7. Meet with financial aid officers at target schools to discuss your specific situation.
8. Consider the long-term financial implications of student loans and debt levels.
9. Look into in-state vs. out-of-state tuition differences and residency requirements.
10. Create a budget plan for college expenses and discuss financing options with your family.

CATEGORY 5: Application Process and Decision Making
1. Create a master calendar with all application deadlines and requirements for each college.
2. Start working on your personal essay and application essays well in advance of deadlines.
3. Request letters of recommendation from teachers, counselors, or mentors early in the process.
4. Prepare for and schedule standardized tests like the SAT or ACT if required.
5. Gather all necessary documents including transcripts, test scores, and activity lists.
6. Submit applications by the stated deadlines, allowing extra time for technical issues.
7. Apply for early decision or early action if you have a clear first choice school.
8. Follow up with colleges to ensure all application materials have been received.
9. Compare acceptance letters and financial aid offers when they arrive in spring.
10. Make your final decision by May 1st and submit your enrollment deposit to secure your spot.
