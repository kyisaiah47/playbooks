CATEGORY 1: Getting Started & Education
1. Research the history and characteristics of your chosen antique category online.
2. Visit local antique shops to observe pricing and condition variations.
3. Join online forums or social media groups dedicated to your antique interest.
4. Purchase a reputable price guide or reference book for your collecting focus.
5. Attend a local antique show or flea market to see variety and meet dealers.
6. Learn to identify authentic maker's marks, signatures, or manufacturing details.
7. Study the difference between reproductions and genuine antiques in your category.
8. Set a realistic budget for both purchasing and maintaining your collection.
9. Find a local antique appraiser or expert willing to share knowledge.
10. Create a list of specific items or periods you want to focus on collecting.

CATEGORY 2: Finding & Acquiring Antiques
1. Check estate sale listings and arrive early for the best selection.
2. Build relationships with reputable antique dealers in your area.
3. Search online auction sites and marketplaces for specific pieces.
4. Visit garage sales and yard sales in established neighborhoods.
5. Explore antique malls and co-op shops for diverse inventory.
6. Network with other collectors who might sell or trade pieces.
7. Subscribe to auction house catalogs in your collecting specialty.
8. Ask family members if they have inherited pieces to sell or give away.
9. Check thrift stores and consignment shops regularly for hidden gems.
10. Attend liquidation sales from closing businesses or institutions.

CATEGORY 3: Authentication & Evaluation
1. Learn to use a magnifying glass to examine maker's marks and signatures.
2. Research the provenance or ownership history of expensive pieces.
3. Compare your potential purchase to documented examples in reference materials.
4. Check for signs of repair, restoration, or alteration that affect value.
5. Photograph all angles of an item before purchasing for later research.
6. Consult with experts or appraisers before making significant investments.
7. Learn the telltale signs of common fakes or reproductions in your category.
8. Verify age-appropriate wear patterns and patina on older pieces.
9. Document any certificates of authenticity or previous appraisal documents.
10. Use UV light or other tools to detect repairs or replacements when appropriate.

CATEGORY 4: Care & Preservation
1. Clean new acquisitions using appropriate methods for their materials.
2. Store items in climate-controlled environments away from direct sunlight.
3. Use archival-quality materials for wrapping and storing delicate pieces.
4. Take detailed photographs of each item's condition upon acquisition.
5. Handle pieces with clean hands or cotton gloves when necessary.
6. Keep antiques away from heat sources, humidity, and temperature fluctuations.
7. Learn proper display techniques that don't damage or stress the items.
8. Create a maintenance schedule for dusting and inspecting your collection.
9. Find conservation professionals for items needing professional restoration.
10. Document any changes in condition or necessary repairs over time.

CATEGORY 5: Organization & Documentation
1. Create a detailed inventory list with descriptions and purchase information.
2. Photograph each item from multiple angles for insurance purposes.
3. Organize receipts, certificates, and provenance documents in a filing system.
4. Research and record the estimated current market value of each piece.
5. Create labels or tags with acquisition date and key identifying information.
6. Develop a catalog system using spreadsheets or specialized collecting software.
7. Keep records of where and when each item was purchased or acquired.
8. Update your insurance policy to cover valuable additions to your collection.
9. Create a backup system for all digital photos and documentation.
10. Maintain a wish list of specific items you're actively seeking to acquire.
