CATEGORY 1: Market Research & Property Analysis
1. Research median home prices and rental rates in your target neighborhood over the past 3 years.
2. Identify the top 3 up-and-coming areas in your city with strong growth potential.
3. Calculate the price-to-rent ratio for 5 properties you're considering.
4. Analyze local employment rates and major employers in your target investment area.
5. Research average days on market for similar properties in your target neighborhood.
6. Study local zoning laws and future development plans that could affect property values.
7. Compare property taxes across different neighborhoods in your investment area.
8. Identify seasonal rental demand patterns in your target market.
9. Research local crime statistics and school ratings for family rental properties.
10. Analyze cash flow potential by calculating estimated monthly expenses versus rental income.

CATEGORY 2: Financing & Investment Strategy
1. Get pre-qualified for an investment property mortgage to understand your budget.
2. Calculate your target return on investment percentage for rental properties.
3. Research different loan options including conventional, FHA, and portfolio lenders.
4. Determine your down payment amount and secure financing sources.
5. Calculate closing costs and holding costs for properties you're considering.
6. Compare interest rates from at least 3 different mortgage lenders.
7. Decide between buy-and-hold, fix-and-flip, or BRRRR investment strategies.
8. Set up a separate business bank account for your real estate investments.
9. Create a monthly budget that includes mortgage, insurance, taxes, and maintenance reserves.
10. Research tax benefits and deductions available for real estate investors.

CATEGORY 3: Property Acquisition & Due Diligence
1. Schedule professional inspections for any property you're seriously considering.
2. Review property history including previous sales prices and any major repairs.
3. Walk through the neighborhood at different times of day and week.
4. Calculate renovation costs by getting quotes from licensed contractors.
5. Research the property's rental history and previous tenant experiences.
6. Verify property taxes and any pending assessments or liens.
7. Review homeowners association rules and fees if applicable.
8. Negotiate purchase price based on comparable sales and needed repairs.
9. Arrange for title insurance and review the title report for any issues.
10. Set a maximum purchase price and stick to it during negotiations.

CATEGORY 4: Property Management & Operations
1. Interview and hire a reliable property management company or prepare to self-manage.
2. Create a comprehensive lease agreement that complies with local laws.
3. Screen potential tenants using credit checks, references, and income verification.
4. Set competitive rental rates based on comparable properties in your area.
5. Establish relationships with reliable contractors for maintenance and repairs.
6. Create a system for collecting rent and handling late payments.
7. Purchase adequate landlord insurance to protect your investment.
8. Set up regular property inspections to maintain the condition of your rental.
9. Create an emergency fund for unexpected repairs and vacancy periods.
10. Develop a tenant retention strategy to minimize turnover costs.

CATEGORY 5: Portfolio Growth & Exit Strategies
1. Track your property's performance using key metrics like cash flow and appreciation.
2. Research opportunities to add value through renovations or rent increases.
3. Consider refinancing when interest rates drop or property values increase significantly.
4. Plan your next property purchase based on lessons learned from your first investment.
5. Evaluate whether to expand in the same market or diversify to new areas.
6. Calculate when it makes sense to sell based on market conditions and tax implications.
7. Research 1031 exchanges to defer capital gains taxes when selling investment properties.
8. Network with other real estate investors to find off-market deal opportunities.
9. Consider partnering with other investors to access larger or better properties.
10. Develop a 5-year plan for your real estate investment portfolio growth.
