CATEGORY 1: Immediate Crisis Response
1. Call 911 or emergency services if there is immediate danger to anyone's safety.
2. Remove all family members from any unsafe environment or situation.
3. Contact your primary care physician or crisis hotline for urgent medical or mental health concerns.
4. Secure essential documents like IDs, insurance cards, and medical records in a safe place.
5. Establish a temporary safe location for family members if your home is compromised.
6. Notify trusted family members or friends about the crisis situation.
7. Take photos or document any evidence of damage, injury, or relevant circumstances.
8. Contact your insurance company to report any property damage or relevant claims.
9. Ensure all family members have access to necessary medications and medical devices.
10. Create a simple communication plan so family members can reach each other.

CATEGORY 2: Financial Stability and Resources
1. Contact your bank to discuss payment deferrals or financial hardship programs.
2. Apply for emergency financial assistance through local government or nonprofit organizations.
3. Review and prioritize which bills must be paid immediately versus which can be delayed.
4. Contact utility companies to set up payment plans or request emergency assistance.
5. Gather all insurance policies to understand what coverage is available for your situation.
6. Apply for unemployment benefits or disability benefits if the crisis affects work ability.
7. Contact creditors to explain your situation and negotiate temporary payment arrangements.
8. Research and apply for emergency food assistance programs in your community.
9. Create a bare-bones emergency budget focusing only on essential expenses.
10. Set up automatic bill payments for critical services to avoid additional late fees.

CATEGORY 3: Legal and Administrative Actions
1. Contact a family law attorney if the crisis involves domestic violence, custody, or divorce issues.
2. Update your will, power of attorney, and emergency contact information.
3. File police reports for any criminal activity or domestic violence incidents.
4. Contact Child Protective Services or elder abuse hotlines if vulnerable family members are at risk.
5. Gather and organize all important legal documents in one secure location.
6. Apply for restraining orders or protective orders if family members are being threatened.
7. Contact your employer's HR department to discuss FMLA or emergency leave options.
8. Update insurance beneficiaries and emergency contacts on all accounts.
9. Research your state's crisis intervention and family support legal resources.
10. Document all interactions with authorities, service providers, and legal representatives.

CATEGORY 4: Communication and Support Systems
1. Create a family communication tree so everyone knows how to reach each other.
2. Join a local support group for families dealing with similar crisis situations.
3. Schedule regular family meetings to discuss progress and ongoing concerns.
4. Establish clear boundaries about what information is shared with extended family and friends.
5. Connect with your religious or spiritual community for emotional and practical support.
6. Find a family therapist or counselor who specializes in crisis intervention.
7. Create a simple system for updating key people about your family's status.
8. Identify which family members or friends can provide specific types of help when needed.
9. Set up regular check-ins with elderly or vulnerable family members.
10. Establish code words or signals that family members can use to communicate distress safely.

CATEGORY 5: Long-term Recovery and Planning
1. Create a realistic timeline for addressing different aspects of the crisis recovery.
2. Develop new family routines and structures that account for changed circumstances.
3. Research and apply for long-term housing assistance if your living situation has changed.
4. Enroll family members in appropriate therapy or counseling programs for ongoing support.
5. Create an emergency fund and savings plan to prepare for future unexpected events.
6. Update your family's emergency preparedness plan based on lessons learned from this crisis.
7. Schedule regular medical and mental health check-ups for all affected family members.
8. Research educational support services if children's schooling has been disrupted.
9. Develop a relapse prevention plan if the crisis involved substance abuse or mental health issues.
10. Create new family traditions and positive activities to help rebuild emotional connections.
