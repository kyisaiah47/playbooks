CATEGORY 1: Learning & Education
1. Research the difference between stocks, bonds, ETFs, and mutual funds.
2. Read one beginner-friendly book about stock market investing fundamentals.
3. Learn what market cap, P/E ratio, and dividend yield mean for stock evaluation.
4. Watch educational videos about technical analysis and chart reading basics.
5. Study the difference between value investing and growth investing strategies.
6. Understand how stock splits, dividends, and stock buybacks affect your investments.
7. Learn about different order types like market orders, limit orders, and stop-loss orders.
8. Research how taxes work on stock gains, dividends, and trading frequency.
9. Study major stock market indices like S&P 500, Dow Jones, and NASDAQ.
10. Learn about market volatility, bull markets, bear markets, and economic cycles.

CATEGORY 2: Account Setup & Platform Selection
1. Research and compare different online brokers for fees, features, and user reviews.
2. Open a brokerage account with your chosen platform and complete identity verification.
3. Link your bank account to your brokerage account for funding transfers.
4. Set up two-factor authentication and strong passwords for account security.
5. Download and familiarize yourself with your broker's mobile trading app.
6. Explore the platform's research tools, charts, and stock screening features.
7. Set up account alerts for price movements, news, and portfolio changes.
8. Configure your account settings for dividend reinvestment preferences.
9. Review and understand your broker's fee structure for trades and account maintenance.
10. Test the platform with a small practice trade to understand the interface.

CATEGORY 3: Financial Planning & Goal Setting
1. Determine how much money you can afford to invest without affecting essential expenses.
2. Set specific financial goals like retirement savings, house down payment, or emergency fund.
3. Decide on your investment timeline and whether you're investing for short or long-term goals.
4. Assess your risk tolerance and comfort level with potential investment losses.
5. Create a monthly budget that includes a dedicated amount for stock investments.
6. Build an emergency fund of 3-6 months expenses before investing significant amounts.
7. Pay off high-interest debt like credit cards before focusing on stock investments.
8. Determine what percentage of your portfolio should be in stocks versus bonds.
9. Set realistic return expectations based on historical market performance.
10. Plan how you'll handle market downturns without panic selling your investments.

CATEGORY 4: Research & Stock Selection
1. Use stock screeners to find companies that match your investment criteria.
2. Read a company's annual report (10-K) and quarterly earnings reports (10-Q).
3. Analyze a company's financial health using key metrics like debt-to-equity ratio.
4. Research the company's competitive position within its industry sector.
5. Check recent news and analyst ratings for stocks you're considering buying.
6. Compare a stock's current price to its 52-week high and low ranges.
7. Study the company's revenue growth, profit margins, and cash flow trends.
8. Research the management team's track record and company leadership quality.
9. Evaluate whether the stock price reflects the company's true value.
10. Create a watchlist of 10-15 stocks you want to monitor before making purchases.

CATEGORY 5: Trading Execution & Portfolio Management
1. Start with a small investment in 1-3 stocks to gain real trading experience.
2. Place your first stock purchase using a limit order to control the price you pay.
3. Set up stop-loss orders to automatically sell if stocks drop below acceptable levels.
4. Diversify your portfolio across different industries and company sizes.
5. Monitor your investments weekly but avoid checking prices multiple times daily.
6. Rebalance your portfolio quarterly to maintain your target asset allocation.
7. Record all trades in a spreadsheet to track performance and tax implications.
8. Reinvest dividends automatically to benefit from compound growth over time.
9. Review and adjust your investment strategy annually based on life changes.
10. Stay disciplined with your investment plan and avoid emotional buying or selling decisions.
