CATEGORY 1: Building Leadership Foundation
1. Schedule weekly one-on-one meetings with each team member to build rapport and understanding.
2. Define your management philosophy and core values in writing to guide decision-making.
3. Meet with your manager to clarify expectations and success metrics for your role.
4. Identify your natural leadership strengths through self-assessment or 360-degree feedback.
5. Create a 30-60-90 day plan outlining your priorities and goals as a new manager.
6. Establish clear boundaries between being a peer and being a leader with former colleagues.
7. Develop a personal mission statement that defines what kind of leader you want to become.
8. Shadow an experienced manager for a day to observe effective leadership practices.
9. Read one foundational management book and apply three key concepts to your role.
10. Join a new manager support group or find a mentor who has successfully transitioned to leadership.

CATEGORY 2: Team Communication & Relationships
1. Learn each team member's preferred communication style and adapt your approach accordingly.
2. Establish regular team meetings with clear agendas and consistent timing.
3. Practice active listening techniques during all team interactions and conversations.
4. Create psychological safety by encouraging questions and admitting when you don't know something.
5. Develop skills for giving both positive recognition and constructive feedback effectively.
6. Set up informal coffee chats or lunch meetings to build personal connections with team members.
7. Learn to facilitate productive meetings that respect everyone's time and input.
8. Create open channels for team members to share ideas, concerns, and suggestions anonymously.
9. Practice having difficult conversations with team members about performance or behavior issues.
10. Establish team norms and communication protocols that everyone understands and follows.

CATEGORY 3: Performance Management & Development
1. Set clear, measurable goals with each team member using SMART criteria.
2. Create individual development plans that align personal growth with business needs.
3. Learn to conduct fair and thorough performance reviews that motivate improvement.
4. Identify each team member's strengths and find ways to leverage them effectively.
5. Develop skills for coaching team members through challenges rather than solving problems for them.
6. Create opportunities for team members to take on stretch assignments and grow their skills.
7. Learn to recognize and address performance issues early before they become major problems.
8. Establish systems for tracking and measuring team performance against objectives.
9. Practice delegating meaningful work that develops others while achieving business goals.
10. Create succession planning discussions to help team members advance their careers.

CATEGORY 4: Strategic Thinking & Decision Making
1. Learn to balance short-term tactical needs with long-term strategic objectives.
2. Develop processes for gathering input from multiple stakeholders before making important decisions.
3. Create systems for prioritizing competing demands and resources across your team.
4. Practice communicating the "why" behind decisions to help your team understand context.
5. Learn to make decisions with incomplete information while managing risks appropriately.
6. Establish regular reviews of team goals and strategies to ensure alignment with company direction.
7. Develop skills for presenting your team's work and needs to senior leadership effectively.
8. Create contingency plans for common challenges your team might face.
9. Learn to think systematically about problems and consider unintended consequences of solutions.
10. Practice saying no to requests that don't align with your team's priorities and capacity.

CATEGORY 5: Personal Growth & Adaptation
1. Schedule regular reflection time to assess your management effectiveness and areas for improvement.
2. Seek feedback from your team, peers, and manager about your leadership performance.
3. Develop emotional intelligence skills to better manage your reactions under pressure.
4. Learn time management techniques that allow you to balance individual work with management duties.
5. Build resilience strategies to handle the increased stress and responsibility of leadership.
6. Create boundaries between work and personal life to prevent management burnout.
7. Develop confidence in your decision-making abilities while remaining open to course corrections.
8. Learn to manage up effectively by keeping your manager informed and asking for support when needed.
9. Practice self-compassion as you navigate the inevitable mistakes and learning experiences of new leadership.
10. Continuously update your skills through training, reading, or professional development opportunities.
