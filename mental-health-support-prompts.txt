CATEGORY 1: Self-Assessment and Awareness
1. Take a few minutes to identify your current emotional state and write it down.
2. Notice three physical sensations in your body right now and acknowledge them without judgment.
3. List five specific situations or triggers that tend to affect your mental health.
4. Rate your overall stress level on a scale of 1-10 and note what's contributing to it.
5. Identify one recurring negative thought pattern you've noticed this week.
6. Write down three emotions you've experienced today and what prompted them.
7. Reflect on your sleep quality over the past week and note any patterns.
8. Assess your current energy levels and identify what might be draining you.
9. Notice how you typically respond to stress and write down your usual patterns.
10. Identify one area of your mental health you'd like to focus on improving.

CATEGORY 2: Building Support Systems
1. Reach out to one trusted friend or family member and share how you're feeling.
2. Research mental health professionals in your area and save their contact information.
3. Join an online support group or community related to your specific challenges.
4. Schedule a check-in conversation with someone you trust for later this week.
5. Make a list of people you feel comfortable talking to about mental health.
6. Ask a friend or family member to be your accountability partner for self-care.
7. Connect with a mental health crisis hotline to have the number readily available.
8. Find a local peer support group and attend one meeting this month.
9. Tell one person about a specific way they can support you during difficult times.
10. Create a contact list of supportive people you can reach out to when needed.

CATEGORY 3: Professional Help and Treatment
1. Schedule an appointment with your primary care doctor to discuss your mental health.
2. Research different types of therapy and identify which approach interests you most.
3. Call three therapists in your area to ask about availability and approach.
4. Prepare a list of questions to ask a potential therapist during your first consultation.
5. Schedule an initial appointment with a mental health professional.
6. Ask your doctor about whether medication might be helpful for your situation.
7. Research your insurance coverage for mental health services and understand your benefits.
8. Create a list of your symptoms and concerns to discuss with a healthcare provider.
9. Schedule a follow-up appointment with your mental health provider to track progress.
10. Ask your therapist about homework or exercises you can do between sessions.

CATEGORY 4: Daily Coping and Self-Care
1. Practice deep breathing for five minutes when you feel overwhelmed.
2. Take a 10-minute walk outside to clear your mind and get fresh air.
3. Write in a journal for 10 minutes about your thoughts and feelings.
4. Practice one grounding technique when you notice anxiety or stress building.
5. Set a consistent bedtime routine to improve your sleep quality.
6. Limit social media or news consumption for one hour before bed.
7. Practice saying "no" to one commitment that feels overwhelming this week.
8. Engage in one creative activity that brings you joy for 20 minutes.
9. Prepare a healthy meal or snack to nourish your body and mind.
10. Set aside 15 minutes for meditation or mindfulness practice today.

CATEGORY 5: Crisis Management and Safety
1. Create a safety plan with specific steps to follow during a mental health crisis.
2. Save crisis hotline numbers in your phone under easy-to-find contacts.
3. Identify warning signs that indicate you need immediate professional help.
4. Remove or secure any items that could be harmful during a crisis.
5. Create a list of safe places you can go if you need immediate support.
6. Practice using your crisis plan during a less intense moment to familiarize yourself with it.
7. Tell a trusted person about your crisis plan and ask them to help you follow it.
8. Keep a crisis kit ready with comfort items, important numbers, and medications.
9. Know the location of your nearest emergency room or crisis center.
10. Schedule regular check-ins with your support system during particularly difficult periods.
