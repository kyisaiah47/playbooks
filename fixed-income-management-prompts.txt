CATEGORY 1: Income Assessment and Planning
1. Calculate your total monthly income from all sources including Social Security, pensions, and investments.
2. List all guaranteed income streams you can count on each month.
3. Identify any variable income sources that may fluctuate over time.
4. Document the start dates and amounts for any delayed benefits like Social Security.
5. Create a baseline monthly income budget using only your most reliable sources.
6. Research potential income streams you haven't tapped yet, like part-time work or rental income.
7. Calculate how inflation might affect your fixed income over the next 10 years.
8. Determine if your current income will cover your essential expenses.
9. Identify any income gaps that need to be filled through investments or other means.
10. Set up automatic deposits for all regular income sources into designated accounts.

CATEGORY 2: Expense Management and Budgeting
1. Track all your monthly expenses for at least 30 days to understand your spending patterns.
2. Categorize your expenses into essential, important, and discretionary spending.
3. Identify which expenses are fixed versus variable each month.
4. Look for subscription services or recurring charges you can cancel or reduce.
5. Research senior discounts, Medicare benefits, and other cost-saving programs available to you.
6. Create a monthly budget that prioritizes essential expenses first.
7. Establish an emergency fund to cover unexpected expenses without deriving from your regular budget.
8. Review and negotiate bills like insurance, utilities, and phone services for better rates.
9. Plan for seasonal expenses like property taxes, insurance premiums, and holiday spending.
10. Set up a system to review and adjust your budget quarterly as expenses change.

CATEGORY 3: Investment Strategy and Portfolio Management
1. Review your current investment portfolio to ensure it matches your risk tolerance and timeline.
2. Consider shifting toward more conservative, income-generating investments like bonds or dividend stocks.
3. Evaluate whether your asset allocation is appropriate for someone on a fixed income.
4. Research Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS) to help preserve purchasing power.
5. Look into creating a bond ladder to provide predictable income over time.
6. Assess whether you should convert some traditional IRA funds to a Roth IRA while in a lower tax bracket.
7. Consider municipal bonds if you're in a higher tax bracket to reduce tax liability.
8. Evaluate annuities as a way to guarantee income for life, weighing costs against benefits.
9. Review your investment fees and consider moving to lower-cost index funds or ETFs.
10. Determine a sustainable withdrawal rate from retirement accounts that won't deplete your savings too quickly.

CATEGORY 4: Healthcare and Insurance Planning
1. Understand your Medicare options and choose the best plan for your health needs and budget.
2. Research Medicare supplement insurance to cover gaps in your coverage.
3. Consider long-term care insurance to protect your assets from potential healthcare costs.
4. Create a healthcare budget that accounts for premiums, deductibles, and out-of-pocket maximums.
5. Organize all your insurance policies and understand what each covers.
6. Research prescription drug assistance programs if medication costs are straining your budget.
7. Find healthcare providers that accept Medicare and offer competitive rates.
8. Consider switching to generic medications where appropriate to reduce prescription costs.
9. Explore preventive care options that are covered by Medicare to avoid larger health issues later.
10. Set up a Health Savings Account (HSA) if you're eligible to pay for medical expenses tax-free.

CATEGORY 5: Estate Planning and Financial Security
1. Update your will to reflect your current financial situation and wishes.
2. Review and update beneficiaries on all retirement accounts, insurance policies, and investment accounts.
3. Consider creating a revocable living trust to avoid probate and manage assets if you become incapacitated.
4. Organize all important financial documents in one accessible location for your family.
5. Establish power of attorney for both financial and healthcare decisions.
6. Review your life insurance needs and consider whether you still need coverage or can reduce premiums.
7. Plan how you want to distribute assets during your lifetime versus after death for tax efficiency.
8. Document your wishes for end-of-life care and financial management in case of incapacity.
9. Consider the tax implications of leaving assets to heirs and explore strategies to minimize their tax burden.
10. Meet with an estate planning attorney to ensure all legal documents are current and properly executed.
