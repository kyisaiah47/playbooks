CATEGORY 1: Emergency Stabilization
1. List all your current debts with minimum payment amounts and due dates.
2. Calculate your essential monthly expenses including housing, utilities, food, and transportation.
3. Contact creditors to explain your situation and request payment deferrals or reduced payments.
4. Apply for emergency assistance programs in your area for utilities, rent, or food.
5. Sell non-essential items to generate immediate cash for urgent expenses.
6. Create a bare-bones survival budget covering only absolute necessities.
7. Stop using credit cards and switch to cash-only spending.
8. Open a basic checking account if you don't have one for direct deposits.
9. Research local food banks and community resources for immediate support.
10. Document all your financial communications and agreements in writing.

CATEGORY 2: Income Recovery
1. Update your resume and LinkedIn profile to reflect your current skills and experience.
2. Apply for unemployment benefits if you're eligible and haven't already done so.
3. Register with temporary staffing agencies for immediate work opportunities.
4. Explore gig economy options like rideshare, delivery, or freelance work.
5. Contact your network to let them know you're looking for employment opportunities.
6. Consider taking on part-time work while searching for full-time employment.
7. Look into job training programs or certifications that could increase your earning potential.
8. Research government programs that provide job placement assistance.
9. Set a daily goal for job applications and networking activities.
10. Practice interviewing skills and prepare for common interview questions.

CATEGORY 3: Debt Management
1. List all debts from smallest to largest balance to consider the debt snowball method.
2. Contact each creditor to negotiate payment plans that fit your current income.
3. Research debt consolidation options to simplify your monthly payments.
4. Consider meeting with a nonprofit credit counseling service for professional guidance.
5. Prioritize secured debts like mortgages and car loans to avoid asset loss.
6. Request hardship programs from credit card companies and loan servicers.
7. Avoid taking on any new debt during your recovery period.
8. Set up automatic minimum payments to avoid additional late fees.
9. Keep detailed records of all debt payments and agreements.
10. Review your credit report monthly to track progress and identify errors.

CATEGORY 4: Financial Foundation Building
1. Open a savings account and commit to saving even small amounts regularly.
2. Create a simple budgeting system using apps, spreadsheets, or envelope method.
3. Set up automatic transfers to savings even if it's just $5 per week.
4. Learn basic financial literacy through free online courses or library resources.
5. Establish or rebuild your credit by making all payments on time.
6. Consider a secured credit card if you need to rebuild your credit history.
7. Track your daily expenses to identify spending patterns and potential cuts.
8. Set small, achievable financial goals like saving $100 in three months.
9. Research and compare banking options to minimize fees and maximize benefits.
10. Create a simple financial filing system for important documents and records.

CATEGORY 5: Long-term Recovery Planning
1. Set a timeline for when you want to achieve specific financial milestones.
2. Research career advancement opportunities in your field or new fields.
3. Create a plan to build an emergency fund covering three months of expenses.
4. Consider additional education or skills training to increase your earning potential.
5. Develop multiple income streams to create financial stability and security.
6. Plan for major expenses like car repairs or medical bills before they occur.
7. Set long-term goals for homeownership, retirement savings, or other major purchases.
8. Create a debt payoff timeline showing when each debt will be eliminated.
9. Build relationships with financial professionals like accountants or financial advisors.
10. Establish systems and habits that will prevent future financial crises.
