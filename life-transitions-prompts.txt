CATEGORY 1: Self-Reflection and Assessment
1. Write down three core values that have guided your major life decisions.
2. List five things you've learned about yourself in the past year.
3. Identify one recurring pattern in how you handle change.
4. Name three strengths you want to leverage in your next chapter.
5. Describe what success looks like for you personally, not professionally.
6. Write a letter to yourself from five years ago offering advice.
7. List three fears about your current transition and why they exist.
8. Identify one limiting belief you're ready to release.
9. Name three things you're grateful for during this uncertain time.
10. Write down your definition of happiness in one sentence.

CATEGORY 2: Practical Planning and Logistics
1. Create a basic budget for the next six months of transition.
2. Research three potential living situations that align with your goals.
3. Update your resume to reflect your most recent accomplishments.
4. Make a list of important documents you need to organize or obtain.
5. Set up three informational interviews with people in your field of interest.
6. Create a timeline with key milestones for the next 90 days.
7. Research health insurance options if your coverage will change.
8. Make a list of subscriptions or memberships you need to cancel or transfer.
9. Set up a dedicated savings account for transition-related expenses.
10. Schedule appointments with relevant professionals (accountant, lawyer, doctor).

CATEGORY 3: Relationships and Support Systems
1. Identify three people you can count on for emotional support during this transition.
2. Have an honest conversation with your family about your upcoming changes.
3. Reach out to one mentor or advisor for guidance on your next steps.
4. Join one group or community related to your transition or new interests.
5. Schedule regular check-ins with your closest friend or confidant.
6. Write thank-you notes to people who have supported you recently.
7. Set boundaries with people who drain your energy during this vulnerable time.
8. Find one accountability partner to help you stay on track with goals.
9. Plan a meaningful goodbye ritual for relationships or situations you're leaving.
10. Introduce yourself to three new people who share your interests or goals.

CATEGORY 4: Emotional Processing and Mental Health
1. Start a daily journaling practice to process your thoughts and feelings.
2. Identify one healthy coping strategy for managing transition anxiety.
3. Practice saying "I don't know yet" without feeling embarrassed or ashamed.
4. Schedule time for one activity that brings you joy each week.
5. Create a simple mindfulness routine to stay grounded in the present moment.
6. Give yourself permission to grieve what you're leaving behind.
7. Celebrate one small victory or positive step you've taken recently.
8. Practice self-compassion by speaking to yourself as you would a good friend.
9. Identify triggers that increase your stress and plan healthy responses.
10. Consider working with a therapist or counselor to navigate this transition.

CATEGORY 5: Exploration and New Opportunities
1. Try one new activity or hobby you've been curious about.
2. Read three articles or books related to your area of interest or change.
3. Attend one event, workshop, or class in a field you're considering.
4. Take a skills assessment to identify your natural talents and abilities.
5. Shadow someone for a day in a role or lifestyle you're considering.
6. Visit three places you might want to live or work in the future.
7. Start one small project or side venture to test a new interest.
8. Apply for one opportunity that excites you, even if you feel underqualified.
9. Create a vision board or visual representation of your ideal future.
10. Schedule one adventure or experience that's completely outside your comfort zone.
