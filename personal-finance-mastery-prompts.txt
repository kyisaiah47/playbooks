CATEGORY 1: Financial Foundation & Budgeting
1. Calculate your exact monthly take-home income after all deductions and taxes.
2. List every monthly expense you have, from rent to streaming subscriptions.
3. Track every dollar you spend for one full week to identify spending patterns.
4. Create a simple budget using the 50/30/20 rule (needs/wants/savings).
5. Set up automatic transfers to move savings to a separate account each payday.
6. Review and cancel any unused subscriptions or memberships.
7. Negotiate one monthly bill (phone, internet, insurance) to reduce costs.
8. Build a starter emergency fund of $500 in a high-yield savings account.
9. Choose one budgeting app or method to track your finances consistently.
10. Schedule a monthly money date to review your budget and spending.

CATEGORY 2: Debt Management & Credit Health
1. List all your debts with current balances, minimum payments, and interest rates.
2. Check your credit score using a free service like Credit Karma or Experian.
3. Review your credit report for errors and dispute any inaccuracies you find.
4. Choose either debt snowball or avalanche method to tackle your debts strategically.
5. Contact one creditor to negotiate a lower interest rate on existing debt.
6. Set up automatic minimum payments on all debts to avoid late fees.
7. Stop using credit cards for new purchases until existing balances are paid off.
8. Calculate how much extra you can pay toward debt each month.
9. Consider consolidating high-interest debt with a personal loan or balance transfer.
10. Create a visual debt tracker to monitor your progress toward being debt-free.

CATEGORY 3: Emergency Fund & Risk Management
1. Calculate your essential monthly expenses to determine your emergency fund goal.
2. Open a separate high-yield savings account specifically for emergencies.
3. Automate weekly or monthly contributions to build your emergency fund gradually.
4. Review your health insurance coverage and understand your deductibles and copays.
5. Get quotes for renters or homeowners insurance to protect your belongings.
6. Consider term life insurance if you have dependents who rely on your income.
7. Research disability insurance options through your employer or independently.
8. Create a list of emergency contacts and important financial account information.
9. Build your emergency fund to cover 3-6 months of essential expenses.
10. Keep emergency fund money easily accessible but separate from everyday accounts.

CATEGORY 4: Investing & Wealth Building
1. Contribute enough to your employer's 401(k) to get the full company match.
2. Open a Roth IRA account with a low-cost brokerage firm.
3. Start investing with a simple three-fund portfolio of total market index funds.
4. Increase your retirement contributions by 1% whenever you get a raise.
5. Learn about dollar-cost averaging by investing the same amount monthly.
6. Research and understand expense ratios when choosing mutual funds or ETFs.
7. Set up automatic investing to remove emotion from your investment decisions.
8. Calculate how much you need to save monthly to reach retirement goals.
9. Consider opening a taxable investment account for medium-term goals.
10. Review and rebalance your investment portfolio once or twice per year.

CATEGORY 5: Financial Goals & Long-term Planning
1. Write down three specific financial goals with target amounts and deadlines.
2. Calculate the monthly savings needed to reach each of your financial goals.
3. Open separate savings accounts for different goals like vacation or home down payment.
4. Create a timeline showing when you want to achieve debt freedom and other milestones.
5. Research the costs associated with major goals like buying a home or having children.
6. Set up automatic savings transfers for each specific goal you're working toward.
7. Track your net worth monthly by subtracting total debts from total assets.
8. Plan for irregular expenses like car maintenance, holidays, and annual fees.
9. Consider additional income streams like side hustles or passive investments.
10. Review and adjust your financial goals quarterly as your situation changes.
