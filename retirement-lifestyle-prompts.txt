I'll create 5 logical categories for a retirement lifestyle journey with 10 actionable prompts each:

CATEGORY 1: Financial Planning & Security
1. Calculate your monthly retirement income needs based on current expenses.
2. Review and optimize your 401(k), IRA, and pension distributions.
3. Create a withdrawal strategy that minimizes taxes on retirement accounts.
4. Establish an emergency fund covering 6-12 months of retirement expenses.
5. Research Medicare options and supplemental insurance plans.
6. Update your estate planning documents including wills and beneficiaries.
7. Consider long-term care insurance options and costs.
8. Evaluate whether to pay off your mortgage before retirement.
9. Create a budget that accounts for inflation over 20-30 years.
10. Consult with a financial advisor about Social Security claiming strategies.

CATEGORY 2: Health & Wellness
1. Schedule comprehensive health screenings with all your doctors.
2. Establish relationships with healthcare providers in your retirement location.
3. Create a daily exercise routine that fits your physical abilities.
4. Research healthy meal planning and cooking for smaller households.
5. Find age-appropriate fitness classes or walking groups in your area.
6. Set up regular mental health check-ins or counseling if needed.
7. Organize all medical records and create a health information binder.
8. Research preventive care options and wellness programs for seniors.
9. Plan regular vision and hearing checkups to maintain quality of life.
10. Create a medication management system with your pharmacist.

CATEGORY 3: Housing & Living Arrangements
1. Decide whether to downsize, age in place, or relocate for retirement.
2. Research retirement-friendly communities and their amenities.
3. Evaluate your current home for accessibility and safety modifications.
4. Compare cost of living differences between potential retirement locations.
5. Consider proximity to family, healthcare, and activities in housing decisions.
6. Research senior living options from independent to assisted care.
7. Plan home modifications like grab bars, ramps, or stair lifts.
8. Evaluate transportation options if you can no longer drive.
9. Create a maintenance plan for your home or research maintenance-free living.
10. Consider climate preferences and how weather affects your health.

CATEGORY 4: Purpose & Activities
1. Identify hobbies or interests you want to pursue with more time.
2. Research volunteer opportunities that match your skills and passions.
3. Consider part-time work or consulting in your field of expertise.
4. Join clubs, groups, or organizations related to your interests.
5. Plan educational pursuits like auditing classes or learning new skills.
6. Set up a regular schedule to maintain structure in your days.
7. Explore creative outlets like art, music, writing, or crafts.
8. Research travel opportunities and create a bucket list of destinations.
9. Consider mentoring younger people in your profession or community.
10. Plan ways to stay intellectually engaged through reading, puzzles, or games.

CATEGORY 5: Relationships & Social Connections
1. Plan regular visits and communication schedules with family members.
2. Nurture friendships and make effort to stay connected with your network.
3. Join senior centers or community groups to meet new people.
4. Plan activities with your spouse or partner for shared retirement enjoyment.
5. Consider how to maintain relationships with former work colleagues.
6. Research dating or companionship options if you're single in retirement.
7. Plan intergenerational activities to stay connected with younger family.
8. Find ways to contribute to your community through civic engagement.
9. Create traditions and regular gatherings with friends and family.
10. Consider adopting a pet for companionship and routine.
