CATEGORY 1: Financial Stabilization
1. Calculate your total monthly expenses and identify which ones are essential versus optional.
2. Apply for unemployment benefits through your state's unemployment insurance program.
3. Review your savings account balance and determine how many months of expenses you can cover.
4. Contact your bank to discuss temporary payment deferrals or modified payment plans for loans.
5. Research local food banks, utility assistance programs, and emergency financial aid resources.
6. Cancel or pause non-essential subscriptions and memberships to reduce monthly expenses.
7. Create a bare-bones budget that covers only housing, food, utilities, and transportation.
8. Explore temporary income sources like gig work, freelancing, or part-time employment.
9. Contact your mortgage lender or landlord to discuss potential payment arrangements.
10. Review your insurance policies to ensure you maintain essential coverage while reducing costs.

CATEGORY 2: Job Search Strategy
1. Update your resume with your most recent job experience and accomplishments.
2. Create or refresh your LinkedIn profile with a professional photo and current information.
3. Make a list of companies in your industry that you'd like to work for.
4. Set up job alerts on major job boards for positions matching your skills and experience.
5. Research salary ranges for your target positions in your geographic area.
6. Prepare a 30-second elevator pitch that summarizes your professional background and goals.
7. Identify and reach out to three professional contacts who might know about job opportunities.
8. Apply to at least five relevant job postings each week.
9. Follow up on applications you submitted more than two weeks ago.
10. Track your job applications in a spreadsheet including company, position, date applied, and status.

CATEGORY 3: Skill Development
1. Identify the top three skills that employers in your field are currently seeking.
2. Enroll in a free online course related to your industry or desired career path.
3. Obtain a professional certification that's valued in your field.
4. Practice interviewing by recording yourself answering common interview questions.
5. Learn a new software program or tool that's commonly used in your industry.
6. Attend a virtual workshop or webinar related to your professional interests.
7. Read industry publications or blogs to stay current on trends and developments.
8. Join a professional association in your field to access resources and networking opportunities.
9. Complete a portfolio project that demonstrates your skills to potential employers.
10. Seek feedback on your resume and interview skills from a career counselor or mentor.

CATEGORY 4: Network Building
1. Reach out to former colleagues to let them know you're looking for new opportunities.
2. Attend a virtual networking event or industry meetup in your area.
3. Schedule informational interviews with professionals working in companies you're interested in.
4. Join relevant professional groups on LinkedIn and participate in discussions.
5. Reconnect with former supervisors or mentors who could provide references or job leads.
6. Volunteer for a cause you care about to meet new people and gain experience.
7. Ask your personal network of friends and family to share job leads they come across.
8. Attend job fairs, either in person or virtual, to meet recruiters and hiring managers.
9. Connect with recruiters who specialize in your industry or job function.
10. Follow up with new contacts within 48 hours of meeting them to maintain the relationship.

CATEGORY 5: Mental Health & Resilience
1. Establish a daily routine that includes regular wake-up and bedtime hours.
2. Set aside time each day for physical exercise, even if it's just a 20-minute walk.
3. Practice stress-reduction techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.
4. Limit your job search activities to specific hours to avoid burnout.
5. Stay connected with supportive friends and family members through regular check-ins.
6. Consider joining a job seekers support group in your community or online.
7. Celebrate small wins in your job search, like completing an application or getting an interview.
8. Maintain hobbies or interests that bring you joy and help you relax.
9. Seek professional counseling if you're experiencing persistent anxiety or depression.
10. Write in a journal to process your feelings and track your progress during this transition.
