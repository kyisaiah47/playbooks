CATEGORY 1: Emotional Processing and Self-Care
1. Write down three emotions you're feeling right now without judging them.
2. Take a 10-minute walk outside to clear your head.
3. Call a trusted friend or family member to talk about how you're doing.
4. Practice deep breathing for 5 minutes when you feel overwhelmed.
5. Create a playlist of songs that help you process your emotions.
6. Write a letter to yourself expressing what you need to hear right now.
7. Schedule a therapy session or counseling appointment.
8. Take a warm bath or shower to physically comfort yourself.
9. Allow yourself to cry without trying to stop it.
10. Write down three things you're grateful for today despite the pain.

CATEGORY 2: Practical Logistics and Living Arrangements
1. Make a list of all shared belongings that need to be divided or returned.
2. Change your address with banks, utilities, and subscription services if you're moving.
3. Update your emergency contacts on important accounts and documents.
4. Secure your own place to live if you need to move out.
5. Separate joint bank accounts and credit cards.
6. Update your phone plan if you were on a shared account.
7. Retrieve your personal items from your ex-partner's place.
8. Cancel or transfer shared streaming services and memberships.
9. Find new living arrangements for shared pets if applicable.
10. Update your insurance policies and beneficiaries.

CATEGORY 3: Social Boundaries and Communication
1. Decide whether to block, unfollow, or limit your ex on social media.
2. Write down clear boundaries about contact with your ex-partner.
3. Inform close friends and family about your preferred level of support.
4. Plan how to handle mutual friends and social situations.
5. Delete or archive old text messages and photos that cause pain.
6. Create a script for what to say when people ask about your relationship status.
7. Set boundaries with mutual friends about sharing information between you and your ex.
8. Decide on communication rules if you have children or shared responsibilities together.
9. Ask friends not to update you about your ex-partner's activities unless necessary.
10. Practice saying "no" to invitations or activities that feel too overwhelming right now.

CATEGORY 4: Rebuilding Identity and Independence
1. Make a list of hobbies or interests you want to explore or return to.
2. Plan a solo activity you've always wanted to try.
3. Update your dating profiles to reflect your single status or delete them entirely.
4. Reconnect with friends you may have lost touch with during your relationship.
5. Set one small personal goal you can accomplish this week.
6. Cook your favorite meal just for yourself.
7. Rearrange or redecorate your living space to reflect your personal style.
8. Sign up for a class, workshop, or activity that interests you.
9. Write down your personal values and what you want in future relationships.
10. Treat yourself to something special that makes you feel good.

CATEGORY 5: Moving Forward and Growth
1. Reflect on what you learned about yourself during this relationship.
2. Write down the positive qualities you want to develop in yourself.
3. Set a realistic timeline for when you might feel ready to date again.
4. Forgive yourself for any mistakes you made in the relationship.
5. Practice gratitude for the good memories while accepting the relationship is over.
6. Join a support group or online community for people going through breakups.
7. Read a book about healing from heartbreak or personal growth.
8. Write down your non-negotiables for future relationships.
9. Plan a meaningful way to symbolically let go of the relationship.
10. Focus on building a fulfilling single life before considering dating again.
