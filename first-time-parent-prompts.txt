CATEGORY 1: Pregnancy Preparation
1. Schedule your first prenatal appointment with an obstetrician or midwife.
2. Start taking prenatal vitamins with folic acid daily.
3. Create a pregnancy announcement plan for family and friends.
4. Research and choose a birthing location that aligns with your preferences.
5. Begin reading about pregnancy stages and what to expect each trimester.
6. Update your health insurance to include maternity coverage.
7. Start a pregnancy journal to track symptoms and milestones.
8. Plan a babymoon trip before travel becomes more difficult.
9. Research childbirth classes and sign up for your preferred option.
10. Create a list of questions to ask at each prenatal appointment.

CATEGORY 2: Baby Essentials & Nursery Setup
1. Research and purchase a safe crib that meets current safety standards.
2. Stock up on newborn diapers, wipes, and basic baby care supplies.
3. Choose and install a properly fitted car seat before leaving the hospital.
4. Set up a changing station with all necessary supplies within reach.
5. Prepare multiple swaddles, sleep sacks, and newborn clothing sets.
6. Install blackout curtains or shades in the nursery for better sleep.
7. Purchase a reliable baby monitor system for peace of mind.
8. Create a nursing or feeding station with comfortable seating and supplies.
9. Babyproof your home by covering outlets and securing cabinets.
10. Prepare a hospital bag with essentials for both you and baby.

CATEGORY 3: Health & Medical Care
1. Choose a pediatrician and schedule a meet-and-greet appointment.
2. Learn infant CPR and basic first aid through a certified course.
3. Create a list of emergency contacts including pediatrician and poison control.
4. Research your baby's vaccination schedule and discuss with your doctor.
5. Learn how to take a baby's temperature and when to call the doctor.
6. Stock your medicine cabinet with infant-safe thermometer and medications.
7. Understand the signs of common newborn conditions like jaundice or colic.
8. Plan for postpartum mental health support and know warning signs of depression.
9. Schedule your own postpartum follow-up appointments with your doctor.
10. Research local lactation consultants in case breastfeeding support is needed.

CATEGORY 4: Daily Care & Routines
1. Learn proper techniques for diaper changing and practice before baby arrives.
2. Master safe bathing techniques for newborns and gather necessary supplies.
3. Research different feeding methods and prepare bottles or breastfeeding supplies.
4. Create a simple daily routine template for feeding, sleeping, and playtime.
5. Learn how to swaddle a baby properly for better sleep and comfort.
6. Practice safe sleep guidelines by setting up the crib correctly.
7. Understand newborn sleep patterns and prepare for frequent night wakings.
8. Learn to recognize hunger cues, tired signs, and overstimulation signals.
9. Prepare easy meal options for yourself during the demanding newborn phase.
10. Set up a system for tracking feeding times, diaper changes, and sleep.

CATEGORY 5: Support System & Life Adjustments
1. Build a support network of other parents, family members, and friends.
2. Discuss parental leave plans with your employer and understand your benefits.
3. Create a budget that accounts for new baby expenses and potential income changes.
4. Research childcare options if you plan to return to work.
5. Plan how to divide household and baby care responsibilities with your partner.
6. Join local parenting groups or online communities for ongoing support.
7. Arrange for help during the first few weeks after bringing baby home.
8. Update your will and life insurance to include your new child.
9. Create a meal train or freezer meal plan for the postpartum period.
10. Set realistic expectations and give yourself permission to learn as you go.
