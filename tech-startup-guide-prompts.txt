CATEGORY 1: Ideation & Validation
1. Write down 10 problems you've personally experienced that could be solved with technology.
2. Survey 20 potential customers to validate your startup idea before building anything.
3. Research your top 5 competitors and identify what they're missing.
4. Create a one-page problem statement that clearly defines the pain point you're solving.
5. Build a simple landing page to test demand for your product concept.
6. Interview 10 people in your target market about their current solutions.
7. Define your unique value proposition in one clear sentence.
8. Test 3 different versions of your core idea with potential users.
9. Calculate the total addressable market size for your startup idea.
10. Document the key assumptions your business model depends on.

CATEGORY 2: Planning & Strategy
1. Write a comprehensive business plan outlining your startup's goals and strategy.
2. Define your target customer persona with specific demographics and behaviors.
3. Create a detailed competitive analysis of direct and indirect competitors.
4. Develop a go-to-market strategy for acquiring your first 100 customers.
5. Set specific, measurable milestones for your first 12 months.
6. Choose your business model and pricing strategy based on market research.
7. Create a product roadmap with features prioritized by customer value.
8. Define your company's mission, vision, and core values.
9. Research and select the optimal legal structure for your startup.
10. Develop a risk assessment plan identifying potential challenges and solutions.

CATEGORY 3: Building & Development
1. Create a minimum viable product (MVP) with only essential features.
2. Choose your technology stack based on scalability and team expertise.
3. Set up version control and establish coding standards for your development team.
4. Build automated testing processes to ensure product quality and reliability.
5. Create user feedback loops to continuously improve your product.
6. Establish agile development processes with regular sprint cycles.
7. Design a scalable architecture that can handle future growth.
8. Implement analytics tracking to measure user behavior and product performance.
9. Create comprehensive documentation for your codebase and processes.
10. Build security measures and data protection into your product from day one.

CATEGORY 4: Funding & Finance
1. Calculate exactly how much funding you need for the next 18 months.
2. Create a detailed financial model with revenue projections and expense forecasts.
3. Research and target investors who specialize in your industry or stage.
4. Prepare a compelling pitch deck that tells your startup's story clearly.
5. Set up proper accounting systems and financial tracking from the beginning.
6. Explore alternative funding options like grants, competitions, and crowdfunding.
7. Negotiate term sheets carefully with legal counsel to protect your interests.
8. Establish key performance indicators (KPIs) to track financial health.
9. Create a burn rate analysis to monitor cash flow and runway.
10. Build relationships with potential investors before you need funding.

CATEGORY 5: Growth & Operations
1. Hire your first team members based on critical skill gaps and cultural fit.
2. Implement customer acquisition strategies across multiple marketing channels.
3. Create standard operating procedures for all key business processes.
4. Build partnerships with complementary businesses to expand your reach.
5. Establish customer support systems to maintain high satisfaction levels.
6. Set up legal compliance and regulatory requirements for your industry.
7. Create employee onboarding processes and company culture guidelines.
8. Implement data-driven decision making across all business functions.
9. Scale your infrastructure and operations to handle increased demand.
10. Plan your exit strategy or next funding round based on growth metrics.
