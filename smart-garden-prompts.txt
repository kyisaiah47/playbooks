CATEGORY 1: Garden Planning & Design
1. Research which vegetables grow best in your local climate zone and soil conditions.
2. Measure your available garden space and sketch a basic layout plan.
3. Determine how many hours of direct sunlight your garden area receives daily.
4. Test your soil pH and nutrient levels using a home testing kit.
5. Choose between raised beds, container gardening, or in-ground planting based on your space.
6. Plan your garden layout considering plant spacing and companion planting principles.
7. Research local frost dates to determine your planting and harvesting schedule.
8. Decide which smart garden technologies you want to integrate into your setup.
9. Calculate the total square footage needed for your desired vegetable varieties.
10. Create a seasonal planting calendar for continuous harvests throughout the growing season.

CATEGORY 2: Smart Technology Setup
1. Install a smart irrigation system with programmable timers and moisture sensors.
2. Set up weather monitoring stations to track temperature, humidity, and rainfall.
3. Connect soil sensors to monitor pH, moisture, and nutrient levels in real-time.
4. Install automated grow lights for indoor gardening or season extension.
5. Set up a smart greenhouse or cold frame with automated ventilation controls.
6. Configure mobile apps to receive alerts about your garden's conditions.
7. Install security cameras to monitor plant growth and deter pests remotely.
8. Set up automated fertilizer dispensers connected to your irrigation system.
9. Connect smart sensors to track plant growth stages and harvest readiness.
10. Configure backup power systems for critical smart garden equipment.

CATEGORY 3: Planting & Growing
1. Start seeds indoors under grow lights 6-8 weeks before your last frost date.
2. Prepare garden beds by adding compost and working the soil to proper depth.
3. Plant cool-season crops like lettuce and peas in early spring.
4. Transplant seedlings outdoors after hardening them off for one week.
5. Plant warm-season crops like tomatoes and peppers after soil warms to 60°F.
6. Mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7. Install trellises and support structures for climbing plants like beans and cucumbers.
8. Practice succession planting by sowing new seeds every 2-3 weeks.
9. Thin overcrowded seedlings to give remaining plants adequate space to grow.
10. Monitor daily growth progress and adjust plant support systems as needed.

CATEGORY 4: Maintenance & Care
1. Check soil moisture levels daily and water deeply when the top inch feels dry.
2. Apply organic fertilizer according to your soil test recommendations and plant needs.
3. Inspect plants weekly for signs of pests, diseases, or nutrient deficiencies.
4. Prune tomatoes by removing suckers and lower leaves touching the ground.
5. Weed garden beds weekly to prevent competition for nutrients and water.
6. Rotate crops annually to prevent soil depletion and reduce disease buildup.
7. Harvest vegetables at peak ripeness to encourage continued production.
8. Clean and calibrate smart sensors monthly to ensure accurate readings.
9. Apply organic pest control methods like companion planting and beneficial insects.
10. Update your smart system settings seasonally to match changing growing conditions.

CATEGORY 5: Harvesting & Season Management
1. Harvest leafy greens in the early morning when they're crisp and full of moisture.
2. Pick tomatoes when they show first color and let them ripen indoors.
3. Harvest root vegetables like carrots and radishes when they reach full size.
4. Collect herbs regularly by pinching stems just above leaf pairs to encourage growth.
5. Preserve excess harvest by freezing, canning, or dehydrating surplus vegetables.
6. Save seeds from your best-performing plants for next year's garden.
7. Clean up spent plants and add them to your compost pile.
8. Prepare garden beds for winter by adding compost and covering with mulch.
9. Store garden tools properly and winterize smart irrigation systems.
10. Plan next season's garden based on this year's successes and challenges.
