CATEGORY 1: Communication Building
1. Practice active listening by repeating back what your partner said before responding.
2. Schedule 15 minutes daily for uninterrupted conversation without phones or distractions.
3. Use "I feel" statements instead of "you always" or "you never" language.
4. Ask your partner one thoughtful question about their day every evening.
5. Practice expressing appreciation by telling your partner one thing you're grateful for about them.
6. Create a safe word to use when discussions become too heated and you need a break.
7. Write down your thoughts before having difficult conversations to organize your points.
8. Practice summarizing your partner's perspective before sharing your own viewpoint.
9. Set aside time each week to discuss relationship goals and check in on progress.
10. Learn to express your needs clearly without expecting your partner to read your mind.

CATEGORY 2: Conflict Resolution
1. Take a 20-minute cooling-off period when arguments escalate before continuing the discussion.
2. Focus on solving the specific issue at hand rather than bringing up past grievances.
3. Practice compromising by finding solutions that address both partners' core needs.
4. Use the "soft startup" technique by beginning difficult conversations with gentle language.
5. Acknowledge your partner's feelings even when you disagree with their perspective.
6. Create ground rules for fair fighting that both partners agree to follow.
7. Practice apologizing sincerely when you've made mistakes or hurt your partner.
8. Work together to identify recurring conflict patterns and their underlying triggers.
9. Focus on behaviors you can change rather than trying to change your partner.
10. Schedule regular relationship meetings to address small issues before they become big problems.

CATEGORY 3: Emotional Intimacy
1. Share one vulnerable feeling or fear with your partner each week.
2. Practice comforting your partner when they're upset without trying to fix their problems.
3. Create rituals for emotional connection like bedtime check-ins or morning coffee together.
4. Express empathy by validating your partner's emotions even when you don't understand them.
5. Share positive memories and experiences you've had together regularly.
6. Practice being emotionally available by putting away devices during intimate conversations.
7. Learn your partner's emotional needs and find specific ways to meet them.
8. Create a safe space for your partner to express difficult emotions without judgment.
9. Practice self-soothing techniques to manage your own emotional reactions during conflicts.
10. Regularly express love and affection in ways that are meaningful to your partner.

CATEGORY 4: Trust and Security
1. Follow through consistently on commitments you make to your partner, no matter how small.
2. Practice transparency by sharing your thoughts and feelings openly and honestly.
3. Create agreements about boundaries and expectations that both partners understand.
4. Address trust violations immediately and work together to repair any damage.
5. Practice vulnerability by admitting your mistakes and taking responsibility for your actions.
6. Build emotional safety by responding with compassion when your partner shares concerns.
7. Keep confidences and avoid sharing your partner's private information with others.
8. Create predictable routines and rituals that foster stability in your relationship.
9. Practice forgiveness by letting go of past hurts after they've been properly addressed.
10. Demonstrate trustworthiness through consistent actions that match your words over time.

CATEGORY 5: Personal Growth and Self-Awareness
1. Identify your relationship triggers and practice managing your reactions to them.
2. Work on your own emotional regulation skills through mindfulness or therapy.
3. Reflect on patterns from your family of origin that might affect your current relationship.
4. Practice self-care regularly so you can show up as your best self in the relationship.
5. Take responsibility for your own happiness rather than expecting your partner to fulfill all your needs.
6. Identify and work on personal habits or behaviors that negatively impact your relationship.
7. Practice setting healthy boundaries that protect both yourself and your relationship.
8. Develop your own interests and friendships outside of the relationship.
9. Work on communicating your personal values and life goals clearly to your partner.
10. Commit to ongoing personal development through reading, therapy, or self-reflection exercises.
