CATEGORY 1: Health and Medical Management
1. Schedule a comprehensive medical evaluation with your parent's primary care physician.
2. Create a centralized list of all current medications, dosages, and prescribing doctors.
3. Research and interview potential geriatricians or specialists for ongoing care.
4. Set up a medical alert system or emergency response device for your parent.
5. Organize all medical records, insurance cards, and healthcare directives in one location.
6. Establish a relationship with a local pharmacy that offers medication management services.
7. Create a weekly pill organizer system to prevent medication errors.
8. Schedule regular vision and hearing tests to monitor sensory changes.
9. Research physical therapy options to maintain mobility and prevent falls.
10. Discuss advance directives and healthcare wishes with your parent while they're able.

CATEGORY 2: Home Safety and Modifications
1. Conduct a comprehensive home safety assessment focusing on fall prevention.
2. Install grab bars in bathrooms and along stairways for added stability.
3. Improve lighting throughout the home, especially in hallways and staircases.
4. Remove or secure loose rugs and repair uneven flooring surfaces.
5. Install a medical alert system with 24/7 monitoring capabilities.
6. Evaluate the need for a stair lift or bedroom relocation to the main floor.
7. Ensure smoke detectors and carbon monoxide detectors have fresh batteries.
8. Create an emergency contact list and post it in multiple visible locations.
9. Consider installing security cameras or a doorbell camera for remote monitoring.
10. Organize important documents in a fireproof safe or easily accessible location.

CATEGORY 3: Legal and Financial Planning
1. Gather all important financial documents including bank statements and investment accounts.
2. Meet with an elder law attorney to discuss power of attorney documents.
3. Review and update your parent's will and estate planning documents.
4. Research Medicare benefits and supplemental insurance options available.
5. Set up automatic bill payments to prevent missed payments or late fees.
6. Create a comprehensive list of all accounts, passwords, and financial institutions.
7. Investigate long-term care insurance options and coverage details.
8. Establish joint access to bank accounts for emergency financial management.
9. Research veteran benefits if your parent served in the military.
10. Consider setting up a trust to protect assets and simplify estate management.

CATEGORY 4: Daily Living and Independence Support
1. Evaluate your parent's ability to safely drive and consider alternative transportation options.
2. Research grocery delivery services and meal preparation programs in their area.
3. Set up a daily check-in routine via phone calls or visits.
4. Investigate housekeeping and maintenance services to help with home upkeep.
5. Create a simple daily routine chart to help maintain structure and purpose.
6. Research senior center activities and social programs to prevent isolation.
7. Set up technology solutions like tablets or smartphones for easier communication.
8. Evaluate the need for personal care assistance with bathing, dressing, or grooming.
9. Organize a reliable network of family members and friends for regular support.
10. Research adult day programs that provide socialization and supervised activities.

CATEGORY 5: Care Coordination and Future Planning
1. Research assisted living facilities and memory care options in your preferred area.
2. Create a family communication plan to keep everyone informed about care decisions.
3. Interview and establish relationships with home health agencies for future needs.
4. Develop a crisis management plan for medical emergencies or sudden changes.
5. Research respite care options to provide breaks for primary family caregivers.
6. Create a detailed care preferences document outlining your parent's wishes and routines.
7. Investigate adult protective services and elder abuse prevention resources in your area.
8. Establish relationships with local aging services and Area Agency on Aging programs.
9. Plan for potential cognitive decline by researching memory care specialists and resources.
10. Create a transition timeline for different levels of care as needs change over time.
