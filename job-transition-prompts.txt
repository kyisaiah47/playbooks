CATEGORY 1: Self-Assessment & Goal Setting
1. Write down your top 5 skills and how they apply to your desired career path.
2. Identify 3 things you disliked about your previous job to avoid in your next role.
3. Define your ideal work environment, company culture, and team dynamics.
4. Set a realistic timeline with milestones for your job search process.
5. List your salary expectations and non-negotiable benefits requirements.
6. Assess your current financial situation and calculate how long you can job search.
7. Identify any skill gaps you need to address before applying to target roles.
8. Write a brief personal mission statement for your career transition.
9. Determine whether you want to stay in your current industry or pivot completely.
10. Create a list of companies or organizations you'd be excited to work for.

CATEGORY 2: Skill Development & Education
1. Enroll in an online course relevant to your target job requirements.
2. Identify 3 industry certifications that would strengthen your candidacy.
3. Practice new technical skills for 30 minutes daily using free online resources.
4. Join professional associations related to your desired field or role.
5. Read one industry publication or blog weekly to stay current on trends.
6. Find a mentor who works in your target role or industry.
7. Attend virtual or in-person workshops to develop specific competencies.
8. Complete a portfolio project that demonstrates your abilities to potential employers.
9. Take on volunteer work that builds relevant experience for your career goals.
10. Shadow someone in your desired role for a day to understand daily responsibilities.

CATEGORY 3: Resume & Application Materials
1. Rewrite your resume to highlight achievements rather than just job duties.
2. Create a master list of all your accomplishments with quantified results.
3. Tailor your resume keywords to match job descriptions you're targeting.
4. Write 5 different versions of your professional summary for various roles.
5. Gather and organize all your work samples, certifications, and references.
6. Create a one-page cover letter template you can customize for each application.
7. Build or update your LinkedIn profile with a professional photo and compelling headline.
8. Write detailed descriptions of your top 3 career achievements with specific metrics.
9. Have 2-3 trusted colleagues review and provide feedback on your resume.
10. Create a simple portfolio website or digital presence showcasing your work.

CATEGORY 4: Job Search & Applications
1. Set up job alerts on 3-5 major job boards for positions matching your criteria.
2. Apply to at least 5 relevant positions each week during your active search period.
3. Research each company thoroughly before submitting your application materials.
4. Keep a detailed spreadsheet tracking all applications, contacts, and follow-up dates.
5. Customize your cover letter for each application to address specific job requirements.
6. Follow up on applications after 1-2 weeks if you haven't heard back.
7. Expand your search beyond online job boards to include company websites directly.
8. Connect with current employees at target companies through LinkedIn or networking events.
9. Consider working with recruiters who specialize in your industry or role type.
10. Set daily and weekly goals for job search activities to maintain consistent momentum.

CATEGORY 5: Networking & Interview Preparation
1. Reach out to 3 people in your network weekly to inform them about your job search.
2. Attend one networking event, meetup, or industry conference each month.
3. Practice your elevator pitch until you can deliver it confidently in 30 seconds.
4. Prepare specific examples using the STAR method for common behavioral interview questions.
5. Research and practice answers to technical questions relevant to your target roles.
6. Conduct mock interviews with friends, family, or career counselors for feedback.
7. Prepare thoughtful questions to ask interviewers about the role and company culture.
8. Plan your interview outfits and logistics in advance to reduce day-of stress.
9. Send personalized thank-you notes within 24 hours after each interview.
10. Build relationships with people in your target industry through informational interviews.
