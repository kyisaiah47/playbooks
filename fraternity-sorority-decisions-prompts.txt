CATEGORY 1: Research and Exploration
1. Research the history and values of each fraternity or sorority on your campus.
2. Attend informational meetings or open houses hosted by different Greek organizations.
3. Talk to current members about their experiences and what drew them to their organization.
4. Review each organization's philanthropy work and community service commitments.
5. Look up the academic requirements and GPA standards for membership.
6. Investigate the financial commitments including dues, fees, and additional costs.
7. Read online reviews and testimonials from current and former members.
8. Attend Greek life fairs or showcase events to learn about multiple organizations at once.
9. Research the national organization's reputation and any recent news or controversies.
10. Ask about housing options and living arrangements within each organization.

CATEGORY 2: Personal Reflection and Goal Setting
1. Write down your personal values and see which organizations align with them.
2. Identify what you hope to gain from joining a fraternity or sorority.
3. Consider how Greek life fits with your academic goals and schedule.
4. Reflect on your comfort level with social events and party culture.
5. Assess your leadership aspirations and opportunities within each organization.
6. Think about your long-term career goals and potential networking benefits.
7. Evaluate your financial situation and ability to afford membership costs.
8. Consider how joining will impact your existing friendships and relationships.
9. Determine your availability for mandatory meetings, events, and activities.
10. Reflect on whether you're joining for the right reasons or due to peer pressure.

CATEGORY 3: Rush and Recruitment Process
1. Register for formal recruitment and complete all required paperwork.
2. Prepare an elevator pitch about yourself for recruitment conversations.
3. Choose appropriate outfits for each day of the recruitment process.
4. Practice small talk and prepare questions to ask current members.
5. Keep an open mind and give every organization a fair chance during rush.
6. Take notes after each recruitment event to remember your impressions.
7. Follow all recruitment rules and guidelines set by your campus.
8. Be authentic and honest about who you are during conversations.
9. Ask about the new member education process and time commitments.
10. Attend all scheduled events and be punctual to show your serious interest.

CATEGORY 4: Decision Making and Evaluation
1. Create a pros and cons list for each organization you're considering.
2. Compare the financial costs of membership across different organizations.
3. Evaluate which organization's social calendar and activities appeal to you most.
4. Consider the diversity and inclusivity within each organization.
5. Assess the academic support and resources each group provides to members.
6. Think about which organization offers the best leadership development opportunities.
7. Consider the time commitment required and whether it fits your schedule.
8. Evaluate the alumni network and post-graduation connections each offers.
9. Trust your instincts about where you felt most comfortable and welcomed.
10. Seek advice from trusted friends, family, or mentors about your top choices.

CATEGORY 5: Commitment and Integration
1. Accept your bid with enthusiasm and prepare for the new member period.
2. Attend all required new member education sessions and activities.
3. Build relationships with your pledge class and older members.
4. Learn the organization's history, traditions, and important information.
5. Participate actively in philanthropy events and community service projects.
6. Maintain your academic performance while balancing new commitments.
7. Get involved in committees or leadership roles within the organization.
8. Represent your organization positively on campus and in the community.
9. Budget effectively to manage membership dues and activity costs.
10. Embrace the lifelong commitment and continue involvement after college.
