CATEGORY 1: Property Setup & Preparation
1. Research local rental market rates to set competitive pricing for your property.
2. Take high-quality photos of each room and exterior areas for marketing materials.
3. Create a detailed property description highlighting key features and amenities.
4. Obtain necessary permits and licenses required for rental properties in your area.
5. Install essential safety equipment like smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarms.
6. Complete any needed repairs or maintenance before listing the property.
7. Set up utility accounts and determine which utilities will be included in rent.
8. Purchase landlord insurance to protect your investment property.
9. Create a move-in checklist documenting the property's current condition.
10. Establish relationships with reliable contractors for future maintenance needs.

CATEGORY 2: Tenant Screening & Selection
1. Post your rental listing on popular platforms like Zillow, Craigslist, and local sites.
2. Schedule and conduct property showings for interested prospective tenants.
3. Create a rental application form requesting employment, income, and reference information.
4. Run background checks on potential tenants to verify their rental history.
5. Contact previous landlords to ask about the applicant's payment history and behavior.
6. Verify employment status and income by requesting pay stubs or employment letters.
7. Check credit scores to assess the applicant's financial responsibility and reliability.
8. Interview potential tenants to gauge compatibility and communication style.
9. Compare multiple applications and select the most qualified candidate.
10. Send rejection letters to unsuccessful applicants following fair housing guidelines.

CATEGORY 3: Legal Documentation & Agreements
1. Draft a comprehensive lease agreement that complies with local rental laws.
2. Include specific clauses about pet policies, smoking restrictions, and property use.
3. Clearly outline rent payment terms, due dates, and late fee structures.
4. Specify security deposit amounts and conditions for refund or deductions.
5. Document the move-in inspection with photos and written condition reports.
6. Collect first month's rent and security deposit before tenant move-in.
7. Provide tenants with required disclosures about lead paint, mold, or other hazards.
8. Create emergency contact procedures and share important property information.
9. Review lease terms with tenants to ensure understanding of all obligations.
10. Keep copies of all signed documents in organized tenant files.

CATEGORY 4: Ongoing Property Management
1. Establish a reliable rent collection system with clear payment methods and deadlines.
2. Respond promptly to tenant maintenance requests and repair issues.
3. Schedule regular property inspections to identify potential problems early.
4. Maintain detailed records of all income, expenses, and property-related transactions.
5. Handle tenant complaints and disputes professionally and according to lease terms.
6. Coordinate seasonal maintenance tasks like HVAC servicing and gutter cleaning.
7. Monitor property security and address any safety concerns immediately.
8. Communicate regularly with tenants about property updates or policy changes.
9. Process rent increases according to local laws and lease agreement terms.
10. Keep emergency funds available for unexpected repairs or vacancy periods.

CATEGORY 5: Move-Out & Transition Management
1. Provide proper notice requirements when you or tenant wants to terminate the lease.
2. Schedule a pre-move-out inspection to identify potential deposit deductions.
3. Give tenants a checklist of cleaning and repair tasks to maximize deposit return.
4. Conduct final walk-through inspection documenting any damages or needed repairs.
5. Calculate security deposit refunds and itemize any legitimate deductions.
6. Return security deposits within the timeframe required by local law.
7. Change locks and update security codes after tenant move-out for safety.
8. Deep clean and complete necessary repairs to prepare for new tenants.
9. Update rental listings with any new features or improved property conditions.
10. Review the tenancy experience to improve your rental management processes.
