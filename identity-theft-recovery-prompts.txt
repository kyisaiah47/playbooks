CATEGORY 1: Immediate Response & Documentation
1. Contact your bank and credit card companies immediately to report unauthorized transactions.
2. File a police report with your local law enforcement agency to create an official record.
3. Place a fraud alert on your credit reports with one of the three major credit bureaus.
4. Document all evidence of identity theft including screenshots, receipts, and suspicious communications.
5. Create a detailed timeline of when you first noticed signs of identity theft.
6. Change passwords for all your online accounts, starting with financial institutions.
7. Contact the Federal Trade Commission to file an identity theft report at IdentityTheft.gov.
8. Review your recent bank and credit card statements for unauthorized charges.
9. Check your credit reports from all three bureaus for accounts you didn't open.
10. Take photos of any physical documents or cards that may have been compromised.

CATEGORY 2: Credit Protection & Monitoring
1. Place a credit freeze with all three major credit bureaus to prevent new accounts from being opened.
2. Order free credit reports from AnnualCreditReport.com to review for fraudulent activity.
3. Sign up for credit monitoring services to track changes to your credit report.
4. Dispute fraudulent accounts and inquiries directly with each credit bureau in writing.
5. Request extended fraud alerts that last seven years instead of the standard 90 days.
6. Monitor your credit scores regularly using free services or apps.
7. Set up account alerts for all existing credit cards and bank accounts.
8. Review and update your credit report contact information to ensure you receive notifications.
9. Consider placing a ChexSystems security freeze to protect against fraudulent bank account openings.
10. Document all communications with credit bureaus and keep copies of dispute letters.

CATEGORY 3: Account Recovery & Closure
1. Close all compromised financial accounts and open new ones with different account numbers.
2. Contact companies where fraudulent accounts were opened in your name to dispute the accounts.
3. Request debt validation letters from any collection agencies contacting you about fraudulent debts.
4. Update your direct deposit information with your employer for any new bank accounts.
5. Change your Social Security number only if identity theft continues after taking other measures.
6. Contact your insurance companies to report identity theft and check for fraudulent claims.
7. Notify your utility companies to prevent fraudulent service connections in your name.
8. Update automatic bill payments and subscriptions with new account information.
9. Request new debit and credit cards with different numbers from all financial institutions.
10. Close any store credit accounts that were opened fraudulently in your name.

CATEGORY 4: Legal & Administrative Actions
1. Keep detailed records of all identity theft-related expenses for potential tax deductions.
2. Contact the IRS if you suspect tax-related identity theft or receive notice of suspicious filings.
3. File an affidavit of identity theft with companies that accepted fraudulent applications.
4. Request copies of fraudulent applications and transaction records from affected companies.
5. Consider hiring an identity theft attorney if the theft is complex or damages are significant.
6. Update your address with the post office if mail theft was involved in the identity theft.
7. Contact your state's attorney general office to report the identity theft.
8. File complaints with relevant regulatory agencies for specific industries (banking, insurance, etc.).
9. Request law enforcement case numbers and detective contact information for follow-up.
10. Keep a master file of all identity theft documentation organized by category and date.

CATEGORY 5: Prevention & Long-term Recovery
1. Set up two-factor authentication on all important online accounts to prevent future breaches.
2. Invest in a paper shredder to destroy sensitive documents before disposal.
3. Review and strengthen your home security to prevent mail theft and document access.
4. Create a system to regularly monitor your financial statements and credit reports.
5. Educate family members about identity theft prevention and what information to protect.
6. Use secure payment methods and avoid sharing personal information over unsecured channels.
7. Regularly update passwords and use a password manager to create strong, unique passwords.
8. Be cautious about sharing personal information on social media platforms.
9. Sign up for free annual credit reports and space them throughout the year for continuous monitoring.
10. Create an emergency contact list including financial institutions, credit bureaus, and law enforcement.
