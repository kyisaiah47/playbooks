CATEGORY 1: Planning & Preparation
1. Create a comprehensive moving timeline with key dates and deadlines.
2. Research and compare at least three moving companies for quotes.
3. Declutter your home by donating or selling items you no longer need.
4. Create a detailed inventory of all belongings to be moved.
5. Set a realistic moving budget including all anticipated expenses.
6. Research your new neighborhood's amenities and local services.
7. Book time off work for moving day and settling in period.
8. Order moving supplies like boxes, tape, bubble wrap, and labels.
9. Take photos of valuable items for insurance documentation.
10. Create a floor plan of your new home to plan furniture placement.

CATEGORY 2: Address Changes & Documentation
1. Submit a change of address form with the postal service.
2. Update your address with your bank and credit card companies.
3. Notify your employer's HR department of your address change.
4. Update your voter registration for your new location.
5. Change your address on your driver's license and vehicle registration.
6. Update your address with insurance companies for home, auto, and health.
7. Notify subscription services and online retailers of your move.
8. Update your address with the IRS and state tax agencies.
9. Change your address with utility companies at both locations.
10. Update your address with medical providers and transfer prescriptions.

CATEGORY 3: Utilities & Services Setup
1. Schedule disconnection of utilities at your current home for moving day.
2. Set up electricity service at your new home before arrival.
3. Arrange for internet and cable installation at your new address.
4. Schedule gas service connection at your new home.
5. Set up water and sewer services at your new location.
6. Research and select new garbage and recycling pickup services.
7. Find and contact new healthcare providers in your area.
8. Locate the nearest post office and grocery stores to your new home.
9. Research local schools if you have children.
10. Set up security system or change locks at your new home.

CATEGORY 4: Packing & Organization
1. Start packing non-essential items at least 4 weeks before moving.
2. Label each box clearly with contents and destination room.
3. Pack a "first day" box with essentials for immediate needs.
4. Wrap fragile items carefully and mark boxes as "FRAGILE."
5. Pack heavy items in small boxes to make them easier to carry.
6. Keep important documents and valuables with you during the move.
7. Disassemble furniture and keep hardware in labeled bags.
8. Pack clothes in dresser drawers to save space and boxes.
9. Use towels and linens as padding for fragile items.
10. Create an inventory list for each box with detailed contents.

CATEGORY 5: Moving Day & Settlement
1. Confirm moving day details with your moving company 24 hours prior.
2. Pack a survival kit with snacks, water, and basic tools.
3. Do a final walkthrough of your old home before leaving.
4. Be present to supervise the loading and unloading process.
5. Check inventory lists as items are moved into your new home.
6. Test all utilities and appliances on your first day.
7. Locate and test smoke detectors and carbon monoxide alarms.
8. Unpack essentials first, then tackle one room at a time.
9. Explore your new neighborhood and introduce yourself to neighbors.
10. Register children for school and update emergency contacts.
