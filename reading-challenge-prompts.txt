CATEGORY 1: Challenge Setup & Planning
1. Choose a specific reading goal for your challenge (number of books, genres, or pages).
2. Set a realistic timeline that fits your current reading pace and schedule.
3. Create a dedicated reading list with books you're excited to explore.
4. Designate specific reading times in your daily or weekly routine.
5. Set up a cozy reading space with good lighting and minimal distractions.
6. Choose your tracking method (journal, app, or simple checklist).
7. Identify potential obstacles to your reading time and plan solutions.
8. Share your reading challenge goal with friends or family for accountability.
9. Research book recommendations from trusted sources or communities.
10. Prepare backup book options in case your first choices don't engage you.

CATEGORY 2: Reading Habit Development
1. Start with just 15-20 minutes of daily reading to build consistency.
2. Replace one daily screen time activity with reading time.
3. Carry a book or e-reader with you for unexpected free moments.
4. Read a few pages before bed instead of scrolling your phone.
5. Set a daily page minimum that feels achievable and sustainable.
6. Use the "two-page rule" - read two pages before deciding to stop.
7. Create reading rituals like making tea or finding your special reading spot.
8. Read during your commute or while waiting in lines.
9. Try audiobooks during walks, workouts, or household chores.
10. Link reading to an existing habit like having your morning coffee.

CATEGORY 3: Book Selection & Variety
1. Mix different genres to keep your reading experience fresh and engaging.
2. Include both fiction and non-fiction books in your challenge lineup.
3. Try books from authors you've never read before.
4. Include at least one classic book you've always meant to read.
5. Add a graphic novel or comic series to diversify your reading formats.
6. Choose one book outside your usual comfort zone or preferred genre.
7. Include books by authors from different cultural backgrounds.
8. Try both physical books and digital formats to see what you prefer.
9. Include at least one book recommended by someone whose taste you trust.
10. Choose books of varying lengths to maintain momentum throughout your challenge.

CATEGORY 4: Progress Tracking & Motivation
1. Log each book you complete with the date and a brief reaction.
2. Track your daily reading time to see your consistency patterns.
3. Take photos of book covers or create a visual reading progress chart.
4. Write one sentence about what you learned or enjoyed from each book.
5. Share reading updates on social media or with your reading community.
6. Celebrate small wins like finishing your first book or reaching the halfway point.
7. Create monthly check-ins to assess your progress and adjust goals if needed.
8. Rate each book you read to remember your favorites for future recommendations.
9. Keep a running list of quotes or passages that resonate with you.
10. Document any books you didn't finish and briefly note why they didn't work for you.

CATEGORY 5: Community & Reflection
1. Join an online reading group or local book club for discussion and motivation.
2. Follow bookish social media accounts for recommendations and inspiration.
3. Visit your local library or bookstore regularly to discover new titles.
4. Share book recommendations with friends and ask for their suggestions.
5. Write brief reviews or reflections on books that particularly impacted you.
6. Attend author readings, book signings, or literary events in your area.
7. Create a "books to read next" list based on discoveries during your challenge.
8. Reflect on how your reading challenge has changed your daily routine or perspective.
9. Plan what type of reading challenge you might want to try next.
10. Recommend your favorite discoveries from the challenge to others who might enjoy them.
