CATEGORY 1: Regular Maintenance
1. Check your oil level using the dipstick and add oil if needed.
2. Replace your engine air filter every 12,000-15,000 miles.
3. Rotate your tires every 5,000-7,500 miles to ensure even wear.
4. Check tire pressure monthly and inflate to manufacturer specifications.
5. Replace windshield wipers when they start streaking or squeaking.
6. Test your battery voltage and clean terminals if corroded.
7. Check all fluid levels including brake, transmission, and coolant.
8. Replace your cabin air filter every 15,000-25,000 miles.
9. Inspect belts and hoses for cracks, fraying, or wear.
10. Schedule oil changes every 3,000-7,500 miles based on your vehicle.

CATEGORY 2: Seasonal Preparation
1. Switch to winter tires or check tread depth before cold weather.
2. Test your heating and air conditioning systems before extreme weather.
3. Check antifreeze levels and concentration before winter months.
4. Inspect weatherstripping around doors and windows for gaps.
5. Clean and treat leather seats before hot summer weather.
6. Check your spare tire pressure and emergency kit supplies.
7. Wash and wax your car to protect paint from seasonal elements.
8. Test all lights including headlights, taillights, and turn signals.
9. Check windshield washer fluid and use appropriate seasonal formula.
10. Inspect your exhaust system for rust or damage before winter.

CATEGORY 3: Troubleshooting Issues
1. Identify unusual noises by noting when and where they occur.
2. Check warning lights in your dashboard and consult your manual.
3. Test your brakes by listening for squealing or feeling vibrations.
4. Monitor your engine temperature gauge during driving.
5. Notice any changes in how your car starts or idles.
6. Check for fluid leaks by looking under your parked car.
7. Pay attention to steering problems like pulling or difficulty turning.
8. Monitor fuel efficiency and investigate sudden drops in mileage.
9. Test electrical components like radio, lights, and power windows.
10. Document symptoms with dates and mileage to help mechanics diagnose.

CATEGORY 4: Finding Professional Help
1. Research local mechanics by reading online reviews and asking friends.
2. Get multiple quotes for major repairs before choosing a shop.
3. Verify that repair shops are certified and properly licensed.
4. Ask for written estimates that include parts and labor costs.
5. Find a trustworthy mechanic before you need emergency repairs.
6. Check if your car is still under warranty before paying for repairs.
7. Learn basic car terminology to communicate better with mechanics.
8. Keep records of all repairs and maintenance for warranty claims.
9. Ask mechanics to show you the problem and explain the solution.
10. Get a second opinion for expensive repairs over $500.

CATEGORY 5: Cost Management
1. Create a monthly budget that includes routine maintenance costs.
2. Buy quality parts online and bring them to your mechanic.
3. Learn to perform basic maintenance tasks like oil changes yourself.
4. Keep detailed records of all maintenance to track spending patterns.
5. Compare prices between dealerships and independent repair shops.
6. Set aside money each month in a car maintenance emergency fund.
7. Take advantage of seasonal promotions and service specials.
8. Consider extended warranties for older or high-mileage vehicles.
9. Research the most cost-effective maintenance schedule for your car model.
10. Prioritize safety-critical repairs over cosmetic or comfort issues.
