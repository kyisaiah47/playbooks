## Quality Benchmarks for Elder Care Content

### Authority Indicators
- Reference current Medicare, Medicaid, and elder law regulations
- Include evidence-based geriatric care and family caregiving research
- Demonstrate knowledge of various care settings and service options
- Show understanding of family dynamics and caregiver support needs
- Reference reputable aging organizations and professional care standards

### Practical Value Metrics
- Provide specific care assessment tools and evaluation checklists
- Include actionable communication strategies and advocacy techniques
- Offer decision frameworks for care setting selection and service coordination
- Give realistic cost estimates and funding strategy guidance
- Include resource directories for care services and family support

### Professional Credibility Tests
- Would geriatricians and elder care professionals recommend this resource?
- Does this include insights known primarily to aging services specialists?
- Are care recommendations and legal strategies current and appropriate?
- Does this solve real challenges families face in elder care planning?
- Would this prevent common elder care mistakes and improve quality outcomes?

## Content Differentiation Requirements

### Never Create Generic Content About
- Basic elder care advice available in countless aging websites and brochures
- One-size-fits-all approaches ignoring individual health needs and family circumstances
- Oversimplified care recommendations without consideration of complex medical conditions
- Generic facility lists without evaluation criteria and quality assessment guidance

### Always Create Definitive Resources For
- Evidence-based care planning with current healthcare and legal frameworks
- Family-centered approaches that address caregiver needs and family dynamics
- Comprehensive financial strategies that protect assets while ensuring quality care
- Quality-focused guidance that helps families evaluate and coordinate care services
- Professional advocacy strategies that ensure aging adults receive appropriate care

## Success Validation for Elder Care Articles

**Each article must pass these tests:**
1. **Care Quality**: Would geriatric healthcare professionals endorse these care strategies?
2. **Family Support**: Does this address real caregiver needs and family dynamics?
3. **Financial Protection**: Are funding strategies realistic and legally appropriate?
4. **Service Coordination**: Does this help families effectively navigate care systems?
5. **Dignity Preservation**: Does this support aging adults' autonomy and quality of life?

**The Ultimate Test**: If someone could only read one resource about this elder care topic, would this guide them to coordinate quality care that preserves their loved one's dignity while protecting family financial security and supporting caregiver wellbeing?
