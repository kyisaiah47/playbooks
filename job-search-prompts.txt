CATEGORY 1: Job Search Preparation
1. Update your resume with your most recent work experience and achievements.
2. Create a professional LinkedIn profile with a compelling headline and summary.
3. Research salary ranges for your target positions in your geographic area.
4. Identify 3-5 companies you'd love to work for and follow them on social media.
5. Clean up your social media profiles to ensure they're professional.
6. Prepare a 30-second elevator pitch about your background and career goals.
7. Gather professional references and ask for their permission to use them.
8. Create a portfolio or work samples to showcase your skills.
9. Set up a dedicated email address for job search communications.
10. Define your ideal job criteria including role, company size, and culture.

CATEGORY 2: Application Process
1. Tailor your resume for each specific job application.
2. Write a compelling cover letter that addresses the job requirements.
3. Apply to 5-10 jobs per week through various channels.
4. Track all your applications in a spreadsheet with dates and follow-up notes.
5. Set up job alerts on multiple job boards for your target roles.
6. Complete your online profiles on major job sites like Indeed and Glassdoor.
7. Research the hiring manager and try to find their contact information.
8. Follow up on applications after one week if you haven't heard back.
9. Apply directly on company websites rather than just through job boards.
10. Customize your LinkedIn profile to match keywords from job postings.

CATEGORY 3: Networking and Outreach
1. Reach out to former colleagues and let them know you're job searching.
2. Attend industry meetups or virtual networking events in your field.
3. Connect with professionals in your target companies on LinkedIn.
4. Ask your network for informational interviews about their companies.
5. Join professional associations or groups related to your industry.
6. Engage with industry content on LinkedIn by commenting and sharing.
7. Reach out to recruiters who specialize in your field.
8. Schedule coffee chats with contacts who work at your target companies.
9. Ask for introductions to decision-makers through mutual connections.
10. Follow up with new connections within 48 hours of meeting them.

CATEGORY 4: Interview Preparation
1. Research common interview questions for your role and practice answering them.
2. Prepare specific examples using the STAR method for behavioral questions.
3. Research the company's mission, values, and recent news thoroughly.
4. Prepare thoughtful questions to ask the interviewer about the role and company.
5. Practice your interview skills with a friend or family member.
6. Plan your interview outfit and ensure it's clean and professional.
7. Test your technology setup for video interviews in advance.
8. Prepare to explain any gaps in employment or career changes positively.
9. Research your interviewer's background on LinkedIn if possible.
10. Plan your route to the interview location and arrive 10-15 minutes early.

CATEGORY 5: Offer Negotiation and Follow-up
1. Send a thank-you email within 24 hours of each interview.
2. Follow up on your interview status after one week if you haven't heard back.
3. Research market rates for the position to prepare for salary negotiations.
4. Practice negotiating salary and benefits with a trusted friend.
5. Ask for time to consider any job offer rather than accepting immediately.
6. Negotiate beyond salary including vacation time, flexible work, and benefits.
7. Get any job offer details in writing before making your final decision.
8. Professionally decline offers that aren't the right fit for you.
9. Give appropriate notice to your current employer once you accept an offer.
10. Stay in touch with contacts you made during the job search for future opportunities.
